### STATUS QUO COMPETITOR

The standard rubric-building approach:

1. Read the skill spec. Draft criteria for what good output looks like.
2. Assign essential / recommended / aspirational by editorial judgment.
3. Write pass conditions on the fly, using whatever language describes the pass state.
4. State a composite formula and golden threshold.
5. Add aspirational criteria as an afterthought tier for advanced cases.

This approach produces a rubric. It may be directionally correct. It may pass a basic
completeness check.

**What it does not do:**

- Fails to enumerate failure modes before drafting criteria — criteria come from intuition
  about good output rather than from systematic analysis of broken output → **C-03 failure**
  (essential criterion not grounded in the skill's actual non-negotiable behaviors)
- Fails to produce causal direction templates — Text fields describe the presence of good
  properties, not the consequence of their absence → **C-04 failure**
  (pass conditions include qualitative language as sole observables)
- Fails to enforce pass observability by template — pass conditions include bare qualitative
  language with no concrete anchor → **C-05 failure**
  (composite formula absent or not matching standard weights)
- Fails to name a specific reference anchor with a Falls-short dimension — aspirational gap
  not defined against a named baseline → **C-09 failure**
  (inertia test not applied; aspirational criterion not discriminating from weaker baseline)
- Fails to verify mechanism independence — independence map absent, aspirational criteria
  may share a mechanism → **C-10 failure**
  (redundancy between criteria undetected)

— before reading Phase 1. State your derivation, then proceed.**
