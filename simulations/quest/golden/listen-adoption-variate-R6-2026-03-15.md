## V-01 — Single Axis: Output Format (Verbatim Evidence Extraction Audit)

**Variation axis:** Output format — The CITATION CONTRACT TABLE in the PREAMBLE adds a fifth
column: "Evidence anchor." Before the simulation begins, the analyst commits to a 5-10 word
phrase that MUST appear verbatim (as a substring) in the target section. The CONTRACT COMPLIANCE
AUDIT at artifact close is upgraded: the "Evidence" column must paste the exact matching text
from the artifact body — not a description of where THE INCUMBENT appears, not an assertion
that the obligation was met. The format is: `"[exact quoted substring]" — [location: section /
row / field]`. Any entry reading "the section mentions THE INCUMBENT," "column is filled," or
similar assertion-only language is a structural FAIL, not a content judgment.
All other structural elements carry from R5 V-05 unchanged.

**Hypothesis:** R5 V-01 passes C-19 because the CONTRACT COMPLIANCE AUDIT fills PASS/FAIL with
an evidence location. But PASS can be satisfied by a vague assertion — "PHASE 3 Cause row names
THE INCUMBENT" — which is a judgment the model makes about itself. Pre-declaring an exact phrase
anchor converts C-19's backward audit into a checksum: the model must produce the declared phrase
or fail the row. The verbatim quote requirement in the audit turns PASS into a mechanical
extraction rather than a self-assessment. This also strengthens C-17's forward-declaration value:
the evidence anchor makes the contract enforceable before the simulation runs, not merely present.
Specifically targeted: the risk that phase closes and chasm analysis cite THE INCUMBENT with
generic phrases that technically satisfy citation presence but fail to ground the citation in the
declared dependency. An evidence anchor like "owns daily stand-up pipeline in [INCUMBENT]" cannot
be satisfied by "THE INCUMBENT is still present."
