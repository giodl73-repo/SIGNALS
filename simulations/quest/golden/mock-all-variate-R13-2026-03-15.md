## V-01 -- Role Sequence: Seed Phrases in Template, Paraphrase Gate (Single Axis)

**axis:** role-sequence (skill template body pre-authors namespace-level seed phrases for
the inertia gap skeleton column; model reads these into the table during ROLE 1 rather than
inventing them; stop-gate uses paraphrase descriptions rather than verbatim column header
names; no other structural change from R12 V-01)
**hypothesis:** C-29 requires seed content to originate in the skill template body, not be
generated by the model at runtime. V-01 tests this in isolation: the template explicitly
supplies the nine seed phrases, and ROLE 1 copies them verbatim into the skeleton column.
C-27 and C-28 are deliberately not targeted -- the gate uses numbered paraphrase descriptions,
not verbatim column labels. This creates the (C-26 PASS, C-29 PASS, C-27 FAIL, C-28 FAIL)
cell in the 3-way separation matrix and confirms C-29 can be achieved independently of the
gate-structure chain.
**predicted:** C-01 through C-25: PASS (structure matches R12 ceiling). C-26: PASS (seed
phrases authored in template body, namespace-specific). C-27: FAIL (gate uses paraphrase).
C-28: FAIL (no annotation). C-29: PASS (seed phrases are in template, model copies -- skill
is the author). Score: 19/21.
**failure condition:** C-29 PARTIAL if the model substitutes its own phrases rather than
copying the provided seeds verbatim. The diagnostic: compare skeleton column cells in output
to the seed phrases in the template. Any paraphrase or substitution means the model is
generating, not reading -- C-29 degrades from PASS to PARTIAL.
