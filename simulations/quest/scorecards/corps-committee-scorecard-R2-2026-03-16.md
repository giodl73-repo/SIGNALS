### Excellence Signals from V-05 (Top Variation — 100 pts)

1. **Pre-skeleton numbered rule block** — THREE STRUCTURAL RULES declared before the skeleton print instruction primes the model with all three critical mechanisms (injection-default, orientation anchoring, table structure) as co-equal constraints before any phase content is encountered. The rules are active throughout skeleton reproduction and fill steps, not confined to fill rule sections. No other variation foregrounds all three mechanisms simultaneously.

2. **Rewrite-before-proceed loop for distinctiveness** — V-05's ROLE-DISTINCTIVENESS RULE adds "IF FAIL: rewrite the failing voices before proceeding to STANCE MANIFEST." This creates an in-generation self-correction mechanism absent from V-01 and V-04, which both have the same ROLE-DISTINCTIVENESS RULE but no correction instruction. The loop prevents a failing voice from propagating to the STANCE MANIFEST and producing a C-09 failure in the final output.

3. **COUPLING INTEGRITY SYMMETRY annotation** — V-05 uniquely adds "PHASE-3-COMMIT bidirectionality VALIDATE is structurally identical to PHASE-1-COMMIT bidirectionality VALIDATE." Making the symmetric contract explicit as a named structural pattern reduces the risk that the model treats one commit as more authoritative than the other.

4. **Motivational rationale co-located with injection rule** — "an incorrect YES silences the inertia voice without surfacing the silence — that is the harder failure" is present in both the RULE 1 preamble and the INERTIA CONTINUITY BRIDGE fill rules. Double exposure of the motivation increases adherence. V-03 has this in the preamble only; V-05 repeats it at the point of application.

**C-08 PARTIAL for V-02 and V-03** — These variations lack ORIENTATION-LENS and produce the same structural gap as all R1 variations. **C-10 MISS for V-01 and V-03** — Both retain list-format STANCE MANIFEST and ACTION ITEMS. V-01 chose orientation anchoring as its single axis without addressing tables; V-03 chose injection-default without addressing tables. Neither overlap is covered.

**C-11 MISS for V-01, V-02, V-04** — These variations have adequate YES-gate logic for C-05 (incorrect affirmation is a FAILS axis) but do not include the full C-11 package: injection-as-default framing in the bridge section header, motivational rationale for why incorrect YES is worse, and "YES without qualifying participant fails" as an explicit named constraint rather than an implicit FAILS axis.
