### Excellence Signals from V-05

**What made V-05 the top scorer:**

1. **Binary precondition stacking** — By declaring C-25, C-26, and C-27 as blocking preconditions rather than scored items, V-05 transforms them from rubric checkboxes into advancement gates. The mechanism creates zero-partial-credit enforcement: the prompt cannot progress until the contract is closed. This is different from C-20 (which *scores* gates) — this is gates applied *to specific evidence criteria*.

2. **Four-column evidence table as unified orthogonality enforcer** — A single table artifact simultaneously satisfies C-18 (origin-of-claim), C-24 (experience-proximity), and C-27 (non-conflation) by requiring all four columns to be filled per row. Conflation becomes a visible structural error (an empty or merged column), not an abstract rule violation.

3. **Named artifact as multi-criterion anchor** — The Inertia Profile gives C-10 (simulation fidelity) a concrete structural home. By binding the fidelity annotation to a named artifact, V-05 avoids the floating prose problem where meta-notes are present but incidental. The artifact also anchors C-13 (register) and C-22 (vocabulary), making three aspirationals structurally co-located.

4. **Collapse failure named as recognizable error** — `"GROUNDED in Origin-of-Claim = taxonomy collapse"` converts C-27 from an instruction to an auditable error pattern. The author recognizes a mistake by its name rather than by re-reading a rule.
