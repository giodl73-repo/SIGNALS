**Above-ceiling detail -- Step V-B Protocol V completeness verification:**

Step V-B adds a completeness audit table after Protocol V's registry (Step V-A) that:
1. Enumerates all nine Protocol II gates (Phase 0 through Phase 5 incl. Phase 3b)
2. Confirms every output-dependent gate (Phase 2a through Phase 5) has a Protocol V registry entry
3. Explicitly justifies why initiation gates (Phase 0 and Phase 1) require no entry: they consume external parameters, not prior-phase output
4. Produces a "Completeness result" declaration: "Protocol V is exhaustive. No output-dependent gate has been omitted from the registry."

**Bidirectional referenceability**: Protocol II cites Protocol V (one-directional, required by C-46); Protocol V Step V-B accounts for all Protocol II gates (reverse direction). The dependency structure is auditable from either document.

**Explicit no-entry justification**: Absence with justification ("Phase 0 is the initiation gate consuming external input only; no Protocol V entry required") is structurally distinct from bare absence, closing the question of whether initiation phases were accidentally omitted vs. correctly excluded.

**Gap C-46 leaves open that Step V-B closes**: C-46's minimum (Phase 3b->Phase 3 + at least two other dependencies) allows an incomplete registry to pass. A registry naming three dependencies while omitting four others satisfies C-46. Step V-B requires Protocol V to account for every Protocol II gate -- either with an entry or with an explicit no-entry justification.
