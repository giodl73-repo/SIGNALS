## Excellence Signals — V-05

**1. Meta-enforcement: name the failure mode you're guarding against**
V-05 Step 4 includes: *"if Falsified appears only as a row within the Step 3 chain category — this step is not satisfied."* Prior variations said what to do correctly; this one also says exactly what incorrect compliance looks like. Naming the failure mode makes it structurally difficult to rationalize a shortcut.

**2. Double-gating: constraint appears in both heading and body**
Step 2 heading says "required before Step 3 — do not skip"; body says "Complete this step in full before proceeding to Step 3." The redundancy creates two independent attention triggers — a model must miss both to skip synthesis.

**3. Universal applicability marker on mandatory framing**
Step 4 writes "Mandatory framing — include this regardless of classification state" before the framing sentence. This preempts abbreviation under LOCKED state, where models may reason "nothing to celebrate, so skip the frame."

**4. Verbosity risk was falsified**
The R2 hypothesis that "full stack prompt may degrade essential compliance" did not hold. This establishes that the three mechanisms (synthesis gate, enforcement clauses, Falsified isolation) are compositionally safe — they can be combined without crowding out the essential structure.
