### Excellence Signals

**From top-scoring variations (V-01, V-04, V-05)**:

**E-01 -- Bidirectional asymmetry framing closes the structural argument.** V-02 and V-03 state only the CANNOT-side ("cannot be written before the merge is complete") -- a cautionary warning. V-01, V-04, and V-05 state both sides: the CAN-side ("A Y/N integration status CAN be assigned in the same cognitive pass... before the merge has been executed") makes the contrast explicit and structural rather than merely cautionary. The asymmetry IS the argument; one side alone is a partial claim.

**E-02 -- Rationale header naming the constraint type primes bidirectional structure.** V-01, V-04, V-05 title the block "*Why quoted evidence rather than Y/N -- structural asymmetry:*". The header signals that an asymmetry -- two contrasting sides -- will follow. V-02 and V-03 use "*Why quoted evidence rather than Y/N:*" without naming the constraint type, which does not prime the CAN-side statement.

**E-03 -- C-29 ceiling is independent of C-28 and C-30.** V-04 demonstrates that adding Pass 3 asymmetry (C-29) and Amendment 1 search space block (C-28) at structurally independent lifecycle positions is fully additive with no interference. V-05 adds C-30 without cost. All three ceiling criteria are orthogonal across their lifecycle positions (pre-Phase 0, Pass 3, Amendment 1).

**E-04 -- V-05 adds a process-level impossibility claim beyond V-01 and V-04.** V-05 Pass 3 includes "completing all four columns in a single pre-merge pass is structurally impossible -- the extract column cannot be filled until after Phase 3 is complete." This converts the argument from a cognitive constraint ("you cannot write what doesn't exist") into a process constraint ("the workflow cannot be executed out of order"). V-01 and V-04 do not have this; it is a distinguishing addition but does not affect C-29 scoring since the criterion passes with the asymmetry statement alone.
