## Excellence Signals from V-05

**1. criterion-cascade-by-schema**
A single table schema choice simultaneously satisfies multiple criteria. Pre-declaring Rogers rows in Table 5 causes C-09 (% per segment), C-14 (listen-adoption as distinct section), and C-15 (all five named segments) to pass by construction — one schema decision, three criteria. Adding Status-Quo Workaround and Switching Cost as columns in the Top Blockers table causes C-16 (inertia citation) and C-09 (segment anchor) to cascade-pass. The insight: structure the schema so each column or row type enforces a *criterion cluster*, not a single check.

**2. required-columns-as-inertia-enforcement**
Encoding "Status-Quo Workaround" and "Switching Cost" as required table columns (not prose instructions) makes the switching cost comparison systematic across all blockers simultaneously — enabling cross-blocker inertia analysis that prose cannot surface. The column makes the workaround comparison machine-readable and forces completeness: every row must have a workaround, not just "the blockers where the author remembered to mention it."

**3. pre-declared-rows-as-completeness-gate**
Using Rogers segments as named required rows (not just a list to write about) turns segment absence from invisible prose omission into a visually detectable empty table row. This is a generalization of C-11's table-skeleton pattern: skeleton applies to *rows* as well as *tables* — a missing segment appears as a blank mandatory row, not as absent content.

## What Separated V-05 from V-02/V-03

V-02 got C-11 and C-12 but missed C-15 and C-16 because its tables had correct *column* structure but no pre-declared *row* structure — Rogers segments were still prose to fill in. V-03 got C-16 and C-09 via inertia framing but missed C-11, C-12, C-15 because it had no structural format. V-05 combined both axes and added the Rogers row pre-declaration, achieving criterion-cascade on all 8 aspirational criteria plus all essential.

The binding constraint across V-01–V-04: **C-15 (Rogers rows) + C-16 (inertia columns) are structurally incompatible with either pure-gate or pure-inertia framing**. Only table-first format can hold both simultaneously.
