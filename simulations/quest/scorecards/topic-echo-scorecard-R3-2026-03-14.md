## Excellence Signals from V-05

**1. A unifying principle prevents friction between mechanisms.** V-05's risk prediction was that four mechanisms would pull in different directions. They did not, because all four serve the same reader (the newcomer stranger). CF gate: include only what the stranger couldn't discover themselves. Claim voice: assertions the stranger can act on. Per-item cap: synthesized tightly enough for a single reading. Newcomer framing: explained without project context. When mechanisms share a root, they reinforce each other.

**2. Newcomer framing extends the value of the CF field.** V-04's "Why passive observation missed this" field embeds proof that active sensing was necessary. V-05's addition requires this explanation to be in language accessible to the stranger — not team-internal shorthand. The CF field now serves two purposes: it proves the sensing was necessary AND it explains the mechanism to someone who didn't run the investigation. One field, two audiences, zero additional words.

**3. The fourth culling filter is load-bearing.** V-05 adds "Fail anything the stranger cannot understand without reading the source artifact" to Step 2's culling list. This filter operates at the inclusion decision stage, before the author writes anything — it is harder to bypass than a post-write quality check. It closes the gap between C-08 PASS at the structural level and C-08 PASS in actual output.

**4. Rule framing makes mechanisms memorable.** Rules 1/2/3 are numbered and named, giving authors a mental checklist they can recite without re-reading the prompt. "Did this pass Rule 1? Am I following Rule 2 here? Is this under 120 words?" Numbered rules convert mechanism knowledge into execution habits.

## Cross-Variation Patterns (New in R3)

**A single unifying reader orientation resolves multi-mechanism friction.** When all rules point to the same person, apparent conflicts between mechanisms dissolve — each rule becomes a different facet of serving that reader, not a competing constraint.

**Counterfactual at culling ≠ counterfactual in artifact.** Filter C in V-03 applies the inertia test during selection. This earns C-10 PARTIAL, not PASS. The gap: the culling test is internal; the schema field is external and reader-visible. Earning C-10 PASS requires the author to write the counterfactual reasoning into the artifact, not just use it to decide what to include.

**Per-item cap is the mechanically precise form of C-11.** A document-level 800-word ceiling earns PARTIAL because it permits internal imbalance — one 350-word surprise can dominate. The 120-word per-item cap earns PASS because it enforces synthesis at the item level, where synthesis is actually required.
