### EXCELLENCE SIGNAL ANALYSIS — V-01 Distinguishing Patterns

**Pattern 1: Two-level activation declaration (SETUP → C-33)**
V-01's activation instructions provide two layers: trigger identification ("C-07 fires, so cascade fires") and evaluation consequence ("C-31 is NOT auto-PASS; grouping must be evaluated"). V-02's status-only declarations satisfy presence but produce an ambiguous SETUP audit trail — a scorer cannot distinguish "auto-PASS'd" from "not evaluated" without consulting design notes. The two-layer structure is the minimal self-contained declaration.

**Pattern 2: Formula-anchored variation design (Step 1.4 → C-34)**
V-01 commits to formula-derived predicted deltas before any variation is opened, creating a falsifiable pre-commitment. V-03's qualitative descriptors ("minor", "moderate") are unverifiable after scoring — no formula exists to contradict them. The formula anchor transforms the axis summary from narrative intent to testable prediction.

**Pattern 3: Orthogonality of SETUP-instruction quality vs. diagnostic-question depth**
V-01 is the only variation where SETUP instruction quality (C-33, C-34) and diagnostic question depth (C-35, C-36) are simultaneously at PASS. V-02/V-03 confirm that SETUP instructions can be compressed without degrading diagnostic questions; V-04 confirms that diagnostic questions can be truncated without degrading SETUP instructions. This clean orthogonality enables single-axis isolation in future ablation rounds.
