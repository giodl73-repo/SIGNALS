## Excellence Signals from V-05

**What made V-05 uniquely better:**

1. **Three-role separation (EXTRACTOR/ANALYST/AUTHOR)** — splitting artifact reading from pattern analysis from authoring is the key move. Prior variations merged extraction into the ANALYST role, which blurred the boundary between "having read the signals" and "having analyzed them." EXTRACTOR completion is a pre-condition for ANALYST work; ANALYST completion is a pre-condition for AUTHOR work. This closes C-24 more completely than two-role designs.

2. **Multi-stage consistency via structural redundancy** — requiring evidence posture and recommendation verb to appear at *both* the ANALYST PATTERN BLOCK and Beat 5 makes divergence between analysis and narrative self-revealing without a manual comparison. Prior variations named claims once; V-05 names them twice at structurally paired positions. This is what unlocked C-20.

3. **Non-substitution applied at Beat 3 AND Beat 5** — extending the rule to the recommendation beat means the pattern-to-recommendation bridge in Beat 5 must be written independently, not borrowed from the pattern block. This is what moved C-11 and C-17 from PARTIAL to PASS.

4. **Four-section audit organized by concern type** — auditing Role Boundary Compliance, Analytic Completeness, Non-Substitution, and Multi-Stage Consistency as separate named sections forces the author to engage with *why* each requirement exists, not just whether it was done. A flat checklist with 11 items (V-04) is easier to treat as boilerplate; a sectioned audit requires domain awareness.

**Persistent gaps across all variations** — C-12 (decision-cost annotation per uncertainty item) and C-13 (named decision-maker in recommendation) remain at PARTIAL for all. These are not addressed by pre-composition or consistency mechanisms — they require explicit prompt instructions about decision context and cost direction per uncertainty entry.
