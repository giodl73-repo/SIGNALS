## Excellence Signals from R7

**Signal 1 — V-02: Field-ID annotation creates an auditable derivation chain at the item level**

V-02's Required Labeled Fields Inventory with F-01–F-08 row IDs, cross-referenced in checklist items as *(F-01)* through *(F-08)*, provides a level of C-27 traceability that V-01's axis-preamble approach does not. In V-01, the derivation is asserted ("the checklist was produced by a single pass"). In V-02, the derivation is traceable — each checklist item names its inventory source. The structural difference: any future auditor can verify C-27 compliance by checking that each checklist item has a corresponding inventory row, without reading the procedure description. This is a stronger form of C-27 satisfaction than V-01 provides — it demonstrates the procedure from the artifact itself rather than from a statement about the procedure.

**Signal 2 — V-04: Axis registration must be section-location-aware for distributed-field designs**

V-04 is the first variation where a single design axis contributes fields to different sections (Inertia-framing: `**Prior position**:` in Baseline, `**Baseline distance**:`/`**Signals establish**:` in Recommendation). Its axis table records field-section pairs, not just field names. This distinguishes V-04's axis table from V-01's: V-01's table lists fields per axis, V-04's table lists fields per axis per section. The new design rule: when a design axis contributes fields to more than one section, the registration sweep must record the section location for each field — otherwise the checklist cannot verify field placement, only field existence. C-26 compliance for distributed-field axes requires section-location-aware registration.

**Signal 3 — V-05: Semantic consolidation satisfies C-27 by reducing the inventory, not by extending the checklist**

V-05 proves that C-27's guarantee can be achieved by consolidating semantically related fields into one labeled field rather than by adding separate checklist items for each semantic component. The consolidated `**Position delta**:` field carries three semantic components via internal sub-labels (Prior: / Delta: / Established by:), but only one required labeled field appears in the inventory and one locate-and-find item in the checklist. The sub-labels are template prompts, not required labeled fields. This establishes a design principle: the grain at which C-24/C-25/C-26/C-27 operate is the bold-labeled output field, not the internal structure of a field's value template. Consolidation is a valid C-27 strategy when the consolidation decision is made at field design time (not as a checklist shortcut after fields are orphaned).
