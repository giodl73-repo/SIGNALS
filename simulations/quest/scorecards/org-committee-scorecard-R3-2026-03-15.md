## Excellence Signals from Top Variations

### Signal 1: Investigation-to-voice citation chain — labeled findings that participants must cite by label

Both V-02 and V-05 (but not V-01 or V-03) require Tier 3 voices to "explicitly cite at least one investigation finding" — in V-05, this means citing by label ((a), (b), (c), (d)). This creates a chain: investigation produces labeled artifacts → voice instructions specify which participants must cite which labels → reader can verify the citation. This is stronger than "acknowledge switching costs" because it creates a specific reference obligation that can fail audibly. It also makes C-12 and C-09 mutually reinforce C-06: the investigation's (d) non-obvious finding appears in the participant record because voices are required to carry it.

### Signal 2: Gate-check on investigation quality — not just fill the field, but pass a surprise test

V-02 and V-05 both include: "Does (d) name something that would surprise the proposal author? If no — revise the investigation." V-03's pre-check has "revise if you cannot find one" but lacks the explicit surprise-author framing. This gate operationalizes C-09 in the investigation phase rather than relying on the model's judgment during voice generation. The gate is the mechanism that makes C-09 reliably PASS rather than PARTIAL — it moves the non-obvious requirement from a hope to a checkpoint.
