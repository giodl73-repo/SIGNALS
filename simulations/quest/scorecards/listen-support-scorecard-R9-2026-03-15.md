### Excellence Signals

The top-completing variation (V-05) introduces four structural patterns not present in R8 V-05 or measurable by any v9 criterion. These are the signals for v10 criterion extraction:

**Signal 1 — Body-Generation-Site Consequence-Form (C-30 candidate)**
STEP 6, the actual body-writing site, carries the consequence-form clause inline in the template instruction. Prior variations carried consequence-form at *planning* sites (STEP 3B, STEP 4) but STEP 6 was only permission-form. V-05 closes this asymmetry: the site where VM Row IDs are physically placed in ticket headers now carries the criterion-level failure declaration at the moment of placement. Pattern: every site that places a compliance artifact carries the consequence if it's misplaced.

**Signal 2 — Manifest-Header Enforcement Restatement (C-31 candidate)**
The VERIFICATION MANIFEST section header restates the consequence-form declaration in addition to its structural description. Prior form: "this manifest carries C-20 count row and C-21 per-item evidence rows." V-05 form: that + "An output with any vocabulary ID on a subline fails C-20 regardless of other compliance -- verify via the C-20 count row below." The manifest becomes dual-purpose: it simultaneously counts compliance (evidential role) and restates the rule (enforcement role). Pattern: verification artifacts carry the rule they verify.

**Signal 3 — Direct-Site Consequence-Form Independence (C-32 candidate)**
STEP 3B and STEP 4 under R8 V-05 carry consequence-form *via RESTATING POSITIONS back-references that reproduce the clause*. V-05 makes the clause present as direct text at the site body — the back-reference and the direct text coexist, doubly anchoring the rule. Under v9, both forms satisfy C-29 (the clause is "reproduced at the site" in both cases). But V-05's direct form adds *independence*: a scorer can verify the rule from the site text alone, without tracing through RESTATING POSITIONS. Pattern: consequence-form at a site should not require a registry lookup to be complete.

**Signal 4 — Full-Registry Consequence-Form Coverage (C-33 candidate, integration)**
RESTATING POSITIONS under R8 V-05 names four governed sites; V-01 exposes that STEP 6 enforces but is unregistered. V-04 closes the gap; V-05 inherits the closure. When all consequence-form enforcement sites are registered, the registry functions as a complete map: no site is enforcing silently. V-04 first achieves this; V-05 confirms it in the full-synthesis context. Pattern: if a site carries consequence-form, RESTATING POSITIONS should name it with a consequence-form entry — the C-28 analog for body-generation sites.
