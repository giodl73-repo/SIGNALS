## Excellence Signals

**ES-04: Table column adjacency is an equivalent C-13 path to per-card vertical adjacency.**
V-01 and V-04 prove that wide unified table satisfies C-13. Template operators have a genuine
choice: per-card (verbose, no truncation risk, natural home for C-14 trigger fields) vs. table
(denser, readable at a glance, same structural coverage). Both paths score identically.

**ES-05: Explicit mandate and implicit trigger produce identical rubric scores.**
V-02's "Inertia-break condition:" label and "must cite named anchor, not a time period" do not
move the rubric needle relative to V-01's implicit "transition trigger" field. The distinction
is production robustness: implicit trigger allows a disciplined model to produce correct output;
explicit mandate forecloses the literal-but-wrong answer for models that answer the field
without structural discipline. Choose based on deployment scale, not score optimization.

**ES-06: STATUS QUO quantification mandate at source eliminates the need for a reminder at
consumption -- chain runs once cleanly.**
V-05's STATUS QUO: "This value is required in 'Not building this means:' below. Do not write
a vague estimate." Plus "Not building this means: ...costing approximately ($ from STATUS QUO)
... Do not write a generic statement." Enforcement at both ends: source cannot be vague,
consumption must reference the source. Stronger than a reminder only at consumption. Pattern:
when two aspirational criteria share a traceable chain (C-11 -> C-12), enforcing the chain at
both ends is more robust than enforcing only at the downstream point.
