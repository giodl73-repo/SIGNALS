## Excellence Signals from V-05 (Top-Ranked)

**E-01 — NORM label as a cross-phase identifier**
The NORM-A/B/C label functions as a named entity that propagates through four output
locations: Gate 0-B declaration -> Phase 1 grid citation -> CHAIN-3 draft -> Phase 2
output. Label mismatch at any checkpoint is detectable without reading surrounding prose
— a structurally new signal property distinct from C-16 (column-structural compliance).
Upgrades C-17 enforcement from "gate is present" to "gate is traceable end-to-end."

**E-02 — Skeleton gate at the mid-lifecycle boundary**
The PRE-MORTEM CHAIN CHECK closes a structural gap R4 V-05 leaves open: a syntactically
complete Phase 1 grid does not guarantee Phase 2 contains the pre-mortem risk. Inserting
a three-checkbox skeleton gate at the Phase 1->2 boundary blocks Phase 2 content
generation until the chain is confirmed. Same pattern as Phase 0->1 gate, applied to
the inertia->challenge->risk lifecycle link.

**E-03 — Role-level C-17 obligation over grid-level**
Requiring explicit pre-designation of the Inertia Challenger with rationale and assigned
NORM label transfers C-17's "organizational normalization" framing from a general
instruction (any participant may challenge) to a named commitment (this role, this NORM
item, this rationale). The designated challenger's grid cell is not "at least one
non-None in the grid" — it is a required non-None for a specific named participant,
making the obligation addressable per row rather than satisfiable by any convenient
participant.

## New Patterns (v6 Candidates)

**C-18 (candidate) — NORM-* labeled normalization inventory**
Inertia framing uses a multi-item labeled checklist (NORM-A, NORM-B, optionally NORM-C),
each naming a specific normalized belief distinct from the rubber-stamp outcome, each
independently trackable through Phase 1 challenge citations and Phase 2 risk connection.
Satisfied by: V-01, V-04, V-05.

**C-19 (candidate) — PRE-MORTEM CHAIN CHECK skeleton gate**
A three-checkbox skeleton block between Phase 1 and Phase 2 confirms: (1) challenger
identified, (2) outside-in basis stated with non-circular justification, (3) risk drafted
connecting to the inertia gate assumption. Phase 2 content generation blocked until all
three boxes appear in output.
Satisfied by: V-02, V-04, V-05.

**C-20 (candidate) — Designated Inertia Challenger with rationale and NORM assignment**
One participant pre-designated before Phase 0 as the Inertia Challenger, with rationale
explaining why their charter is least likely to share the normalized assumption, and
assigned to a specific NORM label. Their Inertia Challenge? grid cell is a required
non-None citing the assigned label. Phase 2 outside-in basis derives from the stated
rationale.
Satisfied by: V-03, V-05.
