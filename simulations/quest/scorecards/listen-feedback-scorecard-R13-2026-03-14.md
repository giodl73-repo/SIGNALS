## Structural Ranking (tie-break by robustness)

With identical rubric scores, rank by structural innovation and guardrail strength:

1. **V-03** — Numbered field manifest + completeness enforcement rule. Omissions are structurally visible. Strongest A-10, A-16, A-19 language. Only variation that declares malformed cards as protocol violations.
2. **V-05** — Behavioral coaching against one-sided justifications ("This is where most simulations go wrong"). Most direct A-20 prohibition. Status quo competitor + conversational register is the highest-friction guardrail against feature-enumeration in gains/losses.
3. **V-02** — Step 0 status quo competitor with four structured sub-fields creates the most explicit inertia baseline of any variation. Gains/losses as named deltas from a characterized competitor.
4. **V-04** — Phase lifecycle with purpose statements. PM-after-aggregate is the cleanest separation of design critique from strategy verdict. "Expected output" rehearsal per phase.
5. **V-01** — Clean baseline fix with both professional reads after aggregate. Least structural novelty but all criteria satisfied.

## Excellence Signals from Top Variations

**V-03 — Numbered field manifest with completeness enforcement:**
- Replacing prose card templates with a numbered field table makes omissions structurally visible: a card missing Field 3 has a visible gap in the sequence. Structural detection beats semantic detection.
- "Any card missing one or more manifest fields is malformed" — protocol-level failure condition rather than style guidance.
- Explicit "Separate labeled field from field 4 — not parenthetical within the NPS value" (A-16) in the manifest constraints table is the most instruction-level precision across all variations.

**V-05 — Failure-mode coaching:**
- "This is where most simulations go wrong — they skip the cost side. Don't skip it." — Pre-emptive behavioral correction is stronger than a structural requirement for preventing one-sided justifications.
- "If you find yourself writing a justification that only talks about what the spec does well — stop. Ask yourself: what are they giving up?" — Interruption framing forces cost-side reconsideration before completion.
- "if you only name one change you haven't ranked anything" is the most direct articulation of why a single-item output fails A-20.

**V-02 — Status quo competitor section (shared with V-05):**
- Step 0 names the current approach with four structured sub-fields before any persona runs. Gains/losses automatically write as deltas from a characterized baseline rather than as feature enumerations.
- "Floor-level switching cost" sub-field establishes a minimum switching cost that every persona inherits — simplifies per-card losses analysis without reducing specificity.
