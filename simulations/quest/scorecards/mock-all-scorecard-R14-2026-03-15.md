## Excellence Signals from V-05

**Signal 1 — Stop-gate provenance anchoring for skeleton (gate-level C-29 enforcement)**
V-05 ROLE 1 STOP includes: *"must be the verbatim seed phrase from the list above, not a paraphrase"* — the gate doesn't merely check that the skeleton cell is non-empty; it cites the authoritative template list and prohibits paraphrase. This makes C-29 provenance enforcement machine-readable at the gate level, not only at the instruction level.

**Signal 2 — Stop-gate provenance anchoring for rationale (gate-level C-30 enforcement)**
V-05 ROLE 2 STOP includes: *"with the pre-authored rationale above copied verbatim"* — the gate explicitly names the pre-authored rationale block as the required source and directs verbatim copy. This is structurally parallel to C-28's self-annotation of the column-name list, but applied to the rationale-authorship chain.

Together these two signals form an **authorship-enforcement pair at the stop-gate level**: both authorship-residency fields (C-29 skeleton, C-30 rationale) have their provenance enforced at the gate level. The gate does not merely verify completeness — it also binds completion to the template's authored content. This pattern is not yet captured as a criterion; C-29 and C-30 govern whether the template authors the content, but neither requires the stop-gate to enforce provenance explicitly. This gate-level provenance anchor is a candidate for a future aspirational criterion.
