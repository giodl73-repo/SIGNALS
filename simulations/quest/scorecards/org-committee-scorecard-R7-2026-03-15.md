## Excellence Signals

### From V-05 (top scorer, 125/126)

**Signal 1 — Compliance by transcription outperforms compliance by discipline.**
When `INVESTIGATION-ATTEMPT-N`, `SORT-CHECK: Test: / Result:`, and Phase 0–5 headers are pre-printed template slots rather than expected outputs, a missing field is a visible structural gap rather than an omission requiring reviewer inference. V-05 demonstrates that carrying all accumulated structural labels (C-15's `STANCE:`, C-20's `CITE:`, C-21's `RESPONDS-TO:`, and the three new C-24/25/26 slots) into pre-printed positions collapses 17 aspirational criteria from discipline problems into transcription problems.

**Signal 2 — Golden synthesis must include C-12's content axis, not just structural axes.**
V-05's single partial (C-12) reveals that the pre-printed template approach covers structural enforcement criteria but not content-framing criteria. The template guarantees audit trails, gate declarations, and phase structure — but does not mandate switching-cost investigation as the investigation's frame. A future v8 golden synthesis should embed a required investigation framing directive (`INVESTIGATION-FRAME: switching-cost audit` or equivalent) as a pre-printed template slot alongside the structural slots.

### From V-03 (second place, 122/126)

**Signal 3 — Named investigation findings as committee-record artifacts propagate through four criteria simultaneously.**
V-03's `INERTIA-FINDING-A/B` tags are the most leveraged innovation in Round 7. A single structural choice — naming switching-cost findings as labeled committee-record entries — satisfies C-12 (switching-cost investigation), C-17 (advocates ground in investigation), C-20 (`CITE:` structural), and C-21 (`RESPONDS-TO:` specific) in a single pass, because advocates must name a tag (not an inferred connection) and challengers must quote or paraphrase a named tag (not a generic concern). This is the structural enforcement pattern for C-12 that C-16 provided for C-09. A new criterion formalizing `INERTIA-FINDING` tags as named committee-record entries would close this loop.

## New Pattern Candidates for v8

Two patterns from R7 exceed what the current rubric enforces:

**Candidate C-27: Switching-cost findings carry named investigation labels (e.g., `INERTIA-FINDING-A/B`) that become mandatory citation targets in advocacy.**
- C-12 requires switching-cost investigation to occur; C-27 would require the findings to be named committee-record entries that advocates cite by tag name rather than by inference.
- Enforcement pattern: parallel to C-16 → C-09 and C-25 → C-11/18.

**Candidate C-28: Template-slot compliance — all structural labels are pre-printed in the simulation output's skeleton, making missing fields visible gaps.**
- No current criterion requires the simulation to produce a skeleton with pre-printed structural slots; the current criteria verify that labels are present but permit them to appear anywhere.
- A pre-printed template converts 17 structural criteria from "reviewer must verify presence" to "reviewer identifies blank fields."
