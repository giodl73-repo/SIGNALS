### Excellence Signals (Top-Variation Patterns Not Yet Formalized)

**Signal 1 — C-51 per-artifact form: artifact-ID-bound return routing**

V-03 and V-05 use `IF Q3 SHOWS N, RETURN TO Q3 (SECTION 1A)` where the artifact ID from the gate's Y/N table row is directly bound to its named return target in parenthetical section form. This eliminates a lookup step that joint-stage routing (`Return to Stage 2A or 2B if either shows N`) requires: the joint form asks the author to select the right target; the per-artifact form delivers the target without selection. The pattern is: `IF [ARTIFACT-ID] SHOWS N, RETURN TO [ARTIFACT-ID] ([SECTION-LABEL])` — the gate row artifact ID, the gate body artifact ID, and the return target section ID form a triple-threaded resolution chain from the same base identifier.

**Signal 2 — C-52 three-source heading-title form: complete evidence sourcing set**

V-05 names all three sections containing evidence for PROVISIONAL revision: `SECTION 1 (FM INVENTORY), SECTION 2 (WORKAROUND PROFILE), AND SECTION 3 (SWITCHING COST ANALYSIS)`. V-04 names two (the workaround and switching cost stages). The three-source form is notable because it names the FM inventory stage — the section containing the FM-[N] primary keys that anchor the entire referential chain. Without FM-[N] evidence from Section 1, PROVISIONAL assessments of switching resistance and threat magnitude lack a traceable structural basis. Naming Section 1 as an evidence source makes explicit that FM data feeds the PROVISIONAL revision, not just cost and workaround data. The three-source form closes the evidence loop that two-source leaves open at the FM anchor.
