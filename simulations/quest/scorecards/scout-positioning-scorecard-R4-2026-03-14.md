## Excellence Signals from V-05

**1. Category defensibility test converts assertion to proof.**
R3 V-05 scored 9/10 on C-03 because the category was "derived not proven" -- named and sourced,
but never tested against competitors. V-05's DEFENSIBILITY TEST iterates through every named
competitor and inertia, returning YES/NO with evidence per row. ACCEPTED requires all rows NO.
Any YES row forces a revised proposal and re-test. The iterative rejection loop cannot end with a
category already owned by a named competitor. The structural move: test after propose, not instead
of propose -- the category is formed in EXECUTE, then challenged in FINDINGS C-03.

**2. Degradation-path-first makes C-09 always substantive.**
R3 V-05 scored 9/10 on C-09 because "prior run loaded -> N/A." V-05 reframes C-09 as an
intelligence record: when loaded, it records what was confirmed -- competitor positions grounded,
category defensibility rows validated, whitespace dimensions confirmed clear, claims elevated from
[Source: inline] to [Source: artifact]. This is positive evidence rather than absence evidence.
The "Degradation-path rule" is declared in PREAMBLE before SETUP, enforcing always-populated
behavior from the first phase. C-09 becomes useful on every execution.

V-01, V-02, V-04 each close exactly one gap (+1 point). The clean tie confirms the gaps are
independent: fixing C-03 doesn't affect C-09 and vice versa. V-04 adds verbatim-preamble on top
of V-01 and scores identically -- the behavioral ceiling was already locked. The R4 ceiling is
reachable only by stacking both content fixes, which V-05 does.

**4. V-03 confirms behavioral ceiling is fully locked in the R4 baseline floor.**
V-03 produces the strongest preamble of any variation (quoting rubric text for C-18 and C-19
before declaring mechanisms) yet scores 188 -- same as the raw R3 V-05 projection. This

variations. The remaining 2 points are entirely content. The behavioral and content ceilings are
