### Excellence Signals (from V-04)

**Signal 1 — Rationale-as-principle elevation**
V-04 promotes the C-14 rationale from embedded rule-body prose to a first-class named preamble principle. The rationale sentence ("a hypothesis declared before evidence is an anchor masquerading as a prediction") is referenceable by name alongside CALIBRATION RULE, FALSIFICATION RULE, etc. This makes the *why* as auditable as the *what*.

**Signal 2 — Sequencing rule scope extension via role reversal**
By running Intel before Web, V-04 demonstrates that SEQUENCING RULE governs relative evidence-stage ordering — not only hypothesis placement. The handoff from Web Searcher to Intelligence Analyst becomes a sequencing-governed boundary, forcing the access-scope declarations to explicitly prohibit each stage from reading ahead. This extends the rule's enforcement surface beyond the evidence-to-hypothesis boundary.

### New Patterns (not yet criteria)

**From V-05 — Named inertia competitor in decision readiness**
V-05's inertia framing names the status-quo option explicitly in the decision readiness section: "Do nothing / maintain [X]" appears as a discrete named comparator. C-09 requires a posture statement; C-12 compresses it to one sentence. Neither criterion requires naming the option being foregone. A named inertia competitor converts the decision readiness verdict from an absolute claim ("Ready to proceed") into an explicit binary choice ("Ready to proceed over [inertia option: maintain current X]"), making the decision context legible to a reader who hasn't followed the campaign.

This is a candidate C-39.
