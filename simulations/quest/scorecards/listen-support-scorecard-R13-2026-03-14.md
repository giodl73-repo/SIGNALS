### Excellence Signals: What Made V-04 and V-05 Ceiling Variations

**1. Full four-criterion coverage through non-competing structural locations.**
C-36 and C-37/38/39 operate at entirely different structural positions (Part C exception block vs. Step 4 gate header). Adding C-36's Paraphrase clause to the exception block does not interact with, dilute, or conflict with the dual-field gate mechanism. The four criteria form a clean two-location stack: Step 4 gate delivers C-37+C-38+C-39; Part C exception block delivers C-36.

**2. Scope confirmation as gate closure, not gate replacement.**
V-01 demonstrated that the scope confirmation statement ("Confirmation: this rule applies to every severity assignment in the table below.") can follow the dual-field gate without displacing either labeled field or the "both lines" language. The scope confirmation is a declarative closure, not a labeled field — C-38 counts labeled fields, so the scope confirmation is transparent to C-38's pass condition. V-04/V-05 carry this proof forward with all four criteria present.

**3. C-36 independently satisfiable (V-02 evidence).**
V-02 shows that the Paraphrase clause field in the exception block and the no-exception path paraphrase quotation work correctly in the complete absence of a verbatim anchor at Step 4. C-36 and C-37 are truly orthogonal: the exception block mechanism does not require a verbatim anchor to function, and the verbatim anchor at Step 4 does not require the exception block to carry a paraphrase field.

**4. Conversational register does not break structural gates (V-03/V-05 evidence).**
V-03 satisfies C-37 and C-38 in a descriptive register ("Both of those lines need to be written" = structural equivalent of "do not fill the table until both lines are written"). V-05 carries this further: all four criteria survive full conversational register + inertia framing gates. Register is confirmed neutral to the labeled-field gate mechanisms.
