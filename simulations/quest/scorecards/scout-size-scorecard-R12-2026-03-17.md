## Excellence Signals (from V-05, the top-ranked variation)

**Signal 1 — Comprehensive write-time vocabulary fencing (C-36 seed)**
Every vocabulary-constrained field header carries a `[FAIL: ...]` tag — not just C-01/C-02 (Complexity, Timeline) but also Confidence Level, Cost Direction, Cost Trajectory, Tier-Destination (both sensitivity rows), and FTE. C-35 minimum creates a partial compliance surface; V-05 closes every remaining gap. A model filling in Cost Trajectory cannot produce a prose directional statement when the column header reads `[FAIL: directional statement without rate ("getting worse" alone fails) — quantified rate required]`.

**Signal 2 — Quantified Cost Trajectory as forecasting field (C-37 seed)**
The inertia table adds a fifth field: `Cost Trajectory [quantified rate: "+~N%/quarter", "~constant", "declining ~N%/year"]`. Cost Direction (v12 C-34) tells you the trend; Cost Trajectory tells you the velocity. "More expensive and worsening at +~20%/quarter" has different investment urgency than "more expensive but stable." PHASE 0 SEALED also confirms Cost Trajectory is present, preventing silent omission. This converts the inertia check from a status signal to a forecasting signal.

**Signal 3 — PHASE SEALED structural gates (C-38 seed)**
All three phase transitions have explicit SEALED close-out blocks with named-field checklists (Phase 0: 5 items; Phase 1: 9 items; Phase 2: 4 items). Verbal handoffs ("Phase N complete. Proceed.") allow silent phase incompleteness — a model can read "Phase 1 complete" and produce Phase 2 with an empty Complexity Tier. Sealed blocks make incompleteness structurally visible: each deliverable is named with a checkbox, and the phase gate is declared closed only when all items are confirmed. Combined with write-time [FAIL:] tags, this creates a two-layer prevention system: field-level (write-time tags prevent vocabulary violations) and phase-level (sealed blocks prevent completeness violations).

**Signal 4 — Examiner voice (institutional register)**
V-05 shifts from second-person imperative ("You are running...") to third-person institutional ("The Sizing Engineer produces..."). This register change positions the prompt as a specification the output must satisfy rather than instructions the model is following. At maximum encoding density — where dozens of structural constraints compete for attention — specification-satisfier orientation may improve compliance consistency.
