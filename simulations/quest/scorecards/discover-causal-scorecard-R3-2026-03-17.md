## Excellence Signals — V-02

**1. AUTHORIZED SCOPE as a named required field (C-14 full pass)**
V-01 produces an inertia block but the scope adjustment is embedded in a Phase 2 instruction. V-02 promotes scope to a first-class artifact field in Artifact 1 with three explicit legal values. A model filling in the block cannot omit scope without leaving a blank field — visually unacceptable. This is the structural upgrade that turns "inertia check before trace" into "named scope declaration before trace."

**2. Table format for causal chain makes mechanism omissions structurally visible (C-08 reinforcement)**
By converting the mechanism trace from prose into a table with a required "Mechanism (named process)" column, V-02 makes bare assertions ("→") visible as empty cells. Prose traces can hide thin causation in fluent sentences; tables cannot.

**3. Multi-row MARGINAL CONTRIBUTION block enforces C-16 multi-axis dimensioning**
V-01 requires MARGINAL CONTRIBUTION as a single-line field. V-02 requires it as a multi-row block: "dimension 1, dimension 2, dimension 3 if applicable." The structure forces the model to decompose the marginal claim into independent axes rather than collapsing it to one phrase.

**4. Row-validity rule in Artifact 3 enforces C-15 completeness**
"A row with an empty cell does not count toward the minimum" means two rows with missing WHEN TO OBSERVE cells fail the minimum, not just reduce quality. This rule converts the column requirement from a style preference into a structural gate.

**5. Ascending artifact gate ("may not produce Artifact N+1 until Artifact N is complete") composes with content requirements**
The gate in V-02 applies to all five artifacts uniformly. Combined with per-artifact content requirements (scope, table completeness, row validity), the gate ensures each artifact's output is structurally complete before the next begins — not just written in order.
