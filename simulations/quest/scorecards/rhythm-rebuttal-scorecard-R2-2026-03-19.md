### Excellence Signals

Patterns from the R2 top-scoring cluster that distinguish these variations from R1:

1. **Constraint-first register (V-04, V-05)**: Moving all binding rules into a pre-workflow RULE-N block ensures constraints are in active context before generation begins. A constraint embedded mid-skill in a rules section can be encountered after the violation has already occurred. Pre-workflow placement has no such gap.

2. **Amendment-predictive forecast (V-03)**: Predicting which concerns will produce defensive, over-conceding, or under-addressed responses before writing any response creates a self-monitoring frame. The author commits to weakness awareness first; the amendment pass then checks whether predictions held. This makes C-08 a closed loop rather than a post-hoc observation.

3. **Dialogue exchange format (V-02, V-05)**: Structuring each response as a discrete exchange (REVIEWER SAID / AUTHOR RESPONDS / MANUSCRIPT OUTCOME) makes concern merging structurally impossible -- two concerns in one paragraph cannot share one REVIEWER SAID block. Severity-tier partitioning becomes natural rather than imposed.
