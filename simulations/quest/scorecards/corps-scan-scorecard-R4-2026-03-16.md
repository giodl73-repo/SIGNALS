## Excellence Signals (R5 candidates)

The four R4 structural invariants (C-17/18/19/20) are all satisfied uniformly. Differentiating patterns that exceed the current rubric ceiling:

**P-1 — Post-write ownership role separation** (V-04 only)
V-04 assigns the post-write verification (C-19) to a *structurally separate named role* (Verification Auditor) distinct from the YAML producer (Schema Drafter). V-04's C-19 text calls this out explicitly: "owned by the Verification Auditor role — structurally separate from the Drafter that produced the YAML." In all other variations, the post-write is produced by the same role/section that wrote the YAML — the self-labeling is accurate but not independently owned.

**P-2 — Cascading C-20 re-confirmation at every downstream phase entry** (V-05 only)
V-05 doesn't just gate Phases 1–4 on Phase 0's completion once — it re-states `[ ] C-20 -- Compliance Officer (Phase 0) complete STATUS: CONFIRMED` at the ENTRY GATE of Phase 1, Phase 2, Phase 3, and Phase 4. The compliance gate is distributed rather than concentrated. A model skipping Phase 0 would fail every subsequent entry gate, not just one.

**P-3 — Requirements Register as cross-phase criterion-to-section traceability table** (V-05 only)
V-05's Requirements Register (`REQ-N (C-NN): ... Satisfying section: Phase X`) maps each criterion to its satisfying phase before any phase work begins. This is a different structure than the pre-check SCHEDULED items in V-01–V-04: it's a forward-reference table, not a checklist. The satisfying section is declared by name, not just committed to.
