### Excellence Signals from V-05

**Signal 1: Named structural blocks create natural citation addresses.** V-01's CHAMPION FORMATION EVENT is a labeled block with a stable, memorable structural identity. The TRACEABILITY AUDIT Row Citation format supports `[PHASE 2 / Month 3 / CHAMPION FORMATION EVENT]` in addition to `[PHASE N / Month M / Row K]`. A named block is more traceable than an anonymous row because it carries semantic identity — auditors can find it without counting rows. The implication: any simulation event that must be cited downstream should be a labeled block, not an inline field in a phase-close ledger.

**Signal 2: Two independent verification paths for the same coverage claim.** V-05 covers C-15 via two mechanisms: TRACEABILITY AUDIT (source citation) and SCHEDULE RECONCILIATION (schedule obligation). Neither mechanism can silently omit a role without the other exposing the gap. This "dual-path audit" pattern means a deficient simulation fails two distinct checks rather than one, making coverage fraud structurally expensive. The pattern generalizes: any criterion whose failure is hard to detect should have two structurally independent checks, not one.

**Signal 3: Pre-commitment converts traceability from audit to obligation.** V-03/V-05's committed adoption month converts C-08 coverage from "did I mention every role?" (post-hoc audit) to "did I execute the schedule I declared?" (pre-declared obligation). Obligation-first design makes omission a visible deviation rather than a silent gap. The SCHEDULE RECONCILIATION makes any omission a named discrepancy, not an absence. This is the structural analog of hypothesis-first in experimental design: commit the prediction before running the simulation.
