## Excellence Signals (from V-05)

**1. Labeled rationale triple components as structural markers**
V-05 uses `- CONTAMINATION RISK:` and `- ISOLATION PURPOSE:` as explicit labeled bullet items within each lifecycle framing entry. This elevates C-46 from a prose paragraph criterion to a structural marker criterion: the rationale triple can be verified by scanning `CONTAMINATION RISK:` and `ISOLATION PURPOSE:` labels alone, without reading the body text. The form creates a fifth marker type (alongside the four C-48 types) that is scannable by label.

**2. Thread terminal obligation pre-declarations binding terminal discharge**
Both thread declarations in V-05 close with explicit "Thread N terminal obligation: [description]" clauses. This creates a named preamble element that pre-commits to the exact form of the CLOSE COMPLETE multi-criterion confirmation before any container executes. The terminal discharge becomes architecturally inevitable not just because both threads are co-primary (C-47 floor) but because both threads explicitly name their discharge obligations in the preamble — making omission a contradiction of the document's own named commitments, not just a structural inconsistency.

**3. Three-section preamble architecture separating lifecycle justification, thread architecture, and marker inventory**
V-05 creates three distinct preamble sections: `## Competitor Lifecycle Framing` (C-43 + C-46), `## Document Thread Architecture` (C-45 + C-47), and `## Structural Marker Reference` (C-48). Each section addresses a different structural layer: what the lifecycle states mean and why they exist, what the threads are and what they require at the terminal, and where every marker type lives and what form it takes. The three-section structure separates concerns that earlier variations co-located, making each section independently verifiable by scanning its own markers.
