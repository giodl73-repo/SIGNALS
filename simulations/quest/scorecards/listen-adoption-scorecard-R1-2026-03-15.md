### Single-Axis Diagnosis

**V-01 (Format)** closes role completeness and quantification but leaves causal gaps open. Tables force *what* but not *why*.

**V-02 (Lifecycle)** closes temporal completeness and chasm structure but leaves role-level coverage vulnerable — cohort labels absorb individual roles.

**V-03 (Inertia)** is the weakest standalone: it adds causal specificity to C-03/C-04/C-09 but does nothing for role enumeration, S-curve numerics, or month pinning. It depends on the other axes to function.

**The axes are complementary, not redundant.** V-01 closes role/quantification gaps; V-02 closes temporal/chasm gaps; V-03 closes causal gaps. None of the three covers the same rubric surface.

### Excellence Signals from V-05

**Signal 1: Causal column on the champion table**
The "Incumbent argument countered" column transforms champion listing from passive network description into active displacement mechanism. C-09 (cross-archetype influence dynamics) was the hardest criterion — every prior variation left it at PARTIAL. This column is the only structural device that forced C-09 to PASS by making *why* the champion matters explicit, not inferred.

**Signal 2: Triple-axis closure maps onto three orthogonal gap types**
Format → role completeness gap. Lifecycle → temporal completeness gap. Inertia → causal specificity gap. These three gap types are independent. Closing one doesn't close the others. V-05 is the first variation with structural coverage of all three simultaneously.

**Signal 3: Incumbent as a forcing anchor across all tables**
Naming THE INCUMBENT in a PREAMBLE block and then requiring every subsequent table column to reference it created a single coherent thread through all ten criteria. Chasm cause named the incumbent advantage. Interventions named what they displace. Champions named what argument they counter. S-curve added incumbent position per phase. One forcing device propagated specificity into every downstream section.
