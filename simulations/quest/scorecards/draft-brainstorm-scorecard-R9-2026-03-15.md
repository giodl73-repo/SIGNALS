### Excellence Signals from Top-Scoring Variations

**V-03, V-04, V-05 are co-equal at 150. Each achieves the ceiling through the same two structural moves applied to different architectures (Prohibition Battery / Table / Layered). The pattern is architecture-independent.**

**Pattern 1 — Dual-Anchor Phase Gate (C-29)**

The gate condition is not a one-time declaration. It appears at schema definition (Selected? column rule / Step 2b gate block / Registry column rule) **and** is restated verbatim as the first instruction of the gated phase (Phase 3 / Step 3 / Layer 4 opening). A model beginning the finalization phase re-encounters the preconditions at the moment of transition, not only during schema setup. The key syntactic marker: *"Only after both preconditions hold — (a) ... AND (b) ..."* as the opening line of the gated phase. Without this second anchor, the constraint is read-and-forgotten before the phase boundary is crossed.

**Pattern 2 — Comparison-Sentence Incompletability as Causal Trigger (C-30)**

The inversion does not target the generic revision impulse. It targets the specific cognitive state that produces the impulse: the inability to complete the peer-comparison sentence. The formula "if editing the rationale *would make the comparison sentence completable*, that desire confirms the obligation applies" chains the revision desire to its cause. This closes the edge case where any improvement impulse might invoke the inversion. The trigger fires precisely when the comparison has failed and the model knows it.

**Pattern 3 — Architecture Independence of R9 Gains**

V-03 (Prohibition Battery), V-04 (Table), and V-05 (Layered) all reach 150 using different structural formats. Both C-29 and C-30 are phrasing properties, not format properties. The dual-anchor and causal-specificity moves can be applied to any architecture that has a gated phase and an inversion clause. No architectural preference is implied by R9 scores.
