### Excellence Signals from V-05

Three patterns in V-05 that distinguish it from all prior variates:

**1. Guard-body formal titles as cross-reference vocabulary.**
LEVEL 1/LEVEL 2 are not inline descriptors — they are named artifacts with formal titles on their own lines, capitalized in full, co-located in the guard body. This turns the guard from a prose explanation into a reference vocabulary: other sections (SECTION SCOPE, CONTRACT) can cite "LEVEL 2: MOTIVATED REASONING..." by label without restating the prose. The mutual necessity assertion ("LEVEL 1 does not protect against LEVEL 2") is now machine-readable from the label pair, not buried in a paragraph.

**2. Scope-as-navigator rather than scope-as-exclusion-list.**
Prior SECTION SCOPE declarations told auditors what is NOT in a section (exclusion framing). V-05's Phase 6 SECTION SCOPE tells the auditor exactly where to look and what the item is called — including the formal LEVEL 2 label — making the scope declaration a starting point for verification traversal, not a summary the auditor must then resolve by entering phase content.

**3. Self-attesting CONTRACT positional integrity.**
The self-referential confirmation ("No file read instruction appears before this CONTRACT") closes the positional-integrity loop from inside the CONTRACT itself. Prior variates required an external reader to confirm the CONTRACT's first position; V-05's CONTRACT attests to its own position, making positional violations detectable purely from the CONTRACT block — no frame-of-reference required.
