## Excellence Signals from V-04

**1. Mutual reinforcement of coupled criteria**
Counterfactual gate and anti-hedging are naturally coupled: a surprise strong enough to pass the inertia test (required active sensing to surface) carries enough evidential weight to assert as a claim. Combining them creates a tighter selection and expression standard than either criterion alone, and the author experiences them as one cognitive act.

**2. Mandatory "Why passive observation missed this" schema field**
Converting the counterfactual from an authorial test (internal) to a schema field (external, reader-visible) shifts the counterfactual from process compliance to institutional argument. The echo does not just pass the test — it proves in the artifact that active sensing was necessary. This is stronger than V-01's use of the same field because V-04 frames it as one of two named Rules, making it parallel in authority to the voice rule.

**3. Dual-rule framing separates inclusion from expression**
Rule 1 governs what enters the echo (counterfactual gate). Rule 2 governs how it is expressed (claim-only voice). The separation creates a clear mental model: decide if a surprise qualifies, then decide how to state it. Authors cannot conflate the two, which reduces errors on both criteria.

**4. Claim framing in schema labels (all fields)**
V-04 labels all schema fields as "stated as a claim" (Expected: "We assumed X", Found: "X is Y / X is false", Design impact: "This changes / confirms / invalidates"). This extends claim discipline beyond the surface Found field to all four fields — the schema itself is a voice contract.

## Cross-Variation Patterns

**Direct enforcement outperforms emergent enforcement on aspirational criteria**
R2's hypothesis was correct: all five variations scored higher on their targeted aspirational criteria than R1's top variations did on the same criteria as side effects. V-04 achieves C-10 and C-12 as primary targets; V-05 achieves C-11 and C-12. R1's V-03 and V-05 produced these only emergently and only cleared 2 out of 5 aspirational criteria.

**C-10 (counterfactual) requires a named schema field to earn PASS, not just a gate**
V-01 and V-04 both use the counterfactual as a culling gate. Both earn PASS for C-10. But V-04's "Why passive observation missed this" schema field is a stronger enforcement because it requires the author to externalize the counterfactual reasoning — the gate alone is internal. The distinction shows up as reader-facing proof in the artifact.

**C-08 (institutional memory) requires explicit newcomer-reader framing, not just word limits**
V-02 earns PASS for C-08 because it explicitly directs writing for "someone who was not on the team." All other variations have word limits but only address length, not accessibility. Length is necessary but not sufficient for standalone utility.
