### Excellence Signals from V-05

Two patterns appear in V-05 that are not captured by any existing v12 criterion:

**Signal 1 — Coaching language targets implementation failure modes**
V-05 adds explicit behavioral guidance ("this is where most simulations go wrong") alongside structural rules. Structural rules specify correct output format; coaching addresses why outputs fail. The distinction matters: a model can satisfy every structural constraint while still failing at the reasoning boundary (e.g., listing losses as "none" without explanation for a Detractor-tier persona). Coaching closes that gap by naming the expected failure mode in advance, changing internal reasoning rather than just output shape. No existing criterion rewards this; the current rubric is entirely structure/format/depth-oriented with no axis for behavioral coaching.

**Signal 2 — Extended numbered aggregate manifest extends positional verifiability to the aggregate section**
V-02/V-04/V-05 apply the A-23 insight (numbered manifest + enforcement rule) to aggregate fields (A1–A4), making aggregate field omission detectable by counting rather than semantic parsing. This parallels exactly what A-23 achieves for persona cards, but for the aggregate section. The variation summary flags this as a v13 candidate; the R14 data confirms it appears systematically across three variations as a structural discipline choice, not incidental formatting.
