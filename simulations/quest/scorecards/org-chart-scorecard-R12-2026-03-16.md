## Excellence Signals (V-05 patterns beyond rubric ceiling)

V-05 achieves 240/240 and closes both new criteria, but three structural patterns within the ARTIFACT-HANDOFF INVENTORY go beyond C-38's minimum requirements:

**ES-1 — Production-step provenance pinning**
C-38 requires naming the artifact and the consuming gate step. V-05 adds a `Produced by: [step name]` sub-field to each inventory entry (e.g., "Produced by: Step 0.2 ROLE-TYPE-CLASSIFICATION"), making provenance traceable at step granularity rather than gate granularity. The inventory entry in C-38 names what is handed off; ES-1 names precisely where it was made.

**ES-2 — Operational dependency rationale per transition**
V-05's `Required by:` sub-fields explain the causal dependency rather than just naming the consumer. Example: "GATE 2 (structural sections only begin if VERDICT = STRUCTURE-WARRANTED)" explains WHY the downstream gate needs the artifact. This turns the inventory from a cross-reference into a behavior specification — each entry encodes the gate activation condition, not just artifact lineage.

**ES-3 — Authority hierarchy declaration on inventory**
V-05 explicitly designates the ARTIFACT-HANDOFF INVENTORY as the "authoritative cross-gate traceability reference" and declares it governs over local gate declarations if any conflict exists. Local "Named artifact handoff" lines cross-reference the inventory rather than standing independently. This creates a defined resolution rule for ambiguity, preventing specification drift between the consolidated block and gate-local prose.

These three patterns could form C-39 in v13, targeting a two-tier artifact handoff block: one tier naming artifact + production step + consumption dependency (ES-1 + ES-2), one tier declaring authority over distributed gate-local declarations (ES-3).
