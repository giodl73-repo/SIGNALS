### Evaluation Framework

**Base:** All five variations derive from V-05 R16 (295/295 — C-01 through C-49 at ceiling). The R17 changes are additive above-ceiling probes (C-50, C-51, C-52). **Evidence:**
- **C-50 (V-01, V-04, V-05):** Sub-check 3 replaced with a 4-column table (Phase / Bodies generated / Committed (Step 5) / Step 1 target / Match). Row count is self-verifying without sentence parsing — a MISMATCH in Phase 2 is a cell value, not a clause to extract from prose. Exact structural independence mechanism as C-15.
- **C-51 (V-02, V-04, V-05):** Entry 3 gains `Trigger event:` field. Sub-check 2 requires verbatim quote of the trigger event. The field value is independently falsifiable: a day-one action in the `Trigger event:` field fails the constraint without requiring prose evaluation. Converts declarative YES/NO confirmation into evidence-backed structural property.
- **C-52 (V-03, V-05):** Entry 3 `Prohibited:` sentinel moved from Entry-specific guidance section into the template body, field-adjacent above `Expected behavior:`. Constraint visible at the moment Entry 3's content fields are written, not retrievable from a prior instruction block. Applies C-18 spatial-adjacency mechanism to the switching-friction entry constraint.

### Excellence Signals from V-05

V-05 activates all three R17 probes simultaneously. The patterns that produced the top position:

1. **Table-row-per-phase as self-verifying count gate.** The prose Sub-check 3 form (C-48 baseline) requires parsing sentences to extract per-phase results. A 4-column table row per phase makes row count checkable by counting rows, not reading clauses. A MISMATCH is a cell value. Exact analog to the C-15 mechanism: the table eliminates the paragraph-collapse failure mode. The three-way comparison column (generated / committed / Step 1 target) closes two temporal windows simultaneously — the Step 5 commitment and the Step 1 target are both independently visible.

2. **Evidence-backed Phase-2 barrier claim via Trigger-event field.** C-49 requires Sub-check 2 to confirm "Third entry covers Phase 2 migration barrier: YES/NO" — declarative with no evidence requirement. A `Trigger event:` field forces the model to name the specific post-commitment workflow action that distinguishes Phase-2 friction from day-one friction. The field value is falsifiable at the content level: a day-one action in the field fails structurally without requiring the scorer to evaluate surrounding prose. Sub-check 2 quoting the field verbatim converts a claim into a traceable string.

3. **Spatial-adjacency of Entry 3 Prohibited: sentinel.** Moving `Prohibited:` from the guidance section into the Entry 3 template body — field-adjacent, before `Expected behavior:` — applies the C-18 mechanism to switching-friction entries. The constraint is visible at the exact moment the constrained fields are written. Guidance-level prohibitions require retrieval from a prior instruction block after multiple content fields intervene. The per-template placement makes the scorer's check structural: find the Entry 3 template, confirm `Prohibited:` appears before `Expected behavior:`.

4. **Composability without interference.** All three probes are structurally independent (Sub-check 3 format, Entry 3 template body, Entry 3 extended template field). V-05 demonstrates that improvements targeting different failure surfaces — post-generation drift detection, barrier-type evidence, spatial-constraint adjacency — coexist without breaking any of C-01 through C-49.
