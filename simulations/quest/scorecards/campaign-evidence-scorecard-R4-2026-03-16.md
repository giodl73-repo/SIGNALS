## Excellence Signals — patterns from V-04/V-05 not yet in rubric

**Signal 1 — Dual enforcement as a feedback loop**: V-05 combines preventive coverage map (specifies expected invocations before execution) and retrospective audit table (verifies actual invocations after completion). Neither mechanism alone closes the loop: the map can be ignored mid-execution; the audit can be filled performatively. Together, the map creates an obligation and the audit creates a verifiable record — any mismatch between them is a gap the model must correct before submitting. C-16 captures the desired outcome (zero-gap invocation) but does not require the dual-mechanism architecture that makes the outcome structurally guaranteed.

**Signal 2 — Inertia position as decision-status frame**: V-05 introduces an INERTIA POSITION at the brief's opening — a declared prior belief about decision direction — and carries it through the findings table (inertia column), synthesis, and a closing inertia verdict. This recontextualizes the entire brief as a decision-status artifact, not just an evidence collection. It adds a dimension (decision trajectory: confirming inertia vs. breaking inertia) that strengthens C-04 and C-08 but isn't captured by any existing criterion. A brief that explicitly tracks whether evidence shifts the prior is a qualitatively different artifact from one that only records what was found.
