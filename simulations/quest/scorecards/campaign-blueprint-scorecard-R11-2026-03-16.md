### Excellence Signals from Top-Scoring Variants

**Signal 1: Item 0 ordering as hypothesis-first enforcement**
All three 203 variants place CAMPAIGN CONVICTION HYPOTHESIS before the INERTIA MODEL entity. This prevents the most common campaign failure mode: declaring the INERTIA MODEL mechanically (filling in Name/Behavior/Cost) without naming the inertia problem first. Item 0 forces the model to name what it is betting against before defining the evidence entity that documents it. The structural position — item 0, not a section header, not a preamble — is what earns C-33.

**Signal 2: Three-part falsification isomorphic with conviction types**
The a/b/c falsification conditions in V-03/V-04/V-05 map directly onto the three conviction types: (a) spec=factual documentation, (b) proposal=optionality pricing, (c) pitch=emotional translation. The campaign hypothesis is falsifiable by the campaign's own artifact set. This isomorphism is what separates "a falsifiable hypothesis" from "a framing preamble" — the falsification conditions are already the artifact production criteria, not external tests.

**Signal 3: Structural enforcement vs instructed behavior — ceiling neutrality**
V-01 (citation direction table for C-31) and V-02 (sub-skill column for C-32) both exceed the rubric minimum in structural precision — they enforce compliance per cell rather than per instruction. Both hold at FULL for their criteria, not above. The v11 ceiling is stable at 203: the rubric evaluates whether the structural element is present and correctly specified, not how precisely it is implemented. Tighter implementation earns no additional credit unless a new criterion names that tighter form. C-34 will need to be defined for the citation direction and sub-skill forms to unlock more points.

**Signal 4: Minimum density confirms structure over volume**
V-05 reaches 203 at compressed form. The scoring engine is structural: does the named element appear, does it have the required components, does the double-prohibition have both arms? Explanatory prose surrounding the structural elements is rubric-neutral. This confirms that future rubric criteria should be specified as structural elements (named sub-fields, columns, tables, templates) not as density thresholds.
