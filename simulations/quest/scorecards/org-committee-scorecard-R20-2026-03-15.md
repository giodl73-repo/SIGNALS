### Excellence Signals from R20 Top Scorers

**Pattern 1 — Cross-axis symmetry ENFORCE with explicit criterion attribution and asymmetric-coverage consequence**

All three 172/172 variations place an ENFORCE instruction immediately after the STANCE INVARIANT VALIDATE that: (a) explicitly names the parity requirement (both INVARIANT VALIDATE blocks must carry identical (a)/(b)/(c) axis labeling), (b) names the criterion number (C-47), and (c) describes the consequence of violating it ("leaves the vote-anchor manifest seal asymmetrically under-tested"). This pattern elevates C-47 from a structural observation to a named blocking rule. V-05 has the most complete form, citing "both sealing contracts guard equally critical commit-sealed manifests; neither may be tested less rigorously than the other." The parity ENFORCE appears in three locations in the top variations: after STANCE INVARIANT VALIDATE, after PHASE-3-COMMIT bidirectionality VALIDATE, and in the SYNTHESIZE/BRIDGE VALIDATE — one ENFORCE per cross-instance symmetry requirement.

**Pattern 2 — Two-axis SYNTHESIZE/BRIDGE VALIDATE with independently labeled axis names as contractual violations**

R19 V-01 had two FAILS entries in SYNTHESIZE CHECK but neither was labeled as an independent axis — they appeared as sequential entries without explicit axis enumeration. R20 top scorers restructure this as `FAILS (structural absence):` and `FAILS (incorrect affirmation):` — parallel axis labels matching the `(a)/(b)/(c)` structure on INVARIANT VALIDATEs. The ENFORCE then states both axes are "independently required" and names the silent failure mode that the second axis closes: "a SYNTHESIZE step that answers YES regardless of charter composition passes the structural test while defeating the continuity guarantee." This makes YES-without-qualification a named rule violation with its own label, not an advisory concern.
