## Excellence Signals

**Why V-02 and V-03 reached 100 while V-01 and V-04 stalled at 95:**

The gap is entirely C-07. V-01 and V-04 use role identity framing to enforce generation ordering — which works (C-11 PASS, all structural criteria pass) — but provide no depth guidance for artifact bodies. "`{artifact body — apply MUST use / DO NOT use exactly}`" is sufficient for vocabulary compliance but not for plausibility. V-02 adds `{3-5 sentence artifact body}` as a concrete floor. V-03 adds the inertia frame ("what blind spot would be absent if this namespace were skipped?") as a semantic quality anchor. Both achieve plausible bodies through different mechanisms.

**Why V-05 fell to 98.9:**

The skeleton-stub approach (pre-printed columns with `[fill in]` placeholders) introduces C-17 risk. Once the vocabulary rules are in a downstream Vocabulary reference block — even when MUST/DO NOT column stubs are present — C-17 fails by the explicit rubric rule. V-03 and V-04 demonstrate that pre-filling vocabulary in columns is both the structurally clean and rubric-safe approach.

**V-02 vs V-03 (tied at 100):**

Different quality mechanisms, same score. V-02's "3-5 sentence" is explicit and portable. V-03's inertia framing may produce deeper, more namespace-specific bodies — the hypothesis from R5 is that inertia priming beats a word count — but both satisfy the plausibility bar. This is a quality question, not a rubric question; neither variation surfaces a gap at v5.
