## Excellence Signals — V-05

V-05 achieves ceiling on all three candidate criteria simultaneously. The patterns that made it the top scorer:

**1. Phase-dimension extension of role table (C-58)**
The two-column ROLE ALLOCATION TABLE (C-53) and 4-column audit table (C-55) verify per-role totals. V-05 extends the table to a phase-dimension matrix, adding Pre1/Ph2/Ph3 columns to both the pre-declaration (Step 3A) and the post-generation audit (Part B item 4). The same structural argument that C-45/C-48 used for phase-distribution counting applies to the role dimension: phase-concentration per role is detectable in a column scan without cross-referencing card bodies. The mechanism is a direct dimensional extension of an existing table, not a new mechanism.

**2. All-phase symmetry in verbatim verification (C-59)**
C-57 applied verbatim-quote + concreteness-check to Phase 2 only. V-05 recognizes that Phase 1 and Phase 3 templates are equally susceptible to abstract fills — "I still have [tool] open" is a template fill that can be satisfied generically. Extending verbatim quotes to all phases with phase-differentiated properties (not a uniform checklist, but tailored properties per phase) closes selective-phase-verification as a failure mode. The phase-differentiation is load-bearing: different phases have different structural guarantees (dual-tool tension vs. partial migration vs. parity gap), so the properties checked per phase must differ accordingly.

**3. Table-over-prose for property confirmations (C-60)**
C-57 confirmed concreteness properties as three prose YES/NO assertions per ticket. V-05 converts Phase 2 assertions to a 5-column table. The structural argument is the same as C-50 (which converted Sub-check 3 from prose to table) and C-15 (which converted coverage enumeration from prose to table): a prose assertion "specific workflow and blocking event both named: YES" can collapse two properties. A YES/NO cell per column makes each property independently checkable with no possibility of a two-property cell. Row count self-verifies all Phase 2 tickets are independently audited.

**4. Compatible composition (V-05 vs V-04)**
V-04's combination of V-01 + V-02 shows that C-58 and C-59 compose without conflict — the cross-matrix at Step 3A and all-phase verbatim quotes in Part B item 2 operate on entirely separate structural locations (Step 3A vs. Part B item 2 Sub-item 2). V-05 adds C-60 on top: the Phase 2 concreteness table is nested inside the all-phase verbatim structure (Phase 2's "Quoted opening sentence" becomes the second column in the table, while Phase 1 and Phase 3 remain prose). The nesting is structurally coherent and doesn't degrade C-59's all-phase mechanism.
