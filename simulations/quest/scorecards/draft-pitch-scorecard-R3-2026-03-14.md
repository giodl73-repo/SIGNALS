## Excellence Signals (R3)

The rubric ceiling is saturated. The following sub-rubric patterns distinguish structural quality within the Platinum band and are candidates for C-16/C-17 in v4:

**ES-01: Consumes/Produces phase metadata (V-04)**
Explicit `*Consumes: X*` and `*Produces: Y*` declarations at each phase boundary create a dependency graph readable from phase headers alone, without reading body text. A reviewer (or model) can verify the pipeline is intact by scanning only the phase metadata. This is structurally stronger than narrative "Step N references Step M" instructions because it makes violations visible at the header level. No prior analog in R1 or R2.

**ES-02: CTA-level inertia counter with "Instead of" format (V-03)**
Requiring each CTA to name the specific inertia argument and counter it explicitly ("Instead of [inertia argument], [action]") extends C-09 from a positioning assertion in the exec Why It Matters section to a per-audience persuasion structure in every version's call to action. The "Instead of X, do Y" format is verifiable by inspection — a CTA without this structure fails. This is a qualitatively different enforcement form than naming inertia in a positioning statement.
