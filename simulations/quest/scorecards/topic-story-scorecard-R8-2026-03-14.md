### Excellence Signals

Four patterns emerge from the top-scoring variations that exceed the current pass bars:

**Signal 1 (from V-01, C-28)** — *Checklist invariant as navigable block with repair operation*
V-01 promotes the checklist invariant from inline prose to a named block with a heading ("Checklist Design Invariant"), a blockquote, and a repair instruction: "must be rewritten as a structural presence check before it is used." The heading creates a scan anchor — an auditor locates the invariant without reading all preamble. The repair instruction names the corrective action for violations, not just the pass condition. C-28 fires on whether the invariant is stated; no current criterion fires on whether it is navigable or whether violations have a named repair path.

**Signal 2 (from V-02, C-30)** — *Disproof cross-reference as required output field (third axis)*
V-02 introduces `**Revision condition**:` as a required Section 4 output field via a registered "Cross-reference axis." The structural closure is field-to-field: the recommendation sentence references a local field whose definition points back to Section 2's `**Disproof condition**:`. This makes the cross-reference verifiable as an artifact output element (checklist item 13 locates the field label) rather than as a sentence-form instruction the auditor must read to verify. C-30 fires on the sentence form's cross-reference; no current criterion fires on whether the cross-reference is elevated to a required artifact field.

**Signal 3 (from V-04, C-30)** — *Recommendation sentence closes both measurement axes simultaneously*
V-04 extends the C-30 sentence form to cross-reference both `**Prior position**:` + `**Baseline distance**:` (inertia-framing axis) and `**Disproof condition**:` (falsifiability axis): "...because [**Pattern claim**] establishes a [**Baseline distance**] from [**Prior position**], forcing this verdict rather than [adjacent alternative]. This call stands unless [**Disproof condition**] is shown to hold." The verdict is conditioned by both measurement anchors in the same required slot. C-30 fires on the falsifiability cross-reference alone; no current criterion fires on the compound form that closes both axes.

**Signal 4 (from V-05, inventory)** — *Structural Role column makes criteria auditable from inventory alone*
V-05's "Structural Role" column annotates each field's participation in template-level criteria: F-06/F-07/F-08 are marked "Inertia measurement — required separate in S4 (C-29)"; F-03 is marked "Cross-referenced in S4 sentence form (C-30)." An auditor can verify C-28, C-29, and C-30 compliance by reading only the inventory — without consulting section templates. The inventory is not just a field registry but a complete structural specification. No current criterion fires on whether the inventory carries these meta-structural annotations.

V-01/V-02/V-03/V-05 satisfy C-30 but not C-33. V-04 satisfies both.
