## Excellence Signals — V-04 / V-05

The two 90/90 variations share three structural properties that single-axis variations could not reach:

**1. Multi-gap combinations close the denominator ceiling.**
Each new v5 criterion (C-19, C-20, C-21) costs ~0.77 aspirational points if missed. A single-axis variation can close one gap but cannot compensate for the denominator expanding from 10 to 13. The combination approach is mandatory to reach 90 under v5.

**2. Definitions-first enforcement makes aspirational criteria verifiable.**
V-04 and V-05 both state tiebreaker protocol (C-20), SYSTEMIC phase format (C-21), and atomic block structure (C-19) in the *definitions section* — before any execution step. This means a scorer can check compliance from the schema alone, not just from output inspection. Single-axis variations that only apply a criterion in the consolidation step are fragile.

**3. Two reinforcement points per new criterion (V-05 advance).**
V-05 adds a second reinforcement for each criterion: definition AND execution. While the rubric does not score redundancy, double-reinforcement reduces partial-credit risk when execution partially satisfies a criterion — it eliminates "PARTIAL" as a plausible verdict and locks in PASS. V-04 achieves the same 90/90 but with single-point enforcement; V-05 is the more durable scaffold for future rubric versions.
