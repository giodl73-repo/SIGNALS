### Excellence Signals

**From V-05 (top-scoring):**

1. **Inertia label per phase boundary** -- "Prevents: [specific failure mode]" as an italicized subhead under each phase name. Different from STOP CONDITION (which blocks forward progress after failure): the inertia label provides causal framing *before* instructions execute. The model knows why the phase exists, not just what to do in it. Candidate for C-16.

2. **Inertia label on the rewrite trigger itself** -- "The default tendency to 'proceed and note the issue later' produces contaminated experiments that cannot be compared." Extends C-12's named trigger into a diagnostic: the model knows why it's being stopped, not just that it must stop.

**From V-03 (experimental control):**

3. **Intentional degradation as scientific control** -- R3 is the first round to produce a variation explicitly designed to measure what's removed rather than to score well. The hypothesis predicts not just that degradation occurs, but *where* (by variation position: V-04 and V-05 under token pressure). V-03 is a calibration artifact for the rubric itself: if C-10/C-12 are load-bearing, V-03 should perform worse in practice runs. No previous quest-variate round used a variation as a measurement instrument against another variation.

**From V-04 (cross-axis prediction):**

4. **Hypothesis predicts secondary effects across axes** -- V-04 explicitly models the interaction between phrasing register and structural compliance: "increases compliance with the spirit... while reducing compliance with the letter of structural gates." A hypothesis that predicts behavior on an axis it doesn't vary reveals understanding of skill design dependencies. This is a higher-order falsifiability pattern beyond what C-05 currently captures.
