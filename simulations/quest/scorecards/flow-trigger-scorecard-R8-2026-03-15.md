## Excellence Signals from V-01

**Signal 1 — Structural artifact sequencing produces auditability as a side effect.**
The Scope Arbiter's three locked artifacts (SCOPE GATE, EXCLUSION LOG, PROHIBITION CONTRACTS) exist as named, citable objects before enumeration starts. The CLOSURE CHECK in Role 4 can reference them by name: "PROHIBITION CONTRACTS issued? AC-1 on record? EXCLUSION LOG row count matches denominator?" Closure counters become verifiable because the artifacts they count against are guaranteed to exist. No other variation produces this property — V-03 has prohibition blocks per phase but they emerge during execution rather than being locked before enumeration begins.

**Signal 2 — Role-level prohibition contracts with breach markers externalize enforcement.**
`[PROHIBITION BREACH: Role N | action]` is a runtime signal a reader can spot in a completed document. This is different from a rubric criterion or an instruction — it is a named anomaly marker that surfaces violations without requiring the reader to re-check every criterion. Combined with the AC-1 clearance gate, it makes protocol violations visible in the output itself rather than only in the scoring rubric.

**Signal 3 — Global ALL-SLOTS-REQUIRED MANDATE issued once by a gating role collapses per-entry obligations.**
Rather than repeating "Condition (Skipped) must be populated" in C-16, "Side Effects must be populated" in C-17, etc., the Arbiter issues one structural rule that governs all subsequent roles. This is the ceiling of the per-entry obligation family (C-15–C-19): instead of checking five criteria, a scorer checks one mandate and then verifies slot presence in tables. V-02 also achieves C-22 but via format discipline rather than a named mandate artifact — making it harder to audit and less durable under variation.
