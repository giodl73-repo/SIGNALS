## Scoring Methodology

The trace artifact is placeholder, so scoring is structural: each variation is a prompt design. The question is whether each prompt structure *reliably produces* outputs that pass each criterion. All five variations inherit R7 V-05 as the base, so C-01–C-26 are scored against that established floor. C-27 is the discriminating dimension.

C-27 requires three independently testable properties:
- **P1** Tool-name fidelity (STATUS QUO names inertia competitor by product name)
- **P2** Phase-differentiated body templates (P1 stance ≠ P3 stance, pre-printed)
- **P3** Switching-friction gap reframe (gap inventory names competitor as migration barrier)

## Excellence Signals from V-05

**1. Single-root structural anchoring**
Embedding the competitor into the performance-mode declaration makes all three C-27 properties derive from one stance rather than three parallel requirements. Properties enforced by posture cannot be dropped without breaking the prompt's operating premise.

**2. Verification block as output gate**
PASS 2 triple-property check converts aspirational criteria into structural constraints on the final output pass. The model cannot "complete" without explicitly verifying C-27 P1/P2/P3, eliminating the silent-failure mode where criteria are structurally present but not satisfied in the output.

**3. Phase-keyed severity as emergent property**
When adoption-curve position is embedded in the performance-mode stance, severity calibration (C-06) becomes a derived output rather than an independent judgment. This also reinforces C-11 (volume/severity coherence) through the same mechanism with no additional instruction.
