### Excellence Signals (V-05)

V-05 satisfies all three v9 ceiling criteria simultaneously, with no structural interference across the three independent lifecycle positions (pre-Phase 0, Pass 3, Amendment 1). Two patterns distinguish V-05:

**E-01 -- Process-level impossibility amplifies C-29 above minimum compliance.**
The two-sided asymmetry (CAN/CANNOT) is the C-29 pass threshold. V-05 adds a third layer: "completing all four columns in a single pre-merge pass is structurally impossible." This converts the argument from descriptive (the text cannot exist) to procedural (the workflow cannot complete). A reader who understood the two-sided argument abstractly now sees that the card structure itself enforces sequencing -- the impossibility is architectural, not merely epistemic. V-04 shares this formulation and also passes C-29; it is the synthesis pattern established in R8.

**E-02 -- Named-per-path gate self-contains the closure audit.**
The anonymous gate ("All three paths closed. Proceed.") requires back-scanning to verify which paths were checked. The named-per-path gate ("Signal-skip: closed. Nominal gap framing: closed. Non-trivial gaming: closed.") embeds the canonical terms at the closure event itself. The gate line functions as a checklist summary: a reader auditing the gate confirms coverage without re-reading the per-path confirmation lines. This is the C-32 pattern; V-03 also implements it in isolation.

**Confirmation finding**: No new ceiling behavior was discovered in R9. V-05 R8 satisfies all three v9 criteria as written. The rubric closes cleanly.
