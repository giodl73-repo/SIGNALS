### Axis-Specific Delta Analysis

#### V-01 (Axis A: PART 2 expansion displacement state)

Adds `Incumbent position at this chasm element:` mandatory field to each CHASM-A/B/C EXPANSION subsection. This extends C-45's phase-close ledger into expansion slot depth. **No existing v16 criterion covers expansion-slot displacement accounting** — C-45 is phase-close only, C-42 is inertia-carry only. The new field targets **C-48 candidate**; does not shift any existing score.

#### V-02 (Axis B: PART 4 SLOT-KEY: champion rows)

Adds PART 4 header contract + `SLOT-KEY: [Role] — champion entry` row prefix. Gate I now references "SLOT-KEY: champion entries" explicitly. This is the fourth application of the row-level re-assertion pattern (TABLE 3 → C-38, TABLE 1 → C-43, PART 3 → C-46, PART 4 → this axis). **No existing v16 criterion covers PART 4 SLOT-KEY: prefix** — C-38/C-43/C-46 cover TABLE 3, TABLE 1, and PART 3 respectively; PART 4 is the gap. Targets **C-47 candidate**; does not shift any existing score.

#### V-03 (Axis C: SECTION A Downstream Citation Contract)

Adds DOWNSTREAM CITATION CONTRACT block after SECTION A inertia table. Gate H now audits against declared contract rows (I-01 through I-05, each with named downstream citation positions) rather than free-form scan. C-13 requires IDs in four location types — baseline PASS is maintained; V-03 makes the audit more rigorous by tying the source (SECTION A) to sinks (phase ledgers, PART 2, PART 3, PART 4, PART 5) via declared contract. **No existing v16 criterion tests for a DOWNSTREAM CITATION CONTRACT**. Targets **C-47 candidate** (distinct formulation from Axis B); does not shift any existing score.

#### V-04 (Axes B + C)

Combines PART 4 SLOT-KEY: rows + SECTION A citation contract. Both C-47 candidate mechanisms co-present. SECTION I references "SLOT-KEY: champion entries"; SECTION H audits against contract rows. The combination means: champion citations are enforced at row-generation time (Axis B) AND the contract declaring where champion citations must appear is visible at SECTION A generation time (Axis C). Two C-47 candidate mechanisms in one document. No shift to existing scores.

#### V-05 (Axes A + B + C)

Full combination. Expansion displacement state (C-48 candidate) + PART 4 SLOT-KEY: (C-47 candidate Axis B) + SECTION A citation contract (C-47 candidate Axis C). Most structurally complete. The DOWNSTREAM CITATION CONTRACT in V-05 carries an extra load: I-03 contract row now lists CHASM-A EXPANSION as a required citation location — and V-05's Axis A adds a displacement state field in the same subsection, making the expansion slot a three-field structural slot (Named Inertia in effect + Incumbent position at this chasm element + prose body), with the first two both contractually required. No shift to existing scores.

### Excellence Signals from V-05

**Signal 1 — DOWNSTREAM CITATION CONTRACT closes the source-to-sink gap**
SECTION A currently assigns IDs and provides structural reason. Downstream citation enforcement happens at the point of use (Q-BARRIER, Q-CHAMPION, Q-TRIGGER, Q-INTERVENTION). The citation contract makes the full sink map explicit at SECTION A generation time: the model writing I-03 sees immediately that it is required in PHASE 3 Q-BARRIER, PHASE 3 ledger, CHASM-A EXPANSION, PHASE 4 Q-BARRIER, PHASE 4 ledger, PART 2 bridge condition, and at least one PART 5 row. SECTION H then audits against contract rows rather than performing a free-form scan. This closes a structural gap where a deficient SECTION A inertia description would only be caught downstream, not at assignment time.

**Signal 2 — PART 4 SLOT-KEY: completes the four-table re-assertion progression**
The SLOT-KEY: row-level contract re-assertion pattern first appeared in TABLE 3 (C-38), was extended to TABLE 1 (C-43), then to PART 3 (C-46). V-02/V-04/V-05 complete the progression to PART 4. Gate I now audits "SLOT-KEY: champion entries" explicitly — the same enforcement-visibility pattern that C-46 mandates Gate J to reference "SLOT-KEY: churn entries." The progression is architecturally complete across all four structural tables.

**Signal 3 — PART 2 expansion displacement state makes the chasm arc traceable at slot depth**
C-45 places displacement accounting at phase-close boundaries (six ledger entries). C-42 carries the inertia ID into expansion slot depth. V-01/V-05 add `Incumbent position at this chasm element:` per CHASM-X EXPANSION slot, making displacement accounting structurally active at the chasm-element resolution layer — not only at the phase boundary six phases away. In V-05, where the DOWNSTREAM CONTRACT also requires I-03 in CHASM-A EXPANSION, the expansion slot carries three co-present structural fields: inertia re-citation (C-42), displacement state (C-48 candidate), and prose diagnosis — making the expansion slot the most structurally complete slot in the document.
