**Signal 1 — Pre-output FORMAT CONTRACT as schema-first declaration**
Declaring all 6 table schemas before any content is generated closes format drift across every structural element simultaneously. This is qualitatively different from ROLE-NAME LOCK (which covers permitted names only). By committing the Anti-Pattern Watch column layout — including the `[element name] (cat-N) —` open syntax — before the section is reached, C-09 becomes auto-compliant by construction rather than by prose discipline. Schema-first thinking applied beyond roles to every column spec and field format.

**Signal 2 — Three-layer mechanical enforcement stack**
V-05 achieves full compliance by stacking three enforcement layers, each closing a distinct failure mode:
- Layer 1 (central declaration): FORMAT CONTRACT + ROLE-NAME LOCK with downstream checklist — commits all schemas at output start
- Layer 2 (distributed activation): per-section ROLE-LOCK-CHECKs naming specific columns/fields — re-activates the constraint at the point of production
- Layer 3 (embedded structural precondition): REBUTTAL FORM CONTRACT — makes ROLE UNDER PRESSURE a syntactic precondition before OBSERVABLE BREAKDOWN prose can begin

No single layer is sufficient: central declaration without distributed activation allows drift; distributed checks without embedded form still permit retrofit-role in the Rebuttal. The stack closes all three.
