### Excellence Signals from V-01

**Signal 1 -- Evidence-base vs assertion-only gate (C-35 confirmation):** The Variation Completion Manifest creates a durable, populated structural record that serves as the evidence base for the Phase 3 STOP CONDITION. The distinction from R10's Phase 3 STOP CONDITION is precise: R10's gate asserts "all 5 variation bodies confirmed complete" (declarative); R11's gate says "all 5 rows in the Variation Completion Manifest are filled and confirmed" (references artifact). The model cannot fake a populated table the way it can bypass a declarative assertion. The gate is checkable rather than claimable.

**Signal 2 -- Manifest obligation as role-constitutive duty before Phase 1:** V-01 installs the manifest obligation in the pre-Phase-1 preamble: "this manifest is your final act of Phase 2 accountability and the evidence base for the Phase 3 gate." This extends C-25's pattern (obligation framing before Phase 1 instructions) to cover Phase 2 sub-artifacts -- the preamble now carries temporal obligation across the full execution arc, not just checkpoint compliance. V-02 ablates this by omitting the manifest obligation line from the preamble, demonstrating the C-25 pattern applies to newly introduced sub-phase artifacts.

**Signal 3 -- Inertia framing extended to artifact-gate boundaries:** V-01 gives the Variation Completion Manifest its own `*Prevents:*` label ("Phase 3 entry where one or more per-variation checkpoints were noted as failed but the model proceeded anyway; assertion-only completion claims where the model states 'all checkpoints passed' without a populated structural record as evidence"). This applies the C-16/C-28 inertia-framing pattern to a sub-phase artifact gate, not only to the three main phase boundaries. Possible R12 candidate: require *Prevents:* coverage at artifact-level gates within phases, distinct from C-28 (which requires content density at the three phase-level *Prevents:* labels).
