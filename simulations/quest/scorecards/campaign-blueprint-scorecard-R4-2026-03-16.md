## Excellence Signals (from V-04, top variation)

**Signal 1 — Three-layer contract coverage.** Global matrix + per-artifact reminder + named phase gate. Each layer targets a different failure mode: the matrix catches omission at planning time, the reminder catches drift at execution time, the gate catches reflection-bleed at delivery time. No variation with two layers covers all three failure modes simultaneously.

**Signal 2 — Phase gate as structural enforcer.** Moving the double-prohibition from an inline clause ("not before, not during") to a named phase boundary (`CAMPAIGN REFLECTION`) changes the enforcement type from instructed to structural. The phase cannot start until the previous phase writes to disk — the constraint is the architecture, not a memory obligation.

**Signal 3 — Layered proximity.** Per-artifact `Contract reminder — READ: … | PRESERVE: …` lines bring PRESERVE obligations to the execution site. The matrix alone requires the model to hold multi-step context; reminders collapse the recall distance to zero at the moment of highest risk.

## New Patterns Identified in R4

**Pattern 1 — Phase-as-enforcer.** Structural phase separation (with explicit GATE) enforces timing constraints more reliably than inline instruction clauses. First demonstrated cleanly in V-01; carried through V-04 and V-05. Candidate for a v5 aspirational criterion: *phase-gate form of C-18 earns full credit; clause form earns partial.*

**Pattern 2 — Layered contract density.** Three declaration levels — global matrix, per-artifact reminder line, named post-execution block — create defense in depth. V-04 is the first variation to deploy all three simultaneously. Prior rubric versions only required one level (matrix). Candidate for v5 excellence definition.

**Pattern 3 — Conviction typing.** Adding CONVICTION TYPE (Factual/Optionality/Emotional) as a matrix row and section label creates a new structural dimension for narrative arc auditing. The typed CONVICTION DELTA question is more diagnostic than an open FINDINGS block for C-09 assessment. V-03/V-05 introduce this; may become a v5 aspirational criterion for C-09.

**Pattern 4 — Minimal density ceiling confirmation.** V-05 proves 128 is achievable without elaboration. Structural elements (typed labels, phase gates, compact matrix) determine the ceiling, not word count. This is a calibration signal: rubric ceiling should be achievable at minimum viable prompt density; elaboration may signal C-09 effort but cannot be required for C-15–C-18.
