## Excellence Signals from V-05

**Signal 1 -- Verbatim quotation forces actual retrieval**
The `Verbatim anchor from 2C: [copy it word for word, do not paraphrase]` field instruction structurally separates retrieval from re-generation. A free-restatement instruction ("restate from 2C") is satisfied by any topically consistent paraphrase produced fresh at Step 4; the model never has to reproduce its earlier text. A verbatim instruction is satisfied only if the model retrieves the exact string it generated at Step 2C. Any paraphrase drift becomes a structural failure, not just a semantic inconsistency.

**Signal 2 -- Governing paraphrase clause citation per exception**
The 5th `Paraphrase clause:` field in the exception block forces the model to trace every directional exception back to the specific clause in its own paraphrase commitment that permits or prohibits the assignment. Without this field, Grounds can say "this was an outage" without any connection to the paraphrase. With it, every exception is anchored: the model must demonstrate it knows which clause it is applying or overriding. The no-exception path also quotes the governing clause, preventing the paraphrase from being an inert preamble.

**Signal 3 -- Step 4B paraphrase echo places rule as live context at constraint 5**
C-32 solved the decay problem at Step 4 (severity column). The same problem exists at Step 4B constraint 5: the confirmed paraphrase is upstream from Step 4B by the full width of the vocabulary table. V-05's `INFERENCE RULE (4B echo): [copy the verbatim anchor text]` makes the rule text the immediately preceding generation when the model scans Phase 1/3 rows, exactly as C-32 did for the severity table itself. Two decision points are now each independently protected.

**Signal 4 -- Part C text-match chain makes divergence structurally self-exposing**
C-35 confirmed that the audit result was consistent with the paraphrase direction. V-05 elevates this to character-level: three positions (2C, Step 4, Step 4B) are quoted side-by-side with explicit MATCH/DIVERGENCE rulings for each pair. A model that produced subtly different paraphrases at any of the three checkpoints -- satisfying C-34's label-based cross-reference while silently drifting -- cannot pass the text-match check without flagging the divergence or fabricating identical text. The loop is closed: the pre-generation commitment and every downstream echo are verifiably the same commitment.
