## Excellence Signals for v8 Extraction

R7 delivers 210/210 across all five variations. The differentiation now lies entirely in structural patterns not yet captured by any criterion. Three candidates emerged, ranked by extractability:

### Pattern A — Exclusive criterion-cluster ownership per structural unit (V-04)

V-04 introduces "sole job" declarations per role: GATE ROLE's sole job is the pre-check; SCAN ROLE's sole job is the schema; FINALIZE ROLE's sole job is post-write + amend. The GATE ROLE pre-check marks C-30 and C-16+C-08 as "owned exclusively by FINALIZE ROLE" — not just SCHEDULED but exclusively assigned. No other variation distributes criterion ownership with explicit exclusivity per unit. The auditable property: given a criterion ID, a reader can name exactly one role that satisfies it without scanning the entire output.

Distinct from C-20 (ordering enforcement) and C-31 (phase naming): C-20 says a gate unit must precede others; C-31 says names must encode purpose; neither requires that each unit own exactly one criterion cluster with no shared ownership.

### Pattern B — ACKNOWLEDGED as permanent-boundary state vs. deferred-execution state (V-05)

V-05's ACKNOWLEDGED items carry explicit semantic framing distinguishing two sub-cases: C-05 ("Not in SCAN. Not in DELIBERATE. Not in DRAFT. Not in FINALIZE. Not ever. Permanent boundary; no execution commitment needed.") and C-10 ("constraint recorded; you carry it into SCAN and DRAFT.") versus the SCHEDULED items which are deferred-but-assigned.

Other variations use ACKNOWLEDGED for C-05 but do not distinguish the permanent-constraint subtype from the constraint-you-carry-forward subtype. V-05 is the first variation to sub-type the ACKNOWLEDGED state by whether the constraint is ever expected to be satisfied (C-05: never; C-10: carried as decision context, not as an output action).

Distinct from C-32 (three-state vocabulary): C-32 requires the ACKNOWLEDGED state to exist; the new criterion would require the ACKNOWLEDGED state to be semantically sub-typed based on the nature of the constraint.

### Pattern C — Semantic-domain criterion bundling (V-05)

V-05 bundles C-04+C-07 (both YAML structural requirements: named roles and exec office, both satisfied in DRAFT PHASE) and C-16+C-08 (both amend-section format requirements: debt framing and option listing, both satisfied in FINALIZE PHASE). The C-29 bundles used in all variations (C-11+C-21+C-22+C-25+C-26; C-23+C-27) are architectural dependency bundles — criteria that co-occur because they are designed to satisfy each other structurally. V-05's additional bundles are semantic-domain bundles: criteria grouped because they concern the same section or YAML construct, not because they are structurally interdependent.

Distinct from C-29 (criterion-pair bundling): C-29 requires compound bundles to be present; the new criterion would require the bundling principle to be semantic-domain co-location, not only architectural dependency — making the bundling strategy auditable as a design decision rather than a mechanical grouping.
