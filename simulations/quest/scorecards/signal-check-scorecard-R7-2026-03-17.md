## Research Question Findings

### RQ1: Does verbatim PART 2 verdict anchor (V-01/V-04/V-05) richen the inertia case assessment?

YES — structural difference confirmed. V-01/V-04/V-05 make the coaching inertia judgment
architecturally dependent on the mechanism verdict: the team-facing conclusion is the verdict
with additional artifact context, not an independent re-inference. V-02/V-03 instruct
"connect to the MECHANISM VERDICT" — leaving re-inference possible without detection.

The verbatim-quote requirement does not feel rote in V-04/V-05 because the quote is consumed
twice in different registers (SEQUENCE for mechanism audit; PART 2 for coaching judgment),
each serving a distinct function.

### RQ2: Does Pre-specification binary (V-02/V-04/V-05) sharpen coaching?

YES — the binary sharpens downstream analysis by name. STEP 3's root-finding becomes
structurally traceable: "Pre-specification gap: YES" as a named input explains why CAUSAL GAP
and STALENESS co-flag (the mechanism commitment predated evidence that was never gathered).
PART 2 STEP C calibrates coaching urgency directly: YES → "may require revisiting the
specification"; NO → "addressable without spec reconsideration."

No false-precision risk observed in the variation structure — the binary requires a reason
citation ("cite both the gap's first appearance and the spec artifact"), anchoring it in
artifacts rather than judgment.

### RQ3: Does explicit empty-pool branch (V-03/V-04/V-05) surface otherwise-missed cases?

YES — the mandatory branch converts the most common silent failure in CAUSAL GAP (thin
verdict from absent evidence) into a named, remediable finding. V-03 STEP 3 distinguishes
"structurally empty pool" from "partial mechanism case" as distinct root patterns — a
distinction that prose-only handling obscures.

Overhead is minimal: the two-branch structure adds one instruction block to CAUSAL GAP and
one named pattern to STEP 3. The payoff (empty-pool surfaces as an explicit team-facing
finding with artifact-type remediation path) is higher than the overhead.
