## Excellence Signals from V-05

**V-05 is the sole variation to achieve a perfect score** (210/210), driven by one structural decision: the addition of a **"What We Expected (B-[#])" sub-field** in every Stage 4 entry.

**Signal 1 — "What We Expected" as structural B-# citation gate.** The core insight is that C-01's pass condition ("every Stage 4 entry references a specific B-#") cannot be reliably enforced when the B-# connection is only tracked through the deviation chain (Stage 2 deviation → GATE-CONFIRMED-[N] → Stage 4 entry [N]). The chain is implicit: nothing in the Stage 4 entry body forces the model to surface the B-# or state the contradicted belief. V-05 breaks this by adding "What We Expected (B-[#])" as the first sub-field of every Stage 4 entry, requiring a "direct quotation or paraphrase of the referenced B-# belief." The Surprise 0 calibration entry demonstrates it: "The team believed that the `ConsentManager` component would enforce data retention policy uniformly across all storage backends, as stated in the draft specification reviewed prior to investigation." The belief-vs-finding gap becomes visible within the entry itself, not only inferred from the stage chain.

**Signal 2 — "What We Expected" specificity antipattern gate in per-entry EXIT checklist.** V-05's 5-item per-entry checklist includes: "What We Expected: full sentence referencing a specific B-# from Stage 1; states the prior belief that is contradicted — not a generic 'we thought it would work'." This is a named-antipattern exclusion for the inertia sub-field, parallel to C-24's Build Risk specificity gate. Without it, a model could satisfy the structural presence of the sub-field with a vague inertia statement that doesn't actually identify which belief is being contradicted.

**Signal 3 — Five-item Field Reference Block.** V-05's Field Reference Block covers all five sub-fields (including "What We Expected") with consistent structure: each field has a purpose statement and at minimum a formula or structural distinction. The five-field structure is fully modeled in Surprise 0, making the expanded entry format as coherent as the four-field baseline in other variations.

## New Patterns for Rubric v13

Two structural patterns emerge from this round that are not tested by v12:

**Pattern 1 — Inertia sub-field structural B-# citation gate (target: C-33):** V-01 through V-04 achieve PARTIAL on C-01 because the B-# reference per Stage 4 entry is implicit (carried through the deviation chain) rather than structurally required within the entry format. V-05 closes this gap with a dedicated "What We Expected (B-[#])" sub-field. C-33 would test whether Stage 4 entries structurally require the B-# reference through a named sub-field in the live entry template and the EXIT checklist — not merely inferring it from the deviation-chain linkage. The test is whether the B-# reference and belief quotation/paraphrase are visible and enforceable within the entry body itself.

**Pattern 2 — "What We Expected" sub-field specificity antipattern gate in per-entry EXIT checklist (target: C-34):** Parallel to C-32's named Signal Source prohibited phrases and C-24's Build Risk specificity gate, V-05 names the specific failure mode for the inertia sub-field ("not a generic 'we thought it would work'") and prescribes the corrective action ("full sentence referencing a specific B-# from Stage 1"). C-34 would test whether the per-entry EXIT checklist includes a specificity check for the "What We Expected" (or equivalent inertia) sub-field that names the generic placeholder failure mode by example and specifies the corrective action, converting the sub-field from a structural presence requirement into a runtime-enforced specificity constraint where a vague inertia statement blocks COMMIT-ENTRY emission.
