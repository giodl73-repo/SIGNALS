## Excellence Signals from V-05

**1. Phase-level role labeling satisfies C-07 and C-15 simultaneously**
Organising work into Phase 1 (Strategy), Phase 2 (UX), Phase 3 (PM) names roles explicitly while enforcing reversed sequence structurally. Single structural decision resolves two criteria.

**2. FAIL_MONOLITH_ASSUMPTION at Phase 2 UX step**
V-05 adds a new named failure mode at the UX analysis step — "Treating every entry as a single unified group without checking for sub-segments misses the most common source of communication strategy mismatches." This is the first round where this failure is named inline at the UX step rather than appearing as a coaching note. Makes C-17 coverage at Phase 2 substantive rather than merely formal.

**3. Three-position propagation in FAIL_NO_INERTIA**
Phase 1b's FAIL_NO_INERTIA note now reads "the omission propagates to three downstream positions" — updating the count from two (grid tag + veto rank) to three (grid tag + veto rank + prefill assignment). Accurately reflects C-20's addition of the prefill gate as a mandatory inertia tracking position.

**4. Amendment correct-form demonstrates both structural levels**
Step 8 correct form explicitly updates Step 6a prefill table AND Step 6b content row in sequence. The example shows a Frame Type reassignment at the prefill level ("Keep Satisfied Frame Type reassigned from `value-capture` to `compliance-alignment`") followed by the matching content-row revision. Establishes that when a prefill table exists, an amendment touching a Frame Type is never single-layer.

**5. Inertia displacement framing in rule 3 is normative, not just procedural**
V-05's Step 6a rule 3 says: "A value-capture or risk-mitigation frame for an inertia stakeholder's quadrant does not acknowledge displacement — assign the correct value here, in the prefill step." This is stronger than just mandating the assignment — it explains *why* the other Frame Types are semantically wrong for inertia stakeholders, making the rule self-documenting.
