## Excellence Signals (from V-02 and V-05)

**Signal 1 — Ordering context subordinated; emission is the main predicate**
Both top-scoring variations place the ordering information in a subordinate element (fronted adverbial in V-02, trailing adverbial clause in V-05) and keep `emits` as the main predicate with `this step` as nominative subject. This is the canonical pattern that clears C-25, C-28, C-29 simultaneously.

**Signal 2 — Explicit ordering contrast via event-naming (V-05)**
V-05 names the competing event ("before any other step *produces output*"), not just the existence of other steps ("before any other step *begins*" in V-02). The explicit event-naming is stylistically stronger — it specifies what the ordering prevents, not just when it applies.

**Signal 3 — Adverbial placement is symmetric**
V-02 places the adverbial before the subject; V-05 places it after the object. Both pass identically. C-28 does not have a position requirement for adverbials — only that the emission verb remain in the main-predicate slot.

## Open Questions Resolved

**Q1: Does C-28 require emission in the main predicate?**

V-01 (emission in relative clause): C-28 **FAILS** — main predicate required, not just main clause.  
V-04 (emission in infinitival complement): C-28 **boundary** — equative identity declaration may satisfy or may not, depending on whether the rubric treats `is the first to emit` as a compound emission predicate.

Conclusion: If V-04 also fails C-28, R12 motivates **C-36: main-predicate-emission requirement** — the step must be the nominative subject of an emission verb that is the main predicate (not a relative-clause verb, infinitival complement verb, or other subordinate emission).

**Q2: Is possessive-subject displacement (V-03) distinct from existing criteria?**

V-03 fails C-25, C-28, C-29, C-30, C-35 — five criteria. The structural failure mode ("This step's first action") places the step as possessor in the subject NP head, which is:
- **Not C-34** (gerundive-as-subject): the gerundive `emitting` is the predicate, not the subject.
- **Not C-35** (relative-clause-agent): no relative clause; step is possessor, not agent.
- **Not C-29** (nominalization-as-subject): the NP head is an action noun with possessor, not a nominalization derived from the step name.

This pattern is sufficiently distinct to warrant **C-36: possessive-subject displacement** — when the step appears as the possessor in the subject NP rather than as the nominative head, all five failure criteria fire. The possessive NP is the sixth structural displacement mode.
