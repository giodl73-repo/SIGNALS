## Excellence Signals from V-05

Three beyond-rubric patterns distinguish V-05 as the ceiling variation:

**Pattern 1 — Phase 6a table schema carries entity-type column**

V-05's Phase 6a audit table is headed "Evidence category + entity-type referent" (not just "Evidence category + instance"). This propagates C-28's entity-type constraint into the mid-workflow audit surface — the executor must name the entity-type referent during reconciliation, not only when Check 2c runs at Phase 7. A criterion that only lives in Phase 7 can be satisfied by a last-second addition; a criterion that also lives in the Phase 6a table schema forces it into the authoring workflow.

**Pattern 2 — Gate checks include branched repair protocols**

V-05's Check 6b does not just detect contradictions — it names two repair paths: "(a) correct the MANUSCRIPT OUTCOME for the R-ID if the change was actually made but recorded incorrectly; or (b) revise Paragraph 1 to remove or rephrase the false claim." This converts the gate from a detection point into a directed resolution protocol. Every gate check that detects a structural contradiction has a corresponding repair action; the executor knows not only that the artifact fails but how to fix it without backtracking blindly.

**Pattern 3 — Phase 6a forward-annotates Phase 7 gate conditions**

V-05's Phase 6a reconciliation step explicitly warns: "A change claim whose R-ID traces to a no-change MANUSCRIPT OUTCOME fails Check 6b." This forward-reference creates advance detection at Phase 6a (before Phase 7 runs) — the executor catches the contradiction class during reconciliation, not at the gate. Pairing a gate condition in Phase 7 with a warning at the point where the error is most likely to occur (cover letter reconciliation) gives the constraint two enforcement surfaces.
