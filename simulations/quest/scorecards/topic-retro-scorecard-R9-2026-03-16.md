## Excellence Signals — V-01

**1. Phase 0 as a sealed standalone prior-belief capture phase**
V-01 designates Phase 0 as a structurally separated, explicitly sealed phase with its own phase boundary declaration. The sealing is an auditable event — not just an instruction to "record prior belief" but a named phase that closes before Phase 1 opens any signal artifact. This is what makes C-22 (prior belief captured) enforceable by inspection rather than by trusting the author.

**2. Three-row foil table with three named comparison dimensions**
The foil in Phase 5 is not a binary contrast but three named rows — signal accuracy assumption, Echo assumption, signal design default — each targeting a distinct level of consequence. A reader cannot fill in the table by restating one finding; they must address three separable questions about what would have defaulted without the retro.

**3. Calibration section with mandatory graceful fallback**
Phase 4 prohibits conditional skipping: even when no prior retro exists, the calibration section must emit an explicit named baseline record with a forward-continuity statement. The pattern eliminates the "if applicable" opt-out that makes calibration sections unreliable in early runs.

These three patterns are the direct behavioral instantiations of C-23, C-24, C-25 — no new patterns beyond what v8 already codified.
