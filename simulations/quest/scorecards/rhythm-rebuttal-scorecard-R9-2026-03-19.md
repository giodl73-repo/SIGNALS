## Excellence Signals from V-05

**Signal 1 — Three-surface enforcement architecture per constraint class.** V-05 creates three distinct enforcement surfaces for entity-type resolution: (1) Phase 2 forward-annotation warns at pre-commitment time, (2) Phase 6a entity-type referent column enforces during mid-workflow reconciliation, (3) Phase 7 Check 2c detects at write-time gate. No single surface can be bypassed without another detecting the failure. Contrast with V-01 (surface 2+3 only) and the R8 base (surface 3 only).

**Signal 2 — Branched repair embeds the diagnostic inside the gate.** V-05's labeled (a)/(b) repair paths distinguish structurally different failure types at each gate — e.g., Check 2b (a) "valid form in AUTHOR RESPONDS but absent from MANUSCRIPT OUTCOME: move it" vs (b) "no valid form anywhere: introduce one." The executor doesn't improvise; they read the failure type and take the labeled path. The gate itself carries the repair taxonomy.

**Signal 3 — Forward-annotation surfaces constraint at the moment of avoidance, not the moment of detection.** V-05's Phase 2 blockquote tells the author that a location reference alone ("App. B") will fail Check 2c before they write a single exchange. The Phase 5 MANUSCRIPT OUTCOME template warns about Check 6b before the author moves to Phase 6a. A constraint that appears only in Phase 7 is discovered after the draft is done; a constraint that appears in Phase 2 is encountered when the author can still prevent the failure class entirely.

**Signal 4 — Cross-references in Phase 7 close the loop back to upstream warnings.** V-05's Phase 7 Check 4 Part C states "Phase 2 forward-annotation warned that this decision must be recorded in Phase 6a — an entry failing Part C missed that constraint at its earliest visible point." This creates accountability: if the author reaches Phase 7 with a failure, they know they ignored an upstream warning. The cross-reference converts Phase 7 detection into a learning signal about where the author's attention failed.
