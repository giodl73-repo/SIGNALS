## Excellence Signals

V-04 is the only variation that falls short — losing on C-11 because its inertia-first sequencing produces a two-level sort (challenger/advocate) rather than the explicit three-tier CHALLENGERS → CONDITIONALS → ADVOCATES structure with a labeled CONDITIONAL bucket. The absence of a named CONDITIONAL tier means conditional voices may be absorbed into either group, weakening the tally.

Among the four ceiling scorers, V-05 produces the most structurally reliable enforcement of the two new R4 criteria:

**Pattern 1 — C-16 gate as a dedicated numbered step, not a nested instruction.** V-05 gives the gate its own section header (`## Step 3 — C-16 Gate`), its own output format, and its own failure declaration ("This line is required; its absence means C-16 failed"). V-01 and V-03 embed the gate inside the investigation block as a paragraph instruction; a model parsing the instruction stream can elide it without a visible gap. V-05 creates a structural gap.

**Pattern 2 — `CITE:` as a named structural subfield in the voice block format, not a prose rule.** V-02 and V-05 both require a `CITE:` labeled field in every Tier 3 voice block. The absence of this field leaves a visible unfilled label — the model cannot write a syntactically complete Tier 3 block without it. V-01 and V-03 enforce C-17 through prose rules ("Tier 3 voices that endorse without citing (a)/(b)/(c)/(d) fail C-17"), which requires prose discipline rather than template completion.

The R4 insight: **both new criteria (C-16, C-17) are most reliably passed when they are output fields the model must fill in, not behavioral rules the model must remember.**
