## Excellence Signals

Patterns from V-04 (strongest) and cross-variation patterns that distinguish top performance:

1. **Prohibition with explanation of the failure mode** — V-01 and V-04 don't just say "do not mark in order" — they explain *why* ("the first 5 are your starting point, not your best"). Naming the failure mode makes the prohibition harder to ignore than a bare rule.

2. **Phase naming as structural ordering guarantee** — V-04's "PHASE 0 — INERTIA ANCHOR (runs before any ideas)" makes the ordering constraint structural, not instructional. The model cannot confuse sequence because the phases are named and numbered.

3. **Sub-condition labeling for AMEND** — V-04 and V-05 label AMEND sub-conditions (a)(b)(c), making them auditable. A model following labeled sub-conditions is less likely to satisfy (a) and (b) while ignoring (c) than one following a run-on sentence.

4. **Role separation as C-11 structural enforcement** — V-04's diverge/converge role split forces a minimum 25-entry pool before any selection can occur. C-11 compliance becomes a structural side-effect of the lifecycle, not just a rule to follow.

5. **Interrogative self-check as verification layer** — V-03 and V-05 introduce a named verification phase/step that asks the model to actively test its own output. This catches failure modes at a lifecycle gate rather than relying on compliance during generation.
