## Excellence Signals — V-01 Top Patterns

Patterns in V-01 that produced reliable criterion passes but are **not yet captured as named criteria (C-01 through C-35)**:

**Signal 1: Sequential step blocking as a procedural gate**

V-01 uses "Do not begin STEP N+1 until STEP N is written" as an explicit temporal enforcement mechanism. This is qualitatively different from requiring blocks to exist — it prevents the model from using prose composition to generate or refine the analytic blocks retroactively. C-10 and C-15 capture *what* must exist pre-composition; they do not capture the *gating mechanism* that enforces temporal order. A criterion that requires explicit sequential blocking language (not just presence of blocks) would strengthen enforcement of both C-10 and C-15.

**Signal 2: Verbatim restatement protocol for analytic blocks in narrative prose**

V-01 requires BLOCK B to be "restated verbatim" in Beat 5 — not paraphrased, not echoed, exactly as composed. This creates a precise traceability link between the pre-composition analytic layer and the narrative prose: any drift between the analytic output and the narrative is detectable by string comparison. C-17 requires the bridge to be explicit; it does not require verbatim identity. A criterion capturing verbatim restatement as an analytic traceability mechanism (applicable to BLOCK B and potentially BLOCK D) would close this gap.
