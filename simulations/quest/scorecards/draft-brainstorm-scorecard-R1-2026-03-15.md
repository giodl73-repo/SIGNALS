### Excellence Signals (from V-04)

**Anti-default warning in selection criteria**: "Do not default to marking the first 5 you wrote" — naming the failure mode explicitly in the criterion instruction is categorically more effective than positive framing alone ("pick the best 5"). The rubric's C-10 failure mode is positional bias; V-04 is the only variation that names it directly.

**Hard phase separation (diverge before converge)**: Instructing the model to "complete Phase A fully before starting Phase B" and "do not mix them" prevents evaluator-mode contamination during generation. This structural guarantee produces a larger, less filtered pool — making C-10 defensibility an honest selection rather than a post-hoc rationalization.

**Dimension enumeration as diversity scaffold**: Naming 6 specific dimensions in Phase A ("scope, timing, integration, audience, build-vs-buy, status quo") rather than saying "at least 4 categories" gives the model concrete tracks to fill, reducing the risk of category collapse where multiple items share the same underlying dimension with different surface names.
