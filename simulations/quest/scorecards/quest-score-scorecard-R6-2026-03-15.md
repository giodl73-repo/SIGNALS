## Excellence Signals

**Top variations:** V-04 and V-05 (tied 100/100)

### Signal 1 — Pre-evidence REGISTER NOTE injection (V-05 vs V-04)

V-04 injects the REGISTER NOTE before the scoring table. V-05 injects it before CHECK A/CHECK B — before the evaluator collects any evidence at all. The temporal anchor in V-05 prevents register bias from entering at evidence-collection time, not only at tabulation. Both satisfy C-20, but V-05's injection timing is architecturally stronger: it is impossible for a V-05 evaluator to record evidence using register-biased language and later be instructed to switch registers at the scoring table.

**Both V-04 and V-05 score 100, confirming injection timing produces no C-20 differential under v6.** This is itself a structural signal: C-20 tests structural presence of the register-agnostic instruction, not its placement within the ANALYST phase. However, V-05's pre-evidence placement represents a candidate for a future stricter criterion.

### Signal 2 — Dual-family enforcement for C-21 (V-05 exclusive)

V-04 enforces C-21 via a single family: the CRITERIA INDEPENDENCE NOTE in the ANALYST phase (write-time). V-05 adds CHECK D to the Verifier audit table, creating a second enforcement family at post-write audit time. Under v6, both satisfy C-21. But V-05's CHECK D means that even if the ANALYST note were skipped or malformed, the Verifier would catch missing independence tracking. V-04 has no such backstop.

**This dual-family pattern — write-time instruction plus post-write audit check — is the structural pattern that distinguishes V-05 from V-04 at the architectural level even when both score identically under v6.**
