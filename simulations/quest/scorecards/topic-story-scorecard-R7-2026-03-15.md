## Excellence Signals from Top-Scoring Variations (V-04, V-05)

**Signal 1 — Section header as structural gate (the V-01 failure mode, reversed)**

V-01 has the same mutation ledger as V-04. The difference: V-01 embeds the ledger inside Beat 2. V-04 gives the ledger its own named template section (`## Hypothesis Mutation Ledger`) placed before `## Story`. This single change flips C-18 from FAIL to PASS. When the ledger is inside a story beat, it IS the narrative — the pre-composition/narrative boundary is invisible to an auditor. When it is a named section before `## Story`, the boundary is visible in the output artifact without any instruction being read. C-18 is satisfied by template position, not by the instruction "do not begin writing until the ledger is complete."

**Signal 2 — Anti-stagnation check converts delta from style to contract**

V-01 and V-04/V-05 all include the anti-stagnation rule ("if S3 = S0, state why or fail"). This is what produces reliable C-09 passes. V-02 and V-03 lack S0/S3 infrastructure entirely — and correspondingly fail C-09. The anti-stagnation check is not a quality nudge; it is a structural contract: the ledger cannot be completed without producing a visible before/after contrast. The S0→S3 delta is guaranteed as a named output before narrative begins. Without the enforcement mechanism, a mutation ledger could be populated with all CONFIRMS stances, yielding no delta.
