### Excellence Signals (from V-04 / V-05)

**Signal 1 — Dual enforcement closes both structural gaps independently.**
V-01/V-02/V-03 each solve one of C-16/C-17 but not both; the single-axis variations plateau at 130 regardless of how well their chosen fix works. V-04 and V-05 achieve 135 by treating C-16 and C-17 as orthogonal requirements with orthogonal fixes. The takeaway: when two new criteria each have a distinct structural root cause (missing verification gate vs. missing output-visible slot), a single combined variation must address both axes separately.

**Signal 2 — Revision obligation converts verification into a hard gate.**
V-02's pre-commit block names criteria by ID and disqualifies generic self-assessment, which satisfies C-16's pass condition. V-05 goes further: if a GATE fails, the model has a stated obligation to revise the offending section before proceeding. This matters because a model can satisfy C-16 structurally (write a block, cite IDs) while still rationalizing a marginal result as passing. The revision obligation introduces a behavioral commitment that makes rationalization a prompt violation, not just a content weakness.
