### Excellence Signals from V-04 and V-05

**ES-R16-1: Semantic-label preamble declaration creates two-point binding**
V-05's REAL-REQUIRED Rationale section opens with: *'"REAL-REQUIRED" is the semantic identifier for this section. The following rationale content is pre-authored here in the skill template body.'* This declaration appears at the content location itself, before the gate references the identifier. V-04 assigns the identifier via the section header ("REAL-REQUIRED Rationale") but does not declare it explicitly. V-05 creates a two-point binding: the semantic identifier is declared at the content source AND referenced at the gate. A reader of either location can follow the coupling without reading the other. This is structurally parallel to what C-28 does for the seed column: the self-annotation at the gate makes the gate self-documenting; V-05's preamble declaration makes the content block self-documenting. Together they close the binding in both directions.

**ES-R16-2: Three-stage deepening makes coverage summary a constrained derivation, not an independent authoring step**
V-05's Stage 3 (action anchor) requires the GENERATOR to identify the specific skill invocation that closes the Stage 2 gap: *"the next signal is {/namespace:skill}."* ROLE 3 SUMMARIZER derives Recommended Next Step from Stage 3, not from a separate inference. The chain is: seed (Stage 1) → topic-specific gap (Stage 2) → skill invocation (Stage 3) → summary row. V-04 and all prior R15 variations derive the summary step independently by inference from the inertia statement. V-05 makes the derivation path explicit and enforced. A Recommended Next Step that does not trace to Stage 3 fails the ROLE 3 STOP. This is a new quality dimension above C-20 (body-grounding) and above the Stage 1/Stage 2 protocol that was the R15 V-05 ceiling.
