### Excellence Signals

**Signal 1 — Criteria are implementation-form-independent (V-01, V-02)**  
Both minimal-axis variations score 100.00. "Enforcement Protocol K/N" satisfies C-54's "or equivalent" gate; D-before-C declaration order satisfies all column-ownership and sequencing criteria. The 51 criteria correctly bind on structural position and component completeness, not surface terminology or declaration order.

**Signal 2 — Pre-read commitment pattern generalizes to stage mechanics (V-03)**  
The Recovery Path Stage Contract demonstrates that the §1d SLA Budget pre-commitment pattern (pre-specify values before row construction, anti-collapse instruction, reference from row descriptors) applies at stage-mechanics granularity. Stage mechanics (actor-chain type + mechanism type + SLA type + VS type) can be pre-committed as a column-level stage contract, creating a new structural pre-read level between the Column Contract and Row Descriptors.

**Signal 3 — Document-scope section headings create independently navigable structural skeletons (V-04, V-05)**  
§2 — Inertia Synthesis at document-header scope parallels §0 — Enforcement Mandate. The document now has a discoverable structural skeleton (§0 mandate → §1 pre-commitment → scenario table → Gap Register → §2 synthesis) traversable by section-heading scan. This is structurally distinct from the post-Gap content that C-37 requires — §2 as a named heading makes the synthesis section independently reachable without entering the Gap Register body.

**Signal 4 — §0→§2 forward reference closes the mandate-to-verdict arc (V-05)**  
§0's addition of *"This mandate governs the pre-commitment structure (§1 below) and is closed by the Inertia Synthesis (§2 below)"* creates a closed document arc. C-52 and C-56 already require §0 ↔ §1 bidirectional reference; V-05 extends §0 to name §2, completing a three-node verifiable chain. A reader can navigate from the mandate to the synthesis confirmation without entering any section body. No current criterion captures this — it is a new ceiling axis for R21.
