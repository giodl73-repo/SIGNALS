## V-02 / V-03 Innovation Gap

Both V-02 and V-03 introduce structural patterns that improve output quality but do not map to any rubric criterion in v17:

**V-02** (compound close markers): `**NULL REGISTER CLOSE** -- Null baseline for [concept] established` extends C-50 (which requires a close marker) into compound form. The compound close marker names what terminates AND characterizes what closure accomplished. No rubric criterion in C-01–C-54 captures this form beyond C-50's bare-marker requirement. The innovation is real but uncaptured.

**V-03** (dual-sentence manifest closes): DSQ-1 through DSQ-3 explicitly derive the requirement that manifest closes consist of two grammatically independent sentences. This strengthens C-51 compliance reliability (C-51 already requires "distinct co-present sentences" but V-01/V-02 derive this from DQ reasoning without sentence-count explicitness). The sentences are produced either way; V-03 makes the two-sentence requirement structurally derivable from explicit parse-time reasoning. Uncaptured as a separate criterion.

## Excellence Signals — Top Scorers (V-01, V-04, V-05)

**Shared winning patterns:**

**E-1: Compound headers in the causal phase itself prime Role 2 application.** When the CAUSAL PHASE uses `**LABEL** -- descriptor` form in the prompt structure, the model encounters the target pattern before it is required to produce it. The prompt structure models the correct form in the document space where the model reasons, reducing derivation distance between causal understanding and structural application.

**E-2: Compound form + epistemic-function descriptor are inseparable.** NHQ-1 and NHQ-2 do not ask "why use a compound header" (structural register) but rather "what does the compound form make available to the reader that the bare label lacks" (epistemic register). This framing drives the model toward a descriptor that characterizes cognitive function, not topic — satisfying both C-52 and C-54 simultaneously from a single causal derivation chain.

**V-05-specific patterns (not yet criteria):**

**E-3: Schema sub-area labeling (3a/3b/3c) makes multi-template Area 3 individually addressable.** When Area 3 declares multiple structural templates, labeling them as 3a/3b/3c gives Role 2 unambiguous references ("apply Area 3b template to every register header"). Without sub-labels, Role 2 instructions reference Area 3 generically and the model must infer which sub-template applies at each boundary. Sub-area labeling eliminates that inference gap.

**E-4: Unifying structural principle in schema ("no boundary is a bare positional marker — every boundary is a double assertion") generates correct forms for novel boundary types.** Rather than three independent template rules, the unifying principle gives the model a generative frame. A boundary type not explicitly anticipated by any template (e.g., a phase separator not covered by 3a/3b/3c) would still receive compound treatment if the model internalizes the principle rather than only the templates.

**E-5: NCQ questions in NULL REGISTER expand the register's epistemic scope.** V-05 places both header-format questions (NHQ) and close-marker questions (NCQ) in the NULL REGISTER, establishing that null-case epistemic reasoning applies to document boundaries at both their opening and their closing. This cross-boundary reasoning strengthens the structural coherence of the unifying principle.
