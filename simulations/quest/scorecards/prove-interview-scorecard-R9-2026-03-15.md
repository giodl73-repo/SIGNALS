## Excellence Signals (from V-05)

**Signal 1 — Prerequisite-mechanism contract**: The PRE-INTERVIEW MASTER GATE table (Section 0-D) maps each prerequisite to its dependent mechanisms explicitly — not just "is this satisfied?" but "what breaks if it's not?" Columns: `Prerequisite | What COMPLETE Means | Dependent Mechanisms | Status`. This transforms the gate from a confirmation checklist into a causal accountability surface.

**Signal 2 — Document-level format rules as self-enforcement**: SCHEMA CONTRACT RULE declared before content makes example-row presence self-enforcing throughout, removing per-section authorial responsibility. V-02 contributes GATE BLOCK RULE as the parallel for gate format. Both patterns shift enforcement from authorial memory to structural declaration.

**Signal 3 — Row-number cross-references in prerequisite checks**: Each per-phase prerequisite check cites the master gate by row number: "confirmed COMPLETE in PRE-INTERVIEW MASTER GATE (row 1)." This creates a navigable cross-reference chain — reader can jump from mechanism back to gate row and verify the specific completion evidence.

## New Patterns Emerging for R10

**C-33 candidate — Master gate declares dependent mechanisms per prerequisite**: The gate names not only that a prerequisite is satisfied but which mechanisms it enables. This makes the consequence of a PARTIAL prerequisite visible before the mechanism deploys — closing the gap between C-31 (gate exists and names prerequisites) and C-30 (mechanism confirms prerequisite before deploying).

**C-34 candidate — Document-level format rules declared before content sections**: Rules like SCHEMA CONTRACT RULE and GATE BLOCK RULE are declared at document head as self-enforcing contracts that apply throughout, rather than relying on per-section authorial implementation. This shifts compliance from author-remembers to structure-enforces.
