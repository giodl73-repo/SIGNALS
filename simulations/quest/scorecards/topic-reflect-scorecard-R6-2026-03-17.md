## Excellence Signals (V-05)

**Signal 1 — C-19 + C-20 mutual amplification.** Per-stage ENTER/EXIT contracts and Calibration-as-Entry-Zero interact in V-05. The Surprise 0 entry sets the quality floor for Stage 4 output; the Stage 4 EXIT condition then verifies that every live entry meets that floor before COMMIT-STAGE-4 is issued. Individually, each mechanism operates on a different failure mode: C-20 addresses quality drift via imitation anchoring; C-19 addresses premature stage advancement via verifiable post-conditions. Together they close both failure modes at once — the model imitates a high-quality entry and is then gated on having produced entries matching that quality.

**Signal 2 — C-21 + C-19 runtime repair loop.** The Stage 4 EXIT condition in V-05 combines two instructions: (a) check that all dimension names are canonical, and (b) correct any violations using the mapping table before emitting. When embedded inside a per-stage EXIT contract (C-19), this instruction is not advisory — it is a verifiable post-condition the model must satisfy before issuing COMMIT-STAGE-4. The mapping table (C-18) exists as a reference; the EXIT gate (C-21) converts it into an active audit protocol; the ENTER/EXIT structure (C-19) makes that protocol mandatory. Three mechanisms converge on a single compliance point.

**Signal 3 — Build Risk as C-01/C-04 double anchor.** V-03 and V-05 both include a Build Risk sub-field in Stage 4 entries. The field names the specific build or integration consequence if the surprise is ignored. This creates a second specificity anchor alongside Design Impact: Design Impact names the component affected; Build Risk names the outcome if the surprise is not acted on. Together they close two distinct vagueness failure modes. Whether Build Risk produces measurably stronger scoring output than Design Impact alone is the open question V-03 is positioned to answer.

**Signal 4 — Compound structure = surface area with no degradation.** V-05 adds four mechanisms (C-19, C-20, C-21, Build Risk) to the v5 baseline and achieves the maximum composite without any degradation on Essential or Recommended criteria. This confirms that the mechanisms do not conflict: ENTER/EXIT contracts, Surprise 0, EXIT self-repair, and Build Risk each operate on independent output failure modes. Adding all four simultaneously is not harmful; it is additive.
