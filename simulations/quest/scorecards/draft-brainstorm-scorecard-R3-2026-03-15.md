### Excellence Signals — Patterns from V-04 and V-05

**1. Named review-to-output connections as parenthetical labels**
Both 120-scoring variations label each review with its exact downstream step in parentheses — "feeds top-5 marking", "determines AMEND content in Step 5", "determines Category View completeness in Step 4". This converts the review phase from an advisory checklist into auditable pipeline architecture. The output section names then echo back: "Top-5 Selection (output of Review A and B)". Every link is bidirectional and traceable.

**2. Imperative peer test with hard consequence clause**
V-04/V-05 both use: "If you cannot complete this sentence for a candidate, that candidate does not belong in the top-5 -- replace it or sharpen its rationale until the comparison holds." The consequence clause ("does not belong", "replace it") makes the test non-bypassable. The single-axis variations that attempted C-17 (V-01 Check B) used "if you cannot, the selection is not defensible" — softer and suggestive. The 2.5-point delta is entirely attributable to the consequence formulation.

**3. Bias explanation embedded at the prohibition site**
The generation-phase guard in V-03, V-04, and V-05 all include the WHY: "sequential bias -- the first ideas written are the most available, not the best." Attaching the causal explanation to the prohibition in the generation phase makes the instruction semantically self-justifying. The model cannot treat it as a mechanical rule to skip; it has to engage with the reasoning.
