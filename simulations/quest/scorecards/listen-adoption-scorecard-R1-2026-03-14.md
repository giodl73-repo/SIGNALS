### Excellence Signals — V-05

**1. GATE checkpoint pattern eliminates orphan criteria.** Every rubric criterion maps to a named, mandatory output location. No criterion is left to the model's discretion to include or skip. This turns rubric design into prompt architecture.

**2. Removing conditional qualifiers forces complete coverage.** V-01's "if confident" on C-09 is a 3-point leak. GATE checkpoints make sensitivity analysis a required section, converting PARTIAL to PASS on the aspirational tier.

**3. Dual-axis combination captures format and depth without trade-off.** V-02 gets C-09 but loses C-08 (prose format). V-01 gets C-08 but partially loses C-09 (soft qualifier). V-05 inherits table structure from the format axis and Phase 3 + Phase 7 depth from the lifecycle axis — no single-axis trade-off.

**4. Evaluator reliability as a design goal.** The hypothesis frames GATE as serving the evaluator, not just the model. This shifts variation design from "what makes the model output richer" to "what makes the output mechanically auditable" — a distinct and reproducible quality signal.
