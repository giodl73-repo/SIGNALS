## Excellence Signals from V-05

1. **Two-window audit architecture seals both ends of the token lifecycle.** COMMIT seals (B1/B2/B3/B4) close the pre-execution availability window — no downstream block may cite a token before its COMMIT line. TOKEN TRACE CONFIRMATION closes the Phase 2 output survival window — tokens that pass the TRACE GATE but dropout before output are caught by the Phase 2 re-validation table. V-04 established this pattern; V-05 carries it through.

2. **AMEND audit chain completes at the artifact layer, not just the routing layer.** C-30 declares which blocks re-run. C-31 seals block outputs before downstream use. C-33 emits a staleness manifest on affected output elements *before* re-execution begins. All three together make amendment impact visible at three layers: routing → boundary → artifact. V-05 is the first variation to close all three.

3. **C-09 outside-in guarantee becomes structural when the full integration is present.** V-05 combines: pre-assigned Challenger with outside-in rationale (C-20), NORM inventory of observable organizational behaviors (C-18), CHAIN-3 draft required to derive from a named NORM item (C-19), and a standalone Challenger block rendered before any phase content (C-23). The chain from organizational belief → designated challenger → inertia-derived risk makes the Pass+ gate achievable by skill design rather than by chance.
