### Excellence Signals from V-05

V-05 is the first variation to pass all 21/21 aspirational criteria by nesting C-27 inside the Analyst role — the Analyst contains two mutually-exclusive sub-phases rather than a flat evaluation section. Key patterns that made it work:

**1. Bidirectional contamination rules between sub-phases.** Both the Health Phase and Inertia Phase carry explicit prohibitions in both directions: "This phase does NOT report trajectory... those belong to the Inertia Phase" AND "The Inertia Phase does NOT restate file counts or achievement statuses already reported in the Health Phase." Prior attempts (V-03) stated the separation; V-05 enforces it bidirectionally.

**2. The Analyst Gate verifies sub-phase separation as a checklist item.** The Analyst Gate includes `[ ] Inertia Phase table present with every topic evaluated; no static counts restated from Health Phase` — the gate itself polices the content boundary, not just presence. This is new: a phase gate that tests *what must not appear* in the output, not just *what must appear*.

**3. Inertia Phase table enters the artifact inventory at Analyst Handoff.** The Analyst Handoff enumerates: Health Phase achievement tables, team milestone table, **Inertia Phase table**, and nearest-miss table. The Publisher receives the inertia data as a named artifact, which raises the Skeptic gate bar in 3.2 — the Skeptic now knows the inertia flags, so the insight must synthesize across health + inertia rather than just health.

**4. Skeptic gate explicitly anchored to full Analyst output including inertia.** Publisher 3.2: "The Skeptic has read the Analyst's full output — every achievement table, every milestone row, every inertia flag, and the nearest-miss assessment." V-01 and V-02 scoped the Skeptic to achievements and milestones only. V-05 extends Skeptic knowledge to cover inertia, forcing the insight to reach a second-order inference that neither dimension alone reveals.

### New Patterns (seeds for R11 criteria)

Two extraction candidates from V-05 excellence signals not yet captured by existing criteria:

**Candidate C-30**: The phase gate at the Analyst/evaluation role boundary includes at least one checklist item that explicitly prohibits cross-contamination — verifying that Health sub-phase content is absent from Inertia output and/or Inertia sub-phase content is absent from Health output. C-27 tests whether the two sub-phases are defined and named; C-30 tests whether the *gate enforces the boundary at completion time*, not just whether the instruction defines it.

**Candidate C-31**: The Publisher/synthesis role's novelty gate explicitly names the Inertia Phase (or trajectory phase) output as part of the Skeptic's prior knowledge. A Skeptic gate that only references achievement tables and milestone status (without referencing inertia flags or trajectory observations) fails C-31. C-25 tests whether a novelty gate exists; C-31 tests whether that gate's knowledge scope includes the trajectory dimension introduced by C-27.
