## Research Question Findings

### RQ1: Does C-33's named binary from STEP A close a structural gap that prose inertia-case conclusions leave open at STEP E?

**YES -- named binary closes the production-side gap; C-34 is required to close the consumption-side gap.**

V-01 makes the distinction precise. STEP A emits `Inertia case: [BUILT / PARTIAL / OPEN]` -- the named binary exists. STEP E references the label (`Inertia case: [restate from STEP A prose]`). But the consumption is prose-level: STEP E draws on "the inertia case assessment in STEP A above," which means it can re-derive the implications from STEP A's full advisory prose rather than treating the label as a structural constraint. C-33's structural gain is real (the named binary makes the label referenceable), but V-01 shows that existence of the label at production does not prevent re-derivation at consumption. The enforcement mechanism is C-34's per-input prohibition, not C-33's label existence. The two criteria are complementary and sequential: C-33 creates the consumable artifact; C-34 enforces its structural consumption.

### RQ2: Does per-input prohibition at STEP E (C-34) close a partial-compliance gap that grouped joint consumption leaves open?

**YES -- grouped form enables selective compliance that is locally undetectable; per-input makes each specific re-derivation visible.**

V-02 makes the partial-compliance trap explicit. The grouped annotation `Required inputs -- do not re-derive: Confirmed readiness and Inertia case` appears present. But a model executing against V-02 can treat `Confirmed readiness` as a structural constraint (consuming the STEP D label by label) while treating `Inertia case` as contextual background (re-deriving the inertia assessment from STEP A's advisory prose). The grouped annotation remains visibly present at STEP E either way -- the violation is undetectable locally. Per-input form in V-04/V-05 closes this: each prohibition is independently present and independently enforceable; a model that re-derives `Inertia case` at STEP E while the per-input prohibition is present creates a locally visible contradiction.

Additional finding: C-34 FAIL at STEP E with grouped form implies C-28 FAIL when STEP E references multiple named inputs independently. V-02 introduces a C-28 regression that was not present in the single-axis design -- the two criteria are entangled when grouped consumption covers two named inputs at a single consuming step. V-02 is the only variation with a C-28 regression over the R10 baseline, making it the lowest-scoring variation at 98.89.

### RQ3: Does extending the ARCHITECTURE block to PART 2 internal rows (C-35) make the compliance gap visible before execution -- and does it require C-33 + C-34 to actually close the gap?

**YES to both -- C-35 achieves pre-execution detectability; C-33 + C-34 are required for the declared contract to be fulfillable and enforced.**

V-03 isolates the declaration-without-production case. The 11-row ARCHITECTURE table includes `Inertia case: BUILT/PARTIAL/OPEN | PART 2 STEP A | PART 2 STEP E (by label)`. A reader can immediately see from the table that STEP A should produce a named binary for STEP E to consume -- without reading STEP A. But STEP A in V-03 emits only prose. The ARCHITECTURE row promises a named output that never exists. The gap is detectable (C-35's auditability goal is achieved) but not closed. V-03 confirms the dependency chain: C-35 makes the gap visible -> C-33 creates the binary the row promises -> C-34 enforces its per-input consumption at STEP E. All three are required for the declared contract to be both visible and fulfilled.

## Excellence Signals

### EX-01: Two-Input Symmetric Per-Input Prohibition at STEP E as Joint-Grounding Enforcement

V-04 and V-05 open STEP E with two independent per-input prohibitions: `Required input -- do not re-derive: Confirmed readiness from STEP D` and `Required input -- do not re-derive: Inertia case from STEP A`. The symmetric structure makes joint grounding locally enforceable at the single consumption site where both analytical confirmation and strategic assessment converge. V-02 shows the failure mode when symmetry is broken (grouped form allows selective compliance); V-01 shows the failure mode when the second prohibition is absent (prose re-derivation is undetectable). Per-input symmetry is the minimum structure required to make joint grounding architecturally enforced rather than instructionally encouraged.

### EX-02: 11-Row ARCHITECTURE Table as Full Cross-Register Pipeline Contract

V-03 shows that declaration of the Inertia case row creates structural pressure (the gap is visible) even when C-33 is absent; V-04/V-05 show that the table is only accurate when the declared named outputs are actually produced by their declared production steps.

### EX-03: Three R12 Axes Close the Final Open Handoff in the Full Pipeline

Single-axis isolations (V-01, V-02, V-03) confirm each axis closes a distinct and non-redundant gap:
- **C-33** (STEP A named binary): creates the consumable artifact -- transforms inertia-case judgment from prose backdrop into a labeled output that downstream steps can reference structurally
- **C-34** (STEP E per-input): enforces structural consumption -- prevents selective compliance at the joint-grounding site where both analytical and strategic inputs converge
- **C-35** (ARCHITECTURE 11-row): extends pre-execution auditability -- makes the PART 2 internal chain visible in the front-loaded contract before analysis runs

The three axes are structurally sequential: C-33 produces the label that C-34 enforces and C-35 declares. C-35 without C-33 creates a declared-but-unfulfilled contract (V-03 gap). C-33 without C-34 creates a named binary that is re-derivable at consumption (V-01 gap). C-33+C-35 without C-34 or C-33+C-34 without C-35 each score 99.26 -- identically -- because each is a 2-of-3 failure pattern. All three together close the final open handoff.
