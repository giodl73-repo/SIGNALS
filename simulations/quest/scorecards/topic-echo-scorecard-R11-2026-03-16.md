### Excellence Signals — V-05

V-05 is the only variation to reach ceiling. Three structural patterns drove it there:

**1. Lifecycle sub-phasing as structural obligation (Axis A)**
Named sub-phases [SCAN]/[FREEZE]/[COVERAGE AUDIT] make BC-COVERAGE-RECORD a phase milestone gate rather than an optional block. BC exclusion is gated on [COVERAGE AUDIT] completion — omitting the coverage record means leaving a named lifecycle phase open, not skipping a section. The coverage obligation is architecturally unavoidable.

**2. Format-register separation for transition events (Axis B)**
Markdown tables for PHASE-HANDOVER tokens occupy a visually distinct register from code-block protocol declarations. The 5-row table format signals "this is a transition event record" rather than "this is an operational instruction." The distinction is legible at a glance, reducing the risk that handover tokens are treated as optional prose or merged with operational content.

**3. Unconditional imperative grammar removes conditional optionality (Axis C)**
Rewriting STILL-LIVE FILTER as four sequential imperative commands eliminates the conditional branching structure that implies an optional NO-path. When every step is an unconditional command ("Write DISCARD-[N]. Name the label. State the route. State one sentence reason."), the grammar enforces completeness. No candidate path produces zero output. DISCARD-LOG-COMPLETE is a command, not an "if-complete" token.

**4. Compositional independence of structural zones**
The three axes operate in fully non-overlapping zones: Axis A in Step 1 interior (BC sub-phases), Axis B at step boundaries (EF-exit and BC-exit transition tables), Axis C in Step 3 filter grammar. No axis interferes with another. All three can be combined additively.
