## Excellence Signals — R3 Observations

The v3 rubric raised the bar with C-14/C-15/C-16. What the top variations reveal:

**1. Dual-gate architecture (V-03, V-04)** — Separating LOCK (freezes claim) from SEAL (freezes Final list) creates two distinct freeze events with different scopes. Each gate carries a checklist. This is structurally cleaner than a single gate trying to enforce both claim integrity and list completeness. V-04's ARCHIVIST section sits between LOCK and SEAL, making the sequence claim-freeze → consolidation → list-freeze explicit by document structure alone.

**2. Named structural unit for consolidation (V-03, V-04)** — Elevating consolidation to a named phase (V-03: PHASE 4 CONSOLIDATE) or named role (V-04: ARCHIVIST) makes C-16 non-skippable in a way embedded subsections cannot match. A model omitting a named role or phase fails visibly. A model omitting a sub-subsection within the Skeptic section fails silently. This is the single strongest structural bet of R3.

**3. Inertia-lead framing as C-13 primer (V-05)** — Opening with "What does the team do today?" before "What do you believe?" means F-00's gap analysis is written with the status-quo already primed in working context. The "why insufficient" explanation in Step 6 Confidence then cross-references Step 3 naturally. This produces organically grounded F-00 rationale without a separate interrogation pass.

**4. Confidence instruction ties to specific gate name (all R3)** — Every R3 variation adds an explicit behavioral note at the Confidence section: "Written after LOCK gate" (V-01), "Lock is in effect" (V-02), phase ordering enforces it (V-03), "Written after LOCK gate and SEAL gate" (V-04), "Write your confidence after the lock above is in effect" (V-05). This is stronger than R2's implicit ordering.

**Open R4 question confirmed still live:** Under context pressure (long prompts, multi-session use), does V-03's explicit CONSOLIDATE phase or V-04's Archivist role produce fewer C-16 degenerate failures than V-01/V-02/V-05's embedded consolidation subsections? R3 scoring can't distinguish — all five passed every criterion cleanly. A degenerate-run simulation would be needed to test degradation resistance.
