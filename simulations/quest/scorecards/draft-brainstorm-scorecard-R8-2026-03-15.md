## Excellence Signals (Top-Scoring Variations: V-03, V-04, V-05)

**Signal 1 — Dual-precondition phase lock (C-27 mechanism)**
The distinguishing structural move is a shift from permission framing ("Phase 3 may begin when X") to dual-condition precondition framing ("Phase 3 may begin only when X AND Y"). The blocked phase must be named, and the schema state must be explicit about *both* the row-count condition and the blank-column precondition simultaneously. V-04 demonstrates this can span two tables (Registry rows + idea-table Top-5? column), showing the mechanism is architecture-agnostic.

**Signal 2 — Impulse inversion as enforcement trigger (C-28 mechanism)**
The rationalization block reaches its ceiling when the revision impulse is inverted into positive confirmation evidence rather than merely prohibited. "Treat that impulse as confirmation the replacement obligation applies" is structurally different from "do not revise" — it converts the escape-route desire into the enforcement signal itself, closing not just the door but the reasoning path that leads to it.

**Signal 3 — V-05 trajectory-cost anchor (non-criterion quality signal)**
The [TRAJECTORY COST] tag in Layer 0 makes every idea explicitly compete against a named, measurable status quo cost rather than an abstract "do nothing" entry. This is not a new rubric criterion but improves C-07 (rationale specificity) and C-10 (top-5 defensibility) by anchoring every rationale to the same calibration baseline — a structural move that prevents generic rationales without adding new prohibitions.
