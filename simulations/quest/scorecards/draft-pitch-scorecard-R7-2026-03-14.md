## Excellence Signals from V-05

**What V-05 achieves that no prior variation achieves:**

V-05 is the first variation to produce a **formally closed binding declaration** — one that is self-documenting without execution. The tri-part structure (meta-purpose → table → exhaustiveness → prohibition) means a reader can determine the binding intent, the complete binding set, and the consumption constraint from the declaration text alone.

**Pattern 1: Declaration envelope completeness requires all three closure mechanisms**
A binding declaration is structurally complete only when it carries three distinct components: the *intent statement* (why the binding exists — C-27), the *exhaustiveness closure* (the binding set is complete, nothing omitted — C-25), and the *bypass prohibition* (the consuming step cannot shortcut to the source — C-26). Each component addresses a different failure mode: intent prevents the declaration from being skipped as boilerplate; exhaustiveness prevents the reader from assuming unlisted fields might also be bound; prohibition prevents Phase 5 from silently consuming DEFAULTS directly. All three must be present for the declaration to function as an audit boundary rather than a documentation note.

**Pattern 2: Front-loaded auditability intent changes the reader's parse order**
V-03 and V-05 promote the meta-purpose sentence to lead position. In trailing-clause form (R6 V-05), a reader encounters the binding table first and only retrospectively learns its purpose. In leading form, the reader knows *why* the table exists before reading it, which primes correct interpretation of each row. This is a structural reading-order effect, not merely stylistic. When the declaration is used in failure diagnosis (tracing why a Phase 5 output is wrong), front-loaded auditability intent makes the declaration the correct first stop rather than an afterthought.
