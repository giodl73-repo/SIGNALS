### Excellence Signals — Patterns from Top-Scoring Variations

**V-05's distinguishing patterns vs. V-01 (minimal diff):**

1. **Named-criterion traceability in ENFORCE prohibitions** (V-04, V-05): The commit fill rules name C-38 explicitly — "An output that re-enumerates finding labels inline inside the PHASE-1-COMMIT block instead of naming '## INERTIA RECORD above' fails C-38." Linking the prohibition to the criterion ID by name means a reviewer or downstream AI can trace any violation to the responsible criterion without parsing rubric prose. Prior versions stated the rule; V-04/V-05 state the rule and cite its name.

2. **C-39 extended to participant-loading declaration** (V-05 only): C-39 requires bilateral VALIDATE at the committee-type PRINT slot (minimum). V-05 extends coverage to the participant-loading PRINT slot — ACCEPTABLE: named charter with named roles; FAILS: "Participants: team members." This achieves uniform bilateral VALIDATE across all three Phase 0 essential-criterion output slots (committee type, agenda item, participants), not just the one explicitly required by C-39. The extension follows the same coverage principle C-39 itself articulates: wherever an essential criterion's output slot is declared, a bilateral VALIDATE rule should govern it.

3. **Inertia-Advocate synthesis with status tracking** (V-04, V-05): Rather than relying on charter contents to include an adversarial voice, V-04/V-05 make the Inertia-Advocate a structural invariant — synthesized if absent, always Tier 1, status (charter-loaded vs. Signal-default-synthesized) declared in PHASE-0-COMMIT and confirmed in PHASE-2-COMMIT. This converts C-11 and C-12 compliance from a charter-dependent outcome into a structural guarantee: the switching-cost investigation and challenger-first ordering cannot silently degrade due to a missing charter role.
