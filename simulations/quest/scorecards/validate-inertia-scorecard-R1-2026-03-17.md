### Excellence Signals (from top-scoring variations)

1. **Phase gate with explicit "do not proceed" instruction** (V-02, V-05): Placing kill-barrier identification between scoring and verdict makes it structurally impossible to skip C-04, not just inconvenient to omit. The most reliable enforcement mechanism for the criterion the rubric identified as the primary sharpening move.

2. **Status-quo competitor framing** (V-03, V-05): Naming the existing workaround as "the current solution that this feature must displace" triggers product-comparison reasoning, which naturally produces quantified switching costs (C-02) and specific satisfaction assessments (C-06) without requiring the model to generate them from abstract prompts.

3. **Score rationale per persona** (V-02, V-03, V-05): Requiring one sentence explaining why the primary driver dominates for each persona provides partial C-09 methodology transparency without a dedicated section — a lightweight way to earn partial aspirational credit.

4. **Double-reinforced C-06** (V-05): Requiring "what the current solution does well" in Phase 1 AND having a satisfaction column in Phase 2 creates two checkpoints for the same dimension, reducing the chance a surface answer in one phase substitutes for real analysis in the other.

5. **Named social proof threshold** (V-05): Replacing "Y/N/?" with "needs 2+ teammates before committing" or "will adopt solo if X" forces specificity that makes the social proof dimension actionable rather than categorical.
