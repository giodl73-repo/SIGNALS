## Discriminating Question Resolution

**Q1 — Does per-option batching (V-01) affect C-02?**
No. Physical block grouping is irrelevant. INERT-A has its own TOKEN RECALL + FAULT inside the Option A block; INERT-B has its own inside the Option B block. Explicit FAULT at each inertia site is the structural mechanism for independence. Section proximity never was.

**Q2 — Does numeric rating vocabulary (V-02) affect C-10?**
No. C-10 requires that named tokens (FEAS-A, INERT-A, etc.) are recalled at matrix assembly via the LEDGER GATE chain. The fill content of a token (GREEN vs. 4/5) is not the mechanism. Numeric vocabulary is fully neutral to all criteria.

**Q3 — Does AMEND inline compression (V-05) satisfy C-24?**
No — C-24 PARTIAL. Inline path declarations carry the correct operational content (TOKEN RECALL + FAULT + slot + blocking halt) but the sub-ledger form is not structurally identifiable as a discrete checklist block. The fenced-code ```Add-C ledger: FEAS-C [ ]...``` format is the required structural form. C-19 is not affected: slot declarations remain explicit, TOKEN RECALL and FAULT are co-located inline, so C-19 PASS.

**Bonus — Does a two-clause BODY ORDER (V-01/V-03/V-04) satisfy C-29?**
Yes. Whether the directive governs option-sequence, dimension-sequence, or output-order, what matters is that the clause is unconditional (no register branching, no if-then framing). All R15 BODY ORDER clauses are unconditional. Multi-clause BODY ORDER is neutral to C-29.

## Excellence Signals

Top-scoring variations: V-01, V-02, V-03, V-04 (tied at 100.00).

Three structural patterns explain why V-01 through V-04 hold ceiling while V-05 drops to 99.81:

**1. Explicit FAULT site is the C-02 mechanism — not section geometry**
V-01 moves all inertia tokens inside per-option blocks rather than a shared inertia section. C-02 holds because each INERT token has its own co-located TOKEN RECALL + FAULT. The discipline is portable: inertia independence is a property of the token site, not the section boundary. Reshuffling sections cannot weaken it.

**2. Rating vocabulary is token-name–neutral**
V-02 replaces GREEN/YELLOW/RED with 1–5 and adds severity suffixes to RISK tokens. No criterion scores the content of a token's rating fill — only that the token exists, that it is recalled at the right site, and that the FAULT is correctly structured. Numeric vs. qualitative vocabulary is freely interchangeable without rubric consequence.

**3. Gate timing is placement-flexible for C-05**
V-04 fires the build/no-build gate inside a dedicated Inertia Gate section before any FEAS/RISK/COMP analysis. C-05 requires the question be surfaced — it does not constrain when relative to other analysis. Early-gate, late-gate, and inline-gate are all structurally equivalent for C-05.

**What V-05 reveals about C-24:**
The fenced-code sub-ledger block (```Add-C ledger: FEAS-C [ ] INERT-C [ ] RISK-C [ ] COMP-C [ ]```) is the required structural form for C-24 PASS. Inline compression that preserves TOKEN RECALL + FAULT but removes the checklist block is sufficient for C-19 but not for C-24. These are two distinct requirements. C-19 tests whether each AMEND path is self-contained and executable; C-24 tests whether the ledger is visually and structurally identifiable as a discrete sub-ledger form. Satisfying one does not imply the other.
