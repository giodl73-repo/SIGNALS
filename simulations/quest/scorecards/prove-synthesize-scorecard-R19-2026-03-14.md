The axis-invariance result means C-57 is not a fragile construct that depends on a specific structural context to survive — it is portable. Any future variation can adopt the form without needing to protect it from interference from axis-specific language.

#### Signal 2 — The upgrade is surgically isolated: one sentence replaces one sentence

The C-57 upgrade to V-01–V-04 is a single sentence substitution at a fixed structural position (the independence statement in Phase 2, after the reproducibility claim and before the ceiling table). Nothing upstream (Phase 1 annotation definitions, role sequence framing) or downstream (Ceiling Declaration mandate, gate block, output instructions, self-containment check) requires adjustment. The structural isolation is a property of C-57 itself: the explicit closure label concerns what the independence statement claims, not how other structural elements are arranged.

This isolation property has practical implications: future rubric versions that extend C-57 (toward a named full foreclosure taxonomy, for example) would have a single structural attachment point that is clearly delimited from all other elements.

#### Signal 3 — V-05 cross-round carryover confirms descriptive-register stability

This is the first cross-round confirmation of C-57 stability: the same form scores identically under two different versions of the rubric (v18 and v19), across two rounds.
