## Excellence Signals

**From V-04:**

**Pre-scan insight hypothesis** — Phase 1 (before scanning) requires the model to write `INSIGHT HYPOTHESIS: I expect the team insight will be about [topic/pattern].` Phase 8 revisits this hypothesis against scan findings and states whether it was supported, refuted, or refined. This forces the model to maintain a synthesis goal through execution rather than discover it at the end when selection bias from what was found may dominate. The hypothesis also creates a self-contained falsifiability record.

**Labeled step protocol** — Naming the retry steps (Step 1 — TRIAD, Step 2 — DELIMIT, Step 3 — CONTINUE) makes the three-part procedure machine-readable and individually referenceable. A gate that fails only Step 2 (missing delimiter) can be distinguished from one that fails Step 1 (missing triad). This decomposability was missing from the "do these three things" prose form.

**From V-05:**

**Tabular structural audit** — Rendering the structural audit as a compliance table (`Gate Label | Expected IDs | Status`) makes the N=9 completeness check a row count rather than a prose reading. This distinguishes between "the audit mentions 9 gates somewhere" and "the audit has exactly 9 rows, one per gate." C-34 as a criterion was designed to require the latter — the tabular format is the cleanest structural embodiment.

**Pre-declared output schemas** — Declaring `SCHEMA-RISK`, `SCHEMA-ACHIEVE`, `SCHEMA-LEADERBOARD`, `SCHEMA-1AWAY`, `SCHEMA-ACTIONS`, `SCHEMA-MILESTONES` in the preamble shifts gate responsibility from "does this section have the right columns?" to "does this section fill the declared schema?" The schema is stable across runs; per-section redesign drift is prevented structurally, not by gate instruction.

## R13 Seeding

The excellence signals above point at two candidate criteria for v12:

- **C-35 (candidate)**: When the skill includes a cross-topic insight phase, the skill requires the model to commit to a pre-scan insight hypothesis before reading any artifacts — and requires the insight phase to explicitly state whether the hypothesis was supported, refuted, or refined by scan evidence; an insight phase that produces a synthesis conclusion without testing a pre-scan hypothesis does not satisfy this criterion.

- **C-36 (candidate)**: When the skill includes multiple output sections with defined schemas (table columns), all schemas are declared in the preamble as named schema blocks — gates verify that the filled section conforms to the declared schema, not that the section independently declares a correct schema; skills where schema design happens per-section without a preamble declaration do not satisfy this criterion.
