### Excellence Signals from V-05

1. **Machine-readable hypothesis contract**: The IF/PREDICT/FALSIFIED-BY triplet (V-01 component) transforms the gate hypothesis from a prose prediction into a typed contract. The FALSIFICATION-SIGNAL field names exactly one DARK SIGNALS label that, if absent, overturns the claim — making the hypothesis falsifiable by a single named observable.

2. **IS-FALSIFICATION-SIGNAL binary typed field**: The YES/NO binary (`IS-FALSIFICATION-SIGNAL: YES — CONFIRMED|OVERTURNED | NO — corroborates-alternative|neutral`) is cleaner than the qualitative `[CONFIRMED | OVERTURNED | corroborates-alternative | neutral]` used by V-02/V-03/V-04. It forces the model to answer a binary question (IS this the named falsification signal?) before a secondary one (what does that mean?), reducing ambiguity in annotation.

3. **Weight table as structural C-37 enforcement**: The Penalty column (V-02 component) makes DARK SIGNALS label citations mandatory by table structure — you cannot produce the table without naming the label as a Penalty column value. This shifts C-37 from instruction compliance to structural enforcement.

4. **Hypothesis verdict as first field on ALL amend options**: The V-03 axis elevates C-40 beyond AMEND-A. AMEND-B (rename exec office) and AMEND-C (adjust group structure) also lead with hypothesis status. This frames all downstream decisions — not just pivot mode changes — as consequences of the hypothesis result.

5. **Chain Audit as standalone lifecycle artifact**: The Chain Audit block (V-03/V-05) traces C-38→C-39→C-40 in a single place below the amend section. This is a new structural artifact type: a post-amend lifecycle trace that allows a scorer or downstream consumer to audit the full hypothesis chain without reading three separate sections. The block is itself self-labeled and traceable.
