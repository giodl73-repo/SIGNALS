### Excellence Signals

**V-04 (top scorer — 88.6) — Unique passes: C-19, C-18 partial**

1. **Sequence function declaration**: V-04 uniquely passes C-19 by declaring the *function* of the first subject in the investigation, not just their role. "This simulation uses an inertia-first structure: the first subject is always someone deeply invested in the current solution... Their friction is the evidential baseline all subsequent interviews are measured against." One sentence, placed at the top of the prompt, names what the incumbent's position DOES for the evidence structure. This is the minimum viable form for C-19 — a role label alone doesn't pass; naming the purpose does.

2. **Epistemic standing as partial C-18 proxy**: V-04's `[Epistemic Standing]: GROUNDED / CONDITIONAL / INFERRED` annotation captures subject-experience grounding and partially satisfies C-18. The distinction between a CONDITIONAL objection ("they haven't seen the alternative") and a GROUNDED one ("direct operational experience") is the exact evidentiary weight adjustment C-18 targets — even though the verbatim/inferred/corroborated schema is a different dimension.

3. **Inertia as evidence baseline**: Making the status-quo user the required first subject grounds all subsequent evidence against realistic switching cost. This is not just a sequence choice — it changes what STRONG evidence means (must be strong enough to overcome documented inertia, not just abstractly positive).

**V-03 (close second — 88.3) — Unique pass: C-20**

4. **Binary gate language as structural enforcement**: V-03 is the only variation to pass C-20. The pattern: every phase ends with "Do not begin the next phase until..." followed by explicit `[ ]` checkboxes. The gate language is binary — not guidance, not a summary, but a stated condition that must be true. This eliminates the most common simulation failure modes (missing blind spots, hypothesis questions before reality is established) by requiring explicit verification before transition.

**Universal gaps (all 5 variations fail):**

- **C-10** (simulation fidelity): No variation includes a meta-note on grounded vs. constructed claims — this is a structural omission across R5
- **C-11** (voice divergence): All variations guide toward voice distinctiveness but none require a meta-observation naming a concrete contrast — the guidance stays inside the generation loop rather than surfacing in the artifact
- **C-13** (expectation register): No variation pre-populates subject predictions before transcripts — the expectation register required for C-05 and C-21 to work structurally is absent
- **C-22** (vocabulary signature): All R5 subject cards omit the 2–3 role-specific vocabulary terms field — the C-22 criterion is universally unreachable with current subject card designs
