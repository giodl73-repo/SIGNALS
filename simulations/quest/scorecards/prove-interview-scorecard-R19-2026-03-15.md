## Excellence Signals (V-05)

**What made V-05 the highest-scoring variation:**

1. **Self-auditing gate items via inline rationale (C-48):** Every gate item in V-05 carries a failure-mode clause stating *why* the condition exists. Example from Phase 0A: "— fewer rows may miss a dominant stickiness factor and bias Phase 3 PROFILE RELEVANCE tagging toward the single named practice, collapsing the multi-factor inertia model into a single-variable account." A scorer reading a single item can determine the condition (what), verification method (how), and failure mode (why) — no body prose or adjacent phases needed. Gate items become self-contained audit contracts.

2. **Criterion-ID inline citation as a new gate item annotation pattern:** V-05's Phase 2 gate includes "(C-47 for Phase 2 gate-block-first requirement)" — applying an existing criterion ID (C-47: domain naming) to a new context (gate-item ordering, not PROHIBITED clauses). This pattern makes cross-criterion dependencies auditable within the gate block itself without external lookup.

3. **Exact-match + sibling-domain + rationale together produce independent auditability at every gate boundary:** V-04 achieves structural compliance auditability (can a scorer verify the condition without cross-phase inspection?). V-05 adds intent auditability (can a scorer understand why the condition was designed this way without reading surrounding prose?). The V-04 → V-05 delta is a distinct upgrade: compliance auditable; intent not (V-04) vs. both auditable at the gate item level (V-05).
