### Excellence Signals

From **V-01** (top scorer):

1. **Predict-before-simulate structure** — committing to verdict, P1 blocker, P2 consensus, and named outlier referee before any report forces the model to justify when reality diverges from prediction. This is what makes C-09 (editorial dynamics) appear in the verdict naturally: the model has to explain why prediction was wrong, which requires knowing how that journal's process actually weights concerns.

2. **Divergence tracking field in each report** — `Diverges from predictive verdict: YES / NO — [explain]` makes minority positions structurally explicit rather than hoping they emerge from prose. The "Predicted outlier" framing explicitly names which referee will diverge from the other two, which is the C-10 requirement stated in structural terms.

From **V-04** (runner-up, note-worthy):

3. **Coaching register for persona grounding** — "Referee 2 should sound like someone who reviewed the power analysis on three failed replications last month" is harder to produce generic output against than "hostile statistician." The register enforces character where archetype labels can be cited but not inhabited.
