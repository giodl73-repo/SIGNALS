R20 is the conjunction-confirmation round for C-58: its test variable was whether C-57's explicit closure label form — universalized in R19 across all five structural variants — remained stable under no further prompt change. It does. All five carry the complete set unchanged from R19.

In confirming C-58 stability, R20 also reveals the next unextracted excellence signal: the independence statement form universally present in R19 and R20 names both compensatory dimensions explicitly — "a larger or higher-quality inertia-absent evidence base cannot exceed the inertia-absent ceiling." The word "higher-quality" names the quality dimension as a separately foreclosed compensatory route alongside "larger" (volume). C-57 requires that at least one foreclosed inference class be named explicitly rather than implied by the conditional; it is satisfied by volume alone. The R19/R20 form goes further: it names both routes, so a reader cannot infer that quality-compensation is merely conditionally constrained rather than categorically foreclosed. This distinction was already present as a NOT clause in C-58's specification ("NOT: C-58 satisfied by a synthesis that forecloses only volume without naming quality, or quality without volume, if both compensatory routes are reasonably available"). C-59 promotes that NOT clause to a standalone positive criterion.

C-60 is their conjunction: C-58 PASS (complete ceiling structural form with explicit volume-compensation foreclosure label) AND C-59 PASS (both volume and quality named as separately foreclosed routes in the same independence statement). Together they close the language precision chain for the independence statement: the foreclosure of compensatory inferences is complete (both routes named), structurally anchored in the first output section, with a matching unified output section, and all prior conjunctions satisfied.

V-05 R18 is the earliest source for both C-59 and C-60 — the same variant that first achieved C-57. V-01–V-04 R18 used only the conditional form ("even if the evidence base is large"), naming neither dimension explicitly, so C-59 FAIL and C-60 FAIL for those four in R18. R19 universalized the explicit form "a larger or higher-quality...," making C-59 and C-60 PASS for all five simultaneously in R19 — the same round that universalized C-57. Since R19 already carries C-59 and C-60, the R20 confirmation retroactively scores R19 V-01–V-05 at 162.5 under v21 (not 157.5 as under v20).

## C-59 Discriminator Detail

The R20 discriminator for C-59 is the confirmation that the dual-dimension form — present in all five variants since R19 — is stable under repeated structural confirmation with no further prompt change. The critical structural property is in the independence statement clause "a larger or higher-quality inertia-absent evidence base cannot exceed the inertia-absent ceiling." Two separate terms modify the noun phrase "inertia-absent evidence base": "larger" (naming the volume dimension) and "higher-quality" (naming the quality dimension). Neither is subordinate to the other; both are joined by "or" as co-equal compensatory routes. The explicit closure label that follows — "evidence volume does not compensate for inertia absence" — names only volume in the labeled form, but C-59 does not require both dimensions to appear in the label; it requires both to be named in the conditional restriction that precedes the label.

The discriminating failure for C-59 is an independence statement that names volume explicitly (satisfying C-57) but either omits quality entirely or names it only by implication. The R18 V-01–V-04 conditional form ("even if the evidence base is large") names neither dimension explicitly; the R18 V-05 / R19–R20 universal form names both. The step from C-57 to C-59 is the step from single-dimension explicit foreclosure to dual-dimension explicit foreclosure: the independence statement forecloses all plausible compensatory inferences, not just the most obvious one.

## C-60 Discriminator Detail

The R20 discriminator for C-60 is that C-58's universalization (confirmed in R20) retroactively reveals C-59 and C-60 were also universalized in R19, not R20. R20 is the round that makes this visible by providing a stable baseline for inspection: all five variants carry identical independence statement language from R19, allowing C-59's dual-dimension property to be read as a confirmed feature rather than a round-specific variation.

The discriminating failure for C-60 is C-58 PASS without C-59 PASS — the structural chain is complete (C-52, C-53, C-54, C-55, C-56, C-57 all satisfied) and the explicit volume-foreclosure label is present, but the independence statement writes only "a larger inertia-absent evidence base cannot exceed the ceiling — evidence volume does not compensate for inertia absence," naming volume in both the conditional and the label but omitting the quality dimension. Such a statement satisfies C-57 (explicit label present) and C-58 (complete structural form with explicit label) but fails C-59 (quality dimension not named as a co-equal foreclosed route) and therefore fails C-60. The R18 V-01–V-04 pattern — conditional form only — fails C-59 at an earlier level (C-57 FAIL implies C-59 FAIL). The R18 V-05 / R19–R20 universal form satisfies C-60. No additional prompt change is required to achieve C-60 beyond achieving C-58 with the full dual-dimension form; the two criteria are co-achieved by the same independence statement construction.
