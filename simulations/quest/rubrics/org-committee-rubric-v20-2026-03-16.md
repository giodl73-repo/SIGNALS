# org:committee Rubric -- v20

**Skill**: `org:committee`
**Version**: 20 (updated from Round 20 scoring -- 2026-03-16)
**Changelog**: Added C-50, C-51, and C-52 from R20 excellence signals. All three close gaps left by
C-47, C-48, and C-49 respectively -- each new criterion addresses a dimension of enforcement
completeness that the v19 rule required structurally but did not require to be self-documenting or
architecturally visible.

C-50 requires the ENFORCE clause on each symmetry criterion (C-47 and C-48) to include not only a
positive requirement but an explicit named violation condition stating the exact asymmetric
configuration that breaks the rule (e.g., "C-47 fails if INERTIA INVARIANT VALIDATE enumerates
three axes and STANCE INVARIANT VALIDATE enumerates fewer"); without C-50 a symmetry ENFORCE that
states only "must be structurally identical" leaves the violation boundary implicit -- a model must
infer what counts as non-identical rather than reading a named failure boundary at the enforcement
point. The scoring evidence was explicit: V-04's ENFORCE clauses for both C-47 and C-48 each
carried "C-NN fails if [specific asymmetric form]" language as the distinguishing structural signal
beyond V-01's positive-only ENFORCE phrasing.

C-51 requires the enforcement infrastructure for each cross-structure symmetry requirement (C-47
and C-48) to be organized as a named skeleton subsection (e.g., "SEALING CONTRACT INTEGRITY
SYMMETRY -- STANCE INVARIANT", "COUPLING INTEGRITY SYMMETRY -- PHASE-3-COMMIT bidirectionality
clause") rather than embedded as inline ENFORCE instructions appended to the parent fill rule;
without C-51 a symmetry requirement is architecturally invisible -- readable only by examining the
parent fill rule's full text -- while a named subsection makes the parity contract independently
navigable by section heading. V-04's structural signature explicitly identified both symmetry
requirements as named subsections as the architectural distinguishing pattern from V-01's inline
ENFORCE approach.

C-52 requires the ENFORCE clause on the SYNTHESIZE instruction's VALIDATE (C-49) to include a
named independence principle stating that the two FAILS axes test distinct violation classes, not
merely label them with different names (e.g., "structural absence and incorrect affirmation are
distinct testable violations"); without C-52 an ENFORCE that labels both axes as independently
required does not explain why independent labeling is necessary -- a model could carry both labels
as co-required conditions without the logical principle that makes collapsing them incorrect. Both
V-04's ENFORCE ("structural absence and incorrect affirmation are distinct testable violations") and
V-03's explanatory clause ("Why two separate FAILS axes? Because 'absent' and 'wrong' are different
errors") carried the independence principle as the distinguishing element beyond V-01's "both FAILS
axes are independently required" formulation. Aspirational pool grows from 41 to 44 criteria;
aspirational max grows from 82 to 88 pts; composite max is now 178.

## Essential Criteria (60 pts total -- must all pass)

| ID | Criterion | Category | Weight | Pass Condition |
|----|-----------|----------|--------|----------------|
| C-01 | **Committee type declared correctly** | correctness | essential | The output identifies the committee type (ROB, shiproom, arch-board, or user-specified type) in the opening. An output that proceeds without declaring which committee is running fails. |
| C-02 | **Participants loaded from `.craft/roles/`** | correctness | essential | Every speaker is a named role from the relevant charter file. Generic or unnamed speakers fail. Graceful fallback to default Signal roles with disclosure passes if no charter exists. |
| C-03 | **Each participant speaks from their role lens** | depth | essential | Every participant's contribution reflects their role's documented orientation. Role-blind statements fail. |
| C-04 | **Output includes all three required sections** | format | essential | Minutes contain: decisions reached, action items with owners, dissenting opinions or explicit "no dissent" with reasoning. Missing any section fails. |
| C-05 | **At least one challenge, condition, or block is surfaced** | behavior | essential | No pure rubber-stamps. At least one participant must raise a challenge, block, or approval condition. |

## Recommended Criteria (30 pts total)

| ID | Criterion | Category | Weight | Pass Condition |
|----|-----------|----------|--------|----------------|
| C-06 | **Agenda item specificity** | depth | recommended | Simulation references the specific agenda item throughout; speaker contributions respond to prior concerns, not generic positions. |
| C-07 | **Action items are owned and actionable** | format | recommended | Format `[Owner Role] -- [specific action] -- [deadline]`. Vague items ("investigate further") fail. All items must be owned and specific. |
| C-08 | **Dissents include resolution paths** | behavior | recommended | The dissenting opinions section captures each dissent with an explicit path to resolution or re-entry condition. Dissent capture without a path is a partial pass. |
