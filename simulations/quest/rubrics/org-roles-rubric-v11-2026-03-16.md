# Scoring Rubric: org-roles (v11)

Evaluates output from the `org-roles` skill, which generates a typed role registry for a domain by analyzing product, codebase, or spec context and emitting structured role files to `.roles/{area}/`.

**v11 changes**: Added C-48 and C-49 from Round 10 excellence signals. Aspirational denominator updated from 39 to 41. Notes for Evaluators extended.

---
## Criteria

### Essential (60 pts total — all must pass)

| ID | Text | Category | Weight | Pass Condition |
|----|------|----------|--------|----------------|
| C-01 | **Role schema completeness** — every emitted role file contains all six required fields: `name`, `orientation` (with `frame` and `serves` sub-fields), `lens` (with `verify_questions` and `simplify_rules` sub-fields), `expertise` (with `depth` and `relevance` sub-fields), `scope`, and `collaborates_with`. | correctness | essential | PASS if 100% of role files contain all six top-level fields and the required sub-fields under `orientation`, `lens`, and `expertise`. FAIL if any field or sub-field is absent or empty in any role file. |
| C-02 | **Devil-advocate role present** — an inertia-check role exists whose stated purpose is to challenge every proposal by arguing the status quo is sufficient. | correctness | essential | PASS if at least one role file has a name or orientation that explicitly identifies it as a devil-advocate / status-quo challenger, and its `lens.verify_questions` include at least one question of the form "why is the current approach insufficient?" FAIL if no such role exists or its job is generic skepticism without a status-quo framing. |
| C-03 | **Stock roles present** — PM, Architect, and Strategy roles are always emitted regardless of domain input. | coverage | essential | PASS if the output directory contains at least one file identifiable as each of: a product manager role, a software/systems architect role, and a strategy/business role. FAIL if any of the three stock roles is absent. |
| C-04 | **Output location correct** — all role files are written to `.roles/{area}/` as individual Markdown files, where `{area}` reflects the domain derived from context. | format | essential | PASS if all files land in a `.roles/` subdirectory named after the target area, and each file is a `.md` file. FAIL if files are written to a different path, bundled into a single file, or the area subdirectory is missing or named generically (e.g., `default`). |
| C-05 | **At least one context-derived domain expert** — the role set includes at least one domain expert role whose name, frame, and verify questions are specific to the analyzed product/codebase/spec, not a generic template. | correctness | essential | PASS if at least one role has a `frame` that references a domain-specific concern (e.g., "data pipeline correctness", "auth boundary enforcement", "signal fidelity") that could not apply verbatim to an unrelated domain. FAIL if every role beyond the three stock roles is a generic archetype (e.g., "Domain Expert") with no domain-specific content. |

### Recommended (30 pts total — output is better with these)

| ID | Text | Category | Weight | Pass Condition |
|----|------|----------|--------|----------------|
| C-06 | **Orientation coherence** — `frame` describes the role's epistemic viewpoint and `serves` names the stakeholder or system concern that benefits from that perspective; the two sub-fields are internally consistent and non-redundant. | depth | recommended | PASS if, for every role, `frame` is a perspective statement (how this role sees the world) and `serves` is a beneficiary statement (who or what depends on this role's signal), and the two are logically related. FAIL if `serves` is a restatement of `frame`, or if either field is blank or generic ("users", "the team"). |
| C-07 | **Lens quality** — `verify_questions` are actionable and testable (answerable by reading artifacts or running code), and `simplify_rules` are opinionated constraints that reduce scope or remove ambiguity. | depth | recommended | PASS if each role's `verify_questions` list contains at least two questions that reference specific artifacts, behaviors, or measurable outcomes; and `simplify_rules` contains at least one rule that excludes or constrains (not just describes). FAIL if questions are rhetorical or unmeasurable, or if simplify_rules are restatements of scope rather than constraints. |
| C-08 | **Collaborates_with accuracy** — collaboration relationships are architecturally sound and directionally meaningful; if role A lists role B, role B's scope plausibly requires input from or produces output for role A. The instruction explicitly prohibits both phantom role references and universalist listing. | correctness | recommended | PASS if every `collaborates_with` entry names another role that exists in the output set, and the pairing reflects a genuine handoff or dependency (not just "everyone works with everyone"). The skill instruction must prohibit (1) phantom roles — names absent from the role set, and (2) universalist listing — "works with everyone" or equivalent. FAIL if either prohibition is absent from the instruction, or if the output contains phantom names or universalist entries. PARTIAL if only one of the two prohibitions is present in the instruction. |

### Aspirational (10 pts total — raise the bar)

| ID | Text | Category | Weight | Pass Condition |
|----|------|----------|--------|----------------|
| C-09 | **Role differentiation** — each role's `lens.verify_questions` surface concerns that no other role would catch; the set of roles together covers the decision surface without redundant overlap. | depth | aspirational | PASS if a reviewer can identify at least one verify question per role that is unique to that role's frame and would be missed by all other roles in this registry. FAIL if two or more roles share the majority of their verify questions or if any role's questions are a strict subset of another's. |
| C-10 | **Registry summary completeness** — the skill emits a registry summary artifact (inline comment, frontmatter, or companion file) whose content is fully specified: area name, total role count, stock roles listed by name, domain experts listed by name with their derivation source, and any coverage gaps the skill detected in Step 1. | format | aspirational | PASS if a summary artifact exists that explicitly states all five required fields: area, total count, stock role names, domain expert names with derivation source, detected gaps. FAIL if the summary is a heading-only stub (step named but content undefined), or if any of the five fields is absent and the caller must inspect individual files to reconstruct that information. |
| C-11 | **Context-first derivation ordering** — domain experts are derived from context analysis before stock roles are loaded; the skill's step sequence forces the model to name domain-specific concerns before encountering the PM/Architect/Strategy archetypes. | design | aspirational | PASS if the skill's step sequence places context analysis and domain expert derivation in earlier steps than stock role emission, and the domain expert `frame` fields reference concerns identified in the context analysis step. FAIL if stock roles are defined before domain experts are derived, or if domain expert frames could have been written without reading context (i.e., they are generic archetypes with domain labels applied after the fact). |
| C-12 | **Failure-mode-named field constraints** — schema field instructions name the exact failure mode to avoid, not just the positive requirement; for sibling fields that tend to collapse into restatements (e.g., `frame`/`serves`, `verify_questions`/`simplify_rules`), the instruction explicitly states what not to do. | design | aspirational | PASS if at least two schema field instructions include a negative constraint that names the specific failure mode (e.g., "Must be a different statement from frame, not a paraphrase" rather than "frame and serves must differ"), and the stock role examples demonstrate the constraint by construction rather than instruction alone. FAIL if all field instructions are phrased purely as positive requirements, leaving the failure mode implicit. |
| C-13 | **Explicit step output format** — every skill step that names an output artifact defines the artifact's format fields, required content, and structure explicitly; no step is a heading-only stub. | completeness | aspirational | PASS if every step that produces an artifact (role file, registry summary, or other output) specifies the exact fields, format, and minimum required content for that artifact within the step definition. FAIL if any step names an output (e.g., "Write the registry summary") without defining what that output must contain — heading-only stubs are a structural omission, not a documentation gap. |
| C-14 | **Collaborates_with typed dual-failure prohibition** — the `collaborates_with` field instruction names both failure modes with explicit type labels: FAILURE MODE (type 1) for phantom/non-existent role references; FAILURE MODE (type 2) for universalist listing ("works with everyone"). Typed labeling goes beyond the dual-prohibition required by C-08; it gives the model a recovery path when it detects a violation. | design | aspirational | PASS if the instruction labels both failure modes with distinct type identifiers (numbered, named, or equivalent). PARTIAL if both prohibitions are present (satisfying C-08) but neither carries a type label or named classification. FAIL if either prohibition is absent (C-08 already fails in that case; C-14 inherits the failure). |
| C-15 | **Registry heading-stub failure mode** — the REGISTRY.md step explicitly names the heading-only stub as a FAIL condition: a section header present with no required content beneath it is not a complete registry entry. This forces the model to distinguish structural presence from content completion. | design | aspirational | PASS if the registry step (or its associated output format spec) includes a named FAIL example of the form: "'## Registry Summary' with no content below it fails C-10" or equivalent. PARTIAL if the registry step defines a completion condition (see C-16) that implicitly prevents a heading stub, but does not name the stub failure mode explicitly. FAIL if no heading-stub failure mode is named and no completion condition is defined. |
| C-16 | **Step completion condition** — every output-producing step defines a binary completion test the model can apply before proceeding: not just a format template, but a checkable condition that determines whether the step is done. Format definition (C-13) and completion condition (C-16) are distinct requirements. | completeness | aspirational | PASS if every step that emits an artifact includes a completion condition expressed as a verifiable test (e.g., "all five fields are present", "every derived expert names its source concern", "no field is an empty string"). PARTIAL if at least one output step has a completion condition but others do not. FAIL if no step defines a completion condition and the model must infer doneness from format alone. |
| C-17 | **Worked examples in field constraints** — at least one failure-mode-named constraint (see C-12) includes a worked example or before/after illustration, not just a label. A label names the failure; a worked example shows it. Both together allow the model to calibrate rather than guess at the boundary. | depth | aspirational | PASS if at least three schema field instructions include a concrete worked example (a filled-in bad value, a before/after pair, or a labeled counter-example). PARTIAL if at least one worked example is present but fewer than three fields are illustrated. FAIL if all failure-mode constraints are label-only with no illustrative content. |
| C-18 | **Contrastive worked example pairs** — every field instruction that includes a worked example shows both a failure specimen and a passing specimen in the same field comment; showing only one direction leaves the boundary calibration incomplete. A label names the failure (C-12). A bad example shows it (C-17). A bad-plus-good pair closes the loop by also showing what correct looks like from the same vantage. | depth | aspirational | PASS if at least two schema field instructions include a WORKED EXAMPLE (bad) and WORKED EXAMPLE (good) pair in the same field comment — both directions in the same location. PARTIAL if at least one field has a contrastive pair but fewer than two fields have both directions. FAIL if all worked examples (even those satisfying C-17) show only one direction. |
| C-19 | **Enumerated multi-item completion gate** — each output-producing step's completion condition (C-16 level) is expressed as a numbered or bulleted checklist with at least three independently verifiable items, not a single compound predicate; a multi-item gate exposes individual failure points that a single "all conditions met" test would collapse and hide. | completeness | aspirational | PASS if every output-producing step has a completion gate listing at least three independently verifiable items (e.g., Phase 2 gate: 1) all four stock role names present, 2) inertia-advocate has a status-quo question, 3) no domain-expert names appear in stock role frames, 4) each role has a distinct frame). PARTIAL if at least one step has a multi-item gate (three or more items) but other output steps have only a single-condition test or no condition. FAIL if no step has a multi-item gate. |
| C-20 | **Domain-specific registry extension** — the registry summary template explicitly designates at least one field beyond the required five as a domain-specific extension slot, and that field is grounded in the context analysis step; the extension is named in the step output spec before any role is written, not added ad hoc to the final artifact. Registry extensibility is evidence that the skill design reached beyond the required baseline into the domain it was asked to model. | design | aspirational | PASS if the registry step's output template includes at least one named extension field that is (a) distinct from the five required fields and (b) references a concern identified in the context/domain analysis step. FAIL if the registry template contains only the five required fields with no extension mechanism, or if any additional field is not grounded in an earlier context analysis step. |
| C-21 | **Uniqueness mandate by construction** — the skill instruction or domain expert derivation step explicitly requires at least one verify question per role to be uniquely attributable to that role's frame, stated in language the model applies while authoring questions, not as a post-hoc evaluation criterion. The canonical formulation is: "a question no other role in this registry would prioritize first." C-09 tests whether the output is unique; C-21 tests whether the instruction prevents duplication by construction before any output is produced. | design | aspirational | PASS if the role template or derivation step includes an explicit statement requiring at least one verify question per role to be uniquely tied to that role's frame — phrased so the model must confirm uniqueness as part of writing each question, not after the full set is generated. FAIL if uniqueness is detectable only by post-hoc evaluation (C-09 standard) and the instruction does not mandate it by name. |
| C-22 | **Cross-step artifact count integrity** — the registry summary's `total_roles` count (or equivalent aggregate field) is explicitly verified against the number of role files emitted in the role-writing step, via a gate item or inline cross-reference instruction; the model cannot complete the registry without checking the prior step's actual output count. C-10 tests registry content; C-22 tests whether the count field is independently grounded in a prior artifact rather than self-reported by the registry step itself. | completeness | aspirational | PASS if a gate item or cross-reference instruction explicitly requires the registry aggregate count to match the file or entry count produced in the role-writing step (e.g., "total_roles must equal the number of files written in Phase N"). FAIL if the count is written without a cross-step verification requirement — the registry step fills in the number from its own state rather than checking the prior step's output. |
| C-23 | **Extension field commitment block with provenance** — the registry extension field (C-20) is introduced in the skill's earliest step in a named commitment block that specifies three elements: (a) the extension field name, (b) the population source — the step and artifact that will provide the field's value, and (c) the extension's purpose — what domain concern it captures. C-20 requires the extension to be named before role writing begins; C-23 requires a formal three-element commitment structure in the first step, giving the model a complete contract before any context is read. | design | aspirational | PASS if the skill's first step contains a named commitment block (EXTENSION-COMMITMENT or equivalent) that explicitly states all three elements: field name, population source step, and purpose. FAIL if the extension field is introduced informally (name only, without population source or purpose), or if the commitment block appears in any step other than the first, or if any of the three elements is absent. |
| C-24 | **Schema field name precision gate** — the role-writing step's completion gate explicitly names at least two required schema field identifiers as string literals (e.g., `verify_questions`, `simplify_rules`), distinguishing between correct naming and near-miss variants that pass visual review but fail downstream consumers. C-01 tests field presence in output; C-24 tests that the gate enforces naming precision, preventing integration-breaking shortcuts (e.g., `verify`, `simplify`) from passing the gate. | completeness | aspirational | PASS if the role-file completion gate contains at least one item that checks schema fields by their exact identifier names — the field names are quoted or marked as literals in the gate condition, not referenced by description alone. FAIL if the gate checks field presence or count without naming the expected identifiers explicitly. |
| C-25 | **Per-expert attribute gate in domain derivation** — the context analysis step's completion gate enumerates at least four named attributes per derived domain expert (e.g., name, concern link, domain-vocabulary frame, verify focus), verified per expert in the gate rather than as an aggregate count; this ensures each derivation record is complete before stock roles are loaded. C-05 tests output specificity in the final artifacts; C-25 tests that the gate holds each derived expert to a minimum attribute record before the context analysis step is considered done. | completeness | aspirational | PASS if the context analysis gate includes an item that checks at least four named attributes for each domain expert individually (not just "N experts are listed"). FAIL if the gate verifies expert count or existence without enumerating the per-expert attributes that must be present for each entry. |
| C-26 | **Named binding field contract** — the skill defines a dedicated, numbered contract block (e.g., FC-1 through FC-N) that enumerates schema field identifiers as binding specifications — not as template comments but as standalone contract entries that all gates and templates can reference by identifier. C-24 tests that a gate quotes field names as literals; C-26 tests whether a single authoritative contract structure exists, so field specifications are defined once and referenced everywhere rather than restated per step. | design | aspirational | PASS if the skill contains a named, multi-entry contract block (labeled FC-N, FIELD CONTRACT, or equivalent) in which each entry binds a schema field identifier to its specification, and at least one gate or template references that block by name or number rather than restating the spec inline. FAIL if field specifications are only stated within individual step templates or gate comments with no authoritative contract block. |
| C-27 | **Pre-write count variable declaration** — the cross-step count verification (C-22 level) uses a named intermediate variable (e.g., PHASE5_COUNT) declared in a PREPARE sub-step before the WRITE phase of the registry step begins; the registry's `total_roles` field is explicitly bound to this variable. C-22 tests that a gate instruction requires cross-step verification; C-27 tests the mechanism — a named variable declared as a pre-write input rather than a post-write gate check, making the count a confirmed input to the WRITE phase rather than an unverified number the model supplies while writing. | completeness | aspirational | PASS if the registry step contains a named PREPARE sub-step (or equivalent) that (a) enumerates the prior step's output, (b) records the count under a named variable, and (c) the WRITE phase references that variable as the source for `total_roles`. The prohibition must be explicit: derivation from a prior-phase plan (e.g., "I listed 4 roles in Phase 3 so total_roles = 4") is named as a C-27 failure. FAIL if the count is verified only by a post-write gate item or is not bound to a named pre-write variable. |
| C-28 | **Generic placeholder name prohibition** — the skill instruction explicitly names at least one anti-pattern for domain expert naming that would escape superficial review but violate C-05 specificity (e.g., "domain-expert", "expert-1", "generic-expert"). C-05 tests that output domain experts are specific; C-28 tests that the instruction closes a specific failure class by naming the placeholder patterns that superficially resemble domain expert names while carrying no domain content. | design | aspirational | PASS if the skill instruction or domain expert derivation step explicitly prohibits at least one class of generic placeholder name by example (e.g., "'domain-expert' and 'expert-1' are prohibited — the name must come from the context"). FAIL if the instruction states a positive requirement for domain-specific naming (C-05 level) without naming the placeholder anti-patterns that bypass that requirement. |
| C-29 | **Contract violation framing for dual prohibitions** — the `collaborates_with` dual-failure prohibition (C-14 level typed labels) is stated as a named CONTRACT VIOLATION in a dedicated contract block, not merely as failure modes embedded in a template comment; framing a violation as a contract breach makes the prohibition load-bearing rather than advisory, and gives the model a categorical failure state rather than a graduated fault. C-14 requires typed labels (type 1, type 2); C-29 requires those labels to appear inside a named contract structure where each entry is framed as a contract violation. | design | aspirational | PASS if the instruction contains a named contract block (FC-N, FIELD CONTRACT, or equivalent) in which the collaborates_with dual prohibitions are explicitly labeled as CONTRACT VIOLATION (type 1) and CONTRACT VIOLATION (type 2) — the word "violation" (or "breach") appears in the label. PARTIAL if both prohibitions carry type labels (C-14 PASS) but use "FAILURE MODE" or similar advisory framing rather than "CONTRACT VIOLATION." FAIL if either prohibition is absent (C-14 already fails; C-29 inherits). |
| C-30 | **Extension purpose tied to diagnostic question** — the extension commitment block's purpose element (C-23 third element) is explicitly stated as the answer to the opening diagnostic question that motivated the extension — not a generic description of what the field stores, but a direct statement that the field exists to answer a specific named question from Phase 0. C-23 requires three elements (name + population source + purpose); C-30 requires the purpose element to close the loop back to the diagnostic question that opens the skill, grounding the extension in the skill's own design rationale rather than in convenience. | design | aspirational | PASS if the EXTENSION-COMMITMENT block's purpose sub-field is explicitly framed as answering the skill's Phase 0 (or opening) diagnostic question — e.g., "purpose: answers the Phase 0 diagnostic question 'what is the strongest existing-system argument that makes {topic} premature?'" FAIL if the purpose is a description of the field's content (e.g., "preserves the status-quo argument") without explicitly connecting it to a named diagnostic question. |
| C-31 | **Multi-site placeholder prohibition enforcement** — the generic placeholder name prohibition (C-28 level) appears in at least three independent checkpoints within the skill instruction: once in the field contract block, once in the domain expert derivation step's prose (a DO NOT / PROHIBITED / FORBIDDEN section), and at least once in a gate condition (derivation gate or write gate); this ensures the model encounters the prohibition at the point of definition, at the point of execution, and at the point of verification. C-28 requires the prohibition to exist at least once; C-31 requires it to be enforced redundantly across a minimum of three independent checkpoints so that a model that skips or misreads one location will encounter it at another. | design | aspirational | PASS if the placeholder name prohibition (the specific anti-pattern names from C-28) appears in at least three independent locations: (a) the field contract block, (b) the derivation step's prose or DO-NOT section, and (c) at least one gate condition. FAIL if the prohibition appears in fewer than three independent checkpoints, even if C-28 is satisfied. |
| C-32 | **Count bypass failure class named in PREPARE** — the PREPARE sub-step (C-27 level) explicitly names the specific bypass failure it prevents: "deriving total_roles from a prior-phase plan count (e.g., 'I planned N roles in Phase X, so total_roles = N') rather than enumerating the actual output files is a failure." C-27 tests that a named variable is declared before WRITE begins; C-32 tests that the step applies the same anti-pattern-naming discipline (C-28 principle) to count integrity — the instruction names the specific shortcut that the mechanism was designed to prevent, not just the correct procedure. | completeness | aspirational | PASS if the PREPARE sub-step or an adjacent instruction explicitly names the bypass failure class for count integrity — i.e., names "deriving the count from a prior-phase plan rather than enumerating actual output files" as a specific, named failure. The failure class need not use the exact label "C-27 failure" but the anti-pattern must be described by name. FAIL if the PREPARE sub-step declares the mechanism (declare variable, enumerate files) without naming the specific bypass anti-pattern the mechanism was designed to prevent. A variate cannot PASS C-32 while failing C-27 — the bypass naming presupposes the PREPARE mechanism exists. |
| C-33 | **Contract-to-template prohibition mirroring** — the CONTRACT VIOLATION labels from the FC contract block (C-29 level) are also replicated in the role file template's `collaborates_with` field annotation, so the prohibition appears both at the authoritative definition site (the contract block where it is binding) and at the model's write site (the template where collaborations are authored); this dual-site presence means the model cannot write a collaborates_with entry without encountering the prohibition. C-29 tests that the contract block contains CONTRACT VIOLATION framing; C-33 tests that those same labels are mirrored in the template annotation, making the prohibition redundantly enforced where violations are most likely to occur. | design | aspirational | PASS if the role file template's `collaborates_with` annotation includes both CONTRACT VIOLATION (type 1) and CONTRACT VIOLATION (type 2) labels — copied or paraphrased from the FC block — in addition to their appearance in the contract block itself. FAIL if the CONTRACT VIOLATION labels appear only in the FC contract block without any mirroring in the template annotation, even if C-29 is satisfied. PARTIAL if only one of the two violation labels is mirrored in the template. A variate cannot PASS C-33 while failing C-29 — template mirroring presupposes the contract framing exists. |
| C-34 | **Inertia-awareness per expert in derivation gate** — the domain expert derivation gate (C-25 level, four attributes) enumerates a fifth named attribute per derived expert record: the expert's inertia gap — the specific status-quo argument this expert would deploy against the feature or change under review; this attribute connects each domain expert to the inertia-advocate role by anchoring the advocate's claim to a named expert domain. C-25 tests that the gate checks at least four attributes per expert; C-34 tests that a fifth attribute — inertia gap — makes the derivation gate inertia-aware, not just domain-aware, so the inertia-advocate's frame is grounded in per-expert analysis rather than derived generically. | completeness | aspirational | PASS if the context analysis step's completion gate enumerates five named per-expert attributes, and the fifth attribute is explicitly identified as the inertia gap (or status-quo argument, or equivalent formulation) that records what resistance this expert's domain would generate toward change. FAIL if the gate enumerates only four attributes (C-25 level) with no inertia-gap entry, or if an inertia-related field exists but is not checked per expert in the gate. A variate cannot PASS C-34 while failing C-25 — inertia-gap attribution presupposes the per-expert attribute gate exists. |
| C-35 | **Pre-YAML per-role reasoning artifact** — before any YAML role file is written, the skill requires completing a named structured artifact per role (Diagnosis Card or equivalent) that includes an explicit uniqueness argument naming the closest overlap role and stating how the current role is distinct; this externalization of C-21's uniqueness mandate into a verifiable pre-YAML artifact makes uniqueness confirmation a structural prerequisite for YAML authoring, not merely a template annotation. C-21 tests that the instruction mandates uniqueness by language during role authoring; C-35 tests that the mechanism is a named artifact — with explicit format fields and a required uniqueness argument — whose completion gate must pass before the WRITE phase begins. | design | aspirational | PASS if the skill contains a step that requires producing a named per-role artifact (Diagnosis Card, pre-YAML reasoning record, or equivalent) for every role before any YAML is written, and that artifact's format explicitly includes a uniqueness argument that names the closest overlap role and the distinguishing difference. FAIL if uniqueness is enforced only via template language during YAML writing (C-21 level) without a named pre-YAML artifact structure. PARTIAL if a pre-YAML reasoning step exists but does not require naming the closest overlap role — i.e., the uniqueness argument is open-ended rather than comparative. A variate cannot PASS C-35 while failing C-21 — the pre-YAML artifact presupposes the instruction mandates uniqueness. |
| C-36 | **Cross-set pre-YAML uniqueness scan gate** — after all per-role pre-YAML artifacts (C-35 level) are completed and before any YAML writing begins, the skill requires a named cross-set uniqueness scan gate that checks the full registry's role set for shared primary verify questions; the scan is a discrete named step or gate item (not a per-role annotation) that can only be executed after all individual role artifacts exist. C-35 tests per-role uniqueness argumentation via a named artifact; C-36 tests whether the cross-set scan is a separate enforcement gate operating on the completed artifact set as a whole, catching cross-role duplication that per-role reasoning cannot detect at the time each card is written. | completeness | aspirational | PASS if the skill contains a named cross-set uniqueness scan (CROSS-CARD UNIQUENESS SCAN, REGISTRY UNIQUENESS GATE, or equivalent) that is positioned after all per-role pre-YAML artifacts are complete and before the WRITE phase begins, and that scan checks the full role set — not individual roles — for shared primary verify questions. FAIL if the only uniqueness checking is per-role during artifact authoring (C-35 level) with no subsequent cross-set gate. PARTIAL if a cross-set check exists but is positioned inside the WRITE phase (after YAML writing has begun) rather than as a pre-WRITE gate. A variate cannot PASS C-36 while failing C-35 — the cross-set scan presupposes the per-role pre-YAML artifacts exist to scan. |
| C-37 | **Anchor-card ordering in per-role artifact pipeline** — the inertia-advocate's per-role pre-YAML artifact (C-35 level Diagnosis Card or equivalent) is explicitly designated as the first-authored card — the ANCHOR-CARD — and every subsequent non-anchor card is required to include a named orthogonality field that explicitly states how that role's primary verify question is orthogonal to the anchor card's verify question. Ordering the inertia-advocate first makes the status-quo challenge available as a reference axis before any domain expert card is authored; the per-card orthogonality field forces each expert to position itself relative to the advocate rather than in isolation. C-35 tests that named per-role pre-YAML artifacts exist and require a uniqueness argument; C-37 tests whether the artifact pipeline is sequenced with the inertia-advocate card as an explicit anchor and whether non-anchor cards carry a direct orthogonality check against it. | design | aspirational | PASS if the skill's per-role artifact step (a) designates the inertia-advocate card as ANCHOR-CARD with explicit first-authored ordering, and (b) requires every non-anchor card to include a named orthogonality field that references the anchor card and states the distinguishing angle. PARTIAL if the inertia-advocate card is written first without the per-card orthogonality field requirement (ordering without per-card tracing). FAIL if no anchor-card designation exists. A variate cannot PASS C-37 while failing C-35 — anchor designation presupposes the named per-role artifact pipeline exists. |
| C-38 | **Anchor-referenced structured uniqueness scan** — the cross-set pre-YAML uniqueness scan (C-36 level) is expressed as a named multi-step procedure in which the anchor card (inertia-advocate) serves as the explicit reference axis; the scan separates the anchor-orthogonality phase (checking every non-anchor card against the anchor) from the non-anchor pairwise phase (checking non-anchor cards against each other), and includes a named revision step for flagged cards; this structure ensures the inertia-advocate's frame is treated as a first-class axis of differentiation rather than one of N equal comparisons. C-36 tests that a named cross-set scan gate exists; C-38 tests whether that gate is decomposed into named phases with the anchor card as the explicit reference axis, making the status-quo challenge the organizing spine of the uniqueness proof. | design | aspirational | PASS if the cross-set uniqueness scan is expressed as a named multi-step procedure with at least three named phases: (a) a list or enumeration of all verify questions from completed cards, (b) an anchor-orthogonality phase that checks each non-anchor card against the anchor card specifically, and (c) a non-anchor pairwise phase that checks remaining non-anchor cards against each other, followed by a revision step for flagged entries.           + (recommended_pass / 3 * 30)
          + (aspirational_pass / 41 * 10)

## Notes for Evaluators

- **C-01 takes priority**: a role file missing any schema field invalidates C-06, C-07, C-08 for that role. Score what is present; note what is absent.
- **C-01/C-07 naming risk**: the schema must use exact field names `verify_questions` and `simplify_rules` (not `verify`/`simplify`). Downstream skills (org-review, org-chart) read role files by exact field name. A schema that uses shortened names passes manual review but fails integration.
- **C-02 (devil-advocate)** is non-negotiable for Signal's design philosophy. A generic "critic" role that challenges quality rather than the status quo does not satisfy this criterion.
- **C-05 vs. C-03**: the stock roles (C-03) are always expected; domain expert roles (C-05) prove the skill read context. Evaluate them independently.
- **C-08 vs. C-14**: C-08 requires that both prohibitions exist in the instruction. C-14 requires that they be explicitly typed with labels. A variate can PASS C-08 (both present) and PARTIAL C-14 (not typed). Evaluate C-08 first; if C-08 fails, C-14 inherits the failure.
- **C-09 vs. C-21**: C-09 is evaluated by reading the output — does each role have a unique verify question? C-21 is evaluated by reading the instruction — does it mandate uniqueness with explicit language before output is produced? A variate where the output happens to be unique (C-09 PASS) but the instruction never required it (C-21 FAIL) is common. Conversely, a variate whose instruction mandates uniqueness (C-21 PASS) but whose output fails (C-09 FAIL) is a design success with an execution failure. Evaluate both independently.
- **C-09** requires a comparative read across all role files — do not evaluate per-file in isolation.
- **C-10 vs. C-13**: C-10 tests whether the registry summary *content* is complete (all five fields present). C-13 tests whether *every step* that names an output defines its format. A skill can fail C-13 (heading-only stub in Step 5) while also failing C-10 (no summary content). Fix C-13 first — it is the structural cause of C-10 failures.
- **C-10 vs. C-15**: C-10 tests content completeness (five fields). C-15 tests whether the stub failure mode is named as a FAIL condition. A variate can PASS C-10 (all five fields present) and still FAIL C-15 (no explicit stub-failure-mode instruction). Evaluate independently.
- **C-10 vs. C-20**: C-10 tests the required five fields. C-20 tests for domain-specific extension beyond those five. A variate can PASS C-10 (all five present) and FAIL C-20 (no extension field). A variate cannot PASS C-20 while failing C-10 — extension presupposes the baseline.
- **C-10 vs. C-22**: C-10 tests registry content (all five fields present including total_roles). C-22 tests how the count field is populated — is it derived from a prior step's actual output? A variate can PASS C-10 (count present) and FAIL C-22 (count self-reported). A variate cannot PASS C-22 while failing C-10 — count integrity presupposes count presence.
- **C-11 (context-first ordering)**: evaluate by reading the step sequence, not the output. If stock roles appear in Step 1 and domain experts in Step 3, C-11 fails regardless of how domain-specific the output looks.
- **C-12 (failure-mode-named constraints)**: look for negative constructions in schema field comments — "not a paraphrase", "not a description", "not a generic archetype". A schema that only states the positive ("frame is an epistemic viewpoint") is weaker than one that names the failure ("frame must not restate serves").
- **C-13 vs. C-16**: C-13 is about output *format* (field names, structural shape). C-16 is about *completion condition* (a binary test the model applies before proceeding). A step can define its output format (PASS C-13) without defining when that output is considered done (FAIL C-16). Both are required for the strongest skill designs.
- **C-14 vs. C-29**: C-14 requires typed labels (type 1, type 2) on both prohibitions. C-29 requires those labels to use "CONTRACT VIOLATION" (or "breach") framing inside a named contract block. A variate can PASS C-14 (typed labels present) and FAIL C-29 (labels appear as "FAILURE MODE" in template). PARTIAL C-29 corresponds exactly to PASS C-14 with advisory framing. Evaluate C-14 first; C-29 inherits the failure if C-14 fails.
- **C-16 vs. C-19**: C-16 requires any completion condition per step. C-19 requires that condition to be a multi-item checklist (three or more independent items). A step with a single condition ("all five fields are present") PASSES C-16 and FAILS C-19. Evaluate C-16 first; if C-16 fails, C-19 inherits the failure.
- **C-17 vs. C-18**: C-17 requires at least one worked example per field comment (bad value, before/after, or counter-example). C-18 requires that at least two of those field comments show both a bad AND a good example — both directions in the same location. A field comment with only "FAILURE: task list — e.g., 'write tests, review PRs'" is C-17 level. The same comment that also adds "PASS: epistemic lens — e.g., 'Sees every proposal through the lens of operational cost'" is C-18 level. Evaluate C-17 first; a variate that fails C-17 cannot PASS C-18.
- **C-18 (contrastive pairs)**: count field comments that contain both directions, not fields that mention failure AND separate fields that mention success. Both directions must appear in the same field comment to constitute a contrastive pair.
- **C-19 (multi-item gate)**: count independently verifiable items per gate — items that could each individually fail while others pass. A gate that lists "1) output written, 2) output complete" is not multi-item in the C-19 sense; both items test the same condition from different angles. A gate where each item references a distinct schema element or constraint is multi-item.
- **C-20 (domain-specific extension)**: verify that the extension field is named in the step output spec before the role files are written — not discovered by reading the final artifact. An "Inertia surface" field that appears only in the finished REGISTRY.md does not satisfy C-20; it must appear in the step's output template so the model knows to populate it.
- **C-20 vs. C-23**: C-20 requires the extension field to be named before role writing begins. C-23 requires it to be named in the skill's first step with a formal three-element commitment (name + population source + purpose). A variate can PASS C-20 (extension named in Phase 2 prose, before Phase 4 role writing) and FAIL C-23 (no commitment block, or commitment appears in Phase 2 rather than Phase 0/first step). A variate cannot PASS C-23 while failing C-20.
- **C-21 (uniqueness mandate)**: look for language that names uniqueness as a writing constraint during role authoring, not just an evaluation criterion. The canonical signal is a formulation like "this question must be one that no other role in this registry would prioritize first." A rubric note telling the evaluator to check for uniqueness is not a C-21 pass; a template instruction telling the model to write a unique question is.
- **C-21 vs. C-35**: C-21 tests that the instruction mandates uniqueness by language during authoring. C-35 tests the stronger mechanism: a named pre-YAML artifact structure that requires a comparative uniqueness argument (naming the closest overlap role) as a format field. A variate can PASS C-21 (uniqueness language in template) and FAIL C-35 (no named pre-YAML artifact). A variate cannot PASS C-35 while failing C-21 — the pre-YAML artifact presupposes the instruction mandates uniqueness.
- **C-22 (cross-step count integrity)**: look for an explicit bridge between the registry count field and the prior step's output — a gate item like "total_roles equals the count from Phase N output" or an instruction like "verify total_roles against Phase N before writing the registry." A registry that happens to have the right count but derives it without checking is a C-22 FAIL.
- **C-22 vs. C-27**: C-22 is satisfied by any gate-level instruction that requires cross-step count verification. C-27 requires the stronger mechanism: a named variable declared in a PREPARE sub-step before WRITE begins. A variate with a post-write gate item that says "confirm total_roles matches Phase N file count" PASSES C-22 and FAILS C-27. Both matter: C-22 ensures the check exists; C-27 ensures it happens before writing starts.
- **C-23 (commitment block)**: the commitment block must appear in the skill's first (earliest) step and must contain all three elements. A block with field name and purpose but no population source FAILS C-23. A block that appears in Step 2 (even before role writing) FAILS C-23 if Step 0 or Step 1 exists without the block.
- **C-23 vs. C-30**: C-23 requires three elements in the commitment block (name + population source + purpose). C-30 requires the purpose element to be explicitly framed as answering the skill's opening diagnostic question. A purpose like "preserves the status-quo argument" is concrete and descriptive (PASS C-23) but does not name a diagnostic question (FAIL C-30). A purpose like "answers Phase 0 diagnostic: what status-quo argument will the inertia-advocate deploy?" satisfies both. A variate cannot PASS C-30 while failing C-23.
- **C-24 (schema field name precision gate)**: look for the field names quoted as literals in a gate condition — e.g., "gate item: `verify_questions` and `simplify_rules` are spelled in full, not shortened." A gate that says "all required fields are present" passes C-16/C-19 but not C-24. The distinguishing feature is that the field name strings appear explicitly in the gate text.
- **C-24 vs. C-26**: C-24 tests that a gate quotes field identifiers as literals. C-26 tests that a dedicated contract block exists as the authoritative source for those identifiers — so gates can reference the contract by number (FC-4, FC-5) rather than restating the spec. A variate can PASS C-24 (gate quotes literals inline) and FAIL C-26 (no contract block, just inline quoting). A variate that PASSes C-26 typically also satisfies C-24 by construction, but evaluate them independently.
- **C-25 (per-expert attribute gate)**: the four attributes must be checked per expert in the gate, not as portfolio-level conditions. A gate that says "all derived experts have domain-vocabulary frames" is closer but still fails C-25 if it does not enumerate all four attributes by name. Look for a gate item that lists each of the required attributes (e.g., "each expert entry has: name, concern link, domain-vocabulary frame, verify focus").
- **C-25 vs. C-34**: C-25 tests that the derivation gate checks at least four named attributes per expert. C-34 tests that a fifth attribute — inertia gap — is added to the per-expert gate record. A variate can PASS C-25 (four attributes) and FAIL C-34 (no inertia gap). A variate cannot PASS C-34 while failing C-25 — inertia-gap attribution presupposes the per-expert attribute gate exists.
- **C-26 (named binding field contract)**: look for a dedicated block with entries labeled FC-N, [FC-1], FIELD CONTRACT entries, or equivalent — not schema field comments within a template. The distinguishing feature is that the block is standalone and referenceable by identifier from other steps. A template that annotates each YAML field with detailed constraints is C-12/C-17/C-18 territory; a separate contract block whose entries are cited by ID in gates is C-26 territory.
- **C-27 (pre-write count variable)**: look for a PREPARE sub-step (or equivalent pre-write phase) within the registry step that names a count variable explicitly. The variable name need not be "PHASE5_COUNT" specifically — any named intermediate (e.g., ROLE_COUNT, N_FILES) satisfies C-27 if it is (a) declared before WRITE begins and (b) the WRITE phase is explicitly told to use it. A gate that says "verify count matches Phase N output" after writing fails C-27 even if it satisfies C-22.
- **C-27 vs. C-32**: C-27 requires a named pre-write variable declared in a PREPARE sub-step. C-32 requires that PREPARE sub-step to also name the specific bypass anti-pattern it prevents. A variate can PASS C-27 (PREPARE sub-step with named variable) and FAIL C-32 (no bypass anti-pattern named — the mechanism exists but does not explain what failure it prevents). A variate cannot PASS C-32 while failing C-27.
- **C-28 (generic placeholder name prohibition)**: look for named anti-patterns in the instruction — e.g., "'domain-expert', 'expert-1', 'generic-expert' are prohibited." A positive requirement ("names must be domain-specific") is C-05 level. The distinguishing feature of C-28 is that the instruction names at least one specific placeholder pattern rather than relying on the positive requirement alone to prevent it.
- **C-28 vs. C-31**: C-28 requires the prohibition to appear at least once and name at least one anti-pattern. C-31 requires the same prohibition to appear at a minimum of three independent checkpoints: the contract block, the derivation step prose, and a gate condition. A variate can PASS C-28 (prohibition present in one location) and FAIL C-31 (only one location). A variate cannot PASS C-31 while failing C-28 — multi-site enforcement presupposes the prohibition exists.
- **C-28 vs. C-40**: C-28 closes a naming failure class by prohibiting specific placeholder patterns. C-40 opens a positive naming source by requiring expert names to derive from gap analysis vocabulary. They are complementary — C-28 is a fence, C-40 is a gate. A variate can PASS C-28 (fence in place) and FAIL C-40 (no positive gate). A variate cannot PASS C-40 while failing C-39 — the positive source presupposes the gap analysis artifact exists.
- **C-29 (contract violation framing)**: the key distinguishing word is "violation" or "breach" in the label — not just "type 1" or "type 2." Look for "CONTRACT VIOLATION (type 1)" or "VIOLATION TYPE 1" or "breach (type 1)" inside a named contract structure. "FAILURE MODE (type 1)" in a template comment is C-14 PASS and C-29 PARTIAL. The distinction matters because contract violation framing changes the model's categorical understanding of the constraint from "a thing that sometimes goes wrong" to "a thing that breaks the contract."
- **C-29 vs. C-33**: C-29 tests that the contract block carries CONTRACT VIOLATION framing. C-33 tests that the template at the collaborates_with write site also carries those labels, making the prohibition visible where the model is most likely to violate it. A variate can PASS C-29 (labels in contract block) and FAIL C-33 (labels not mirrored in template). PARTIAL C-33 means only one of the two labels is mirrored. A variate cannot PASS C-33 while failing C-29.
- **C-30 (extension purpose tied to diagnostic question)**: look for an explicit reference to a named diagnostic question in the purpose sub-field of the commitment block — the purpose must name what question it answers, not just what it stores. A purpose that says "records the inertia surface for downstream use" is content-descriptive (FAIL C-30). A purpose that says "answers Phase 0 diagnostic question: 'what status-quo claim will the inertia-advocate deploy?'" is diagnostic-linked (PASS C-30). The diagnostic question may be paraphrased but must be explicitly identified.
- **C-31 (multi-site enforcement)**: count the number of independent locations where the placeholder name prohibition and its anti-pattern examples appear — contract block counts as one, derivation step prose counts as one, derivation step gate counts as one, write step gate counts as one. Four locations is the strongest; three satisfies the criterion. Two locations or fewer fails C-31. Locations that are redundant within the same artifact section do not count separately.
- **C-32 (count bypass failure class)**: look for a sentence in or adjacent to the PREPARE sub-step that names the failure class it prevents — something equivalent to "deriving the count from a prior-phase plan rather than enumerating the actual output files is a failure." A PREPARE sub-step that says only "list all .md files written in Phase N, record the count as ROLE_COUNT" satisfies C-27 but fails C-32. The distinguishing feature of C-32 is that the instruction names the anti-pattern it was designed to prevent, not just the correct procedure.
- **C-33 (contract-to-template mirroring)**: look for the CONTRACT VIOLATION labels in two locations: the FC contract block (required by C-29) and the role template's collaborates_with field annotation. The template annotation need not be verbatim — "CONTRACT VIOLATION (type 1): phantom role reference" in the template alongside "[FC-10]: CONTRACT VIOLATION (type 1) — PHANTOM" in the contract block satisfies C-33. PARTIAL if only one label is mirrored. A contract block with both labels and a template with neither fails C-33 even when C-29 passes.
- **C-34 (inertia gap per expert)**: look for a fifth attribute in the derivation gate that names the expert's inertia gap, status-quo argument, or the resistance their domain would generate. The fifth attribute need not use the exact term "inertia gap" — "status-quo defense", "resistance claim", "conservative argument" all satisfy C-34 if the gate checks it per expert. A gate item that says "each expert has a named inertia gap" (checked per expert) satisfies C-34; a gate item that says "inertia-advocate has a status-quo question" (checked once for the role, not per expert) does not. The distinguishing feature is per-expert attribution of the inertia claim.
- **C-34 vs. C-39**: C-34 tests that the inertia gap appears as a gate-checked attribute per expert. C-39 tests whether that attribute derives from a separate upstream gap-enumeration artifact. A variate can PASS C-34 (inertia gap as fifth attribute derived inline) and FAIL C-39 (no INERTIA-GAP ANALYSIS sub-step). A variate cannot PASS C-39 while failing C-34 — the upstream artifact presupposes the attribute is gate-checked.
- **C-35 (pre-YAML per-role reasoning artifact)**: look for a named step that requires producing a structured per-role artifact before the WRITE phase begins. The key signals are: (a) the artifact has a defined format (not just a prose reflection), (b) the format includes a uniqueness argument field that requires naming the closest overlap role, (c) all artifacts must be completed before YAML writing begins. A step that says "think through each role's uniqueness before writing" without a structured format fails C-35. PARTIAL if the pre-YAML artifact exists but the uniqueness argument field is open-ended rather than requiring a named overlap role.
- **C-35 vs. C-36**: C-35 requires per-role pre-YAML artifacts. C-36 requires a cross-set scan gate operating on the completed artifact set. A variate can PASS C-35 (Diagnosis Cards required) and FAIL C-36 (no named cross-set scan step). A variate cannot PASS C-36 while failing C-35 — the cross-set scan presupposes the per-role artifacts exist.
- **C-35 vs. C-37**: C-35 requires the per-role artifact pipeline to exist with a uniqueness argument format field. C-37 requires the pipeline to be sequenced with the inertia-advocate card as the first-authored anchor and non-anchor cards to carry an orthogonality field referencing it. PASS C-35 with FAIL C-37 is the common case — the cards exist but are not ordered with an anchor. PARTIAL C-37 covers the ordering-without-orthogonality-field case.
- **C-35 vs. C-41**: C-35 requires the Diagnosis Card format to include a uniqueness argument field. C-41 requires the same format to also include an inertia-gap provenance field referencing the gap analysis artifact. A variate can PASS C-35 and FAIL C-41 (cards have uniqueness argument but no provenance field). A variate cannot PASS C-41 while failing C-35 or C-39.
- **C-36 (cross-set pre-YAML uniqueness scan)**: look for a named gate or step between the "all Diagnosis Cards complete" milestone and the "begin YAML writing" milestone. The scan must be a discrete named artifact or gate item — not just a template annotation in the YAML step. The key signal is sequential positioning: after per-role artifact completion, before YAML writing begins. PARTIAL if a cross-set check exists but is positioned inside the YAML-writing step rather than as a preceding gate.
- **C-36 vs. C-38**: C-36 tests that the cross-set uniqueness scan gate exists and is positioned before YAML writing. C-38 tests that the scan is structured as a named multi-step procedure with the anchor card as explicit reference axis. A variate can PASS C-36 (scan gate present and correctly positioned) and FAIL C-38 (scan is a single-step check without anchor-phase decomposition). PARTIAL C-38 covers scans that reference the anchor as a point but do not separate anchor-orthogonality from non-anchor pairwise phases.
- **C-37 (anchor-card ordering)**: look for two distinct signals — the ordering designation (anchor card written first, explicitly named as ANCHOR-CARD or equivalent) and the per-card field (each subsequent card requires a field whose value states orthogonality to the anchor card's verify question). The ordering alone is PARTIAL; both together is PASS. Do not confuse with C-02 (devil-advocate present) — C-37 is about the artifact pipeline's sequencing and the per-card field requirement, not the role's functional identity.
- **C-38 (anchor-referenced structured uniqueness scan)**: count the named phases in the scan procedure. A scan that says "check each card against the anchor, then check all pairs" without named phase separators is PARTIAL (anchor-referenced but not structurally decomposed). A scan with labeled Phase 1 / Phase 2 / Phase 3 or Step 1 / Step 2 / Step 3 structure is a PASS candidate. The key test is whether the anchor-orthogonality phase is explicitly separated from the non-anchor pairwise phase — combined or undifferentiated checking fails C-38 even if the anchor is mentioned.
- **C-38 vs. C-42**: C-38 tests that the scan has at least three named phases including an enumeration phase. C-42 tests that the enumeration phase is a standalone named phase never merged with the anchor-orthogonality phase. R9 variates PARTIAL C-38 because "Phase 1 — Anchor-Orthogonality Check" combines enumeration and checking under one label — failing C-38 PASS condition and failing C-42. A variate can PARTIAL C-38 (two labeled phases with enumeration embedded) and FAIL C-42. A variate cannot PASS C-42 while failing C-38 — phase separation presupposes the structured multi-phase scan exists.
- **C-38 vs. C-43**: C-38 requires a revision step; C-43 requires that step to be a separately labeled named phase. A variate can PARTIAL C-38 (revision present but embedded) and FAIL C-43 (no phase label on revision). Achieving C-42 PASS (standalone enumeration phase) plus C-43 PASS (named revision phase) is sufficient to also achieve C-38 PASS, because the four-phase scan (enumerate, anchor-check, pairwise-check, revise) satisfies C-38's three-named-phase condition by construction.
- **C-39 (pre-expert inertia-gap analysis artifact)**: the ordering is critical — the gap-enumeration sub-step must precede expert naming, not follow it. A step that names experts first and then validates their inertia claims against the context fails C-39. Look for: (a) a named sub-step (INERTIA-GAP ANALYSIS, GAP IDENTIFICATION, or equivalent), (b) a structured artifact listing Gap Domains and their inertia resistance, (c) explicit statement that this step precedes expert naming, and (d) a reference from the expert derivation step to this artifact as its inertia-gap source.
- **C-39 vs. C-40**: C-39 requires the gap analysis artifact to exist and precede expert naming. C-40 requires expert names to positively derive from that artifact's vocabulary. A variate can PASS C-39 (artifact exists, precedes expert naming) and FAIL C-40 (expert names do not reference gap vocabulary — just happen to be specific). A variate cannot PASS C-40 while failing C-39 — positive sourcing requires the source artifact.
- **C-40 (gap-domain vocabulary as expert naming source)**: the test is whether the naming constraint is a positive sourcing requirement (must come from Gap Domain vocabulary) versus a negative prohibition (must not be 'domain-expert'). Look for language like "expert name must reflect / derive from / be drawn from the Gap Domain label in INERTIA-GAP ANALYSIS." A constraint that says "names must be domain-specific" without naming the gap analysis artifact as the source fails C-40 (C-05 level) even if C-28's placeholder prohibition is also satisfied.
- **C-40 vs. C-45**: C-40 tests that the positive naming constraint exists at the gate or step level. C-45 tests that the constraint is additionally embedded as a required format field within every individual expert derivation record — making sourcing verifiable per-expert at the record level rather than only at the gate. A variate can PASS C-40 (gate-level positive constraint) and FAIL C-45 (no per-expert inline sourcing field in derivation records). A variate cannot PASS C-45 while failing C-40.
- **C-41 (inertia-gap provenance field in Diagnosis Cards)**: look for a named field in the Diagnosis Card format template — not just a prose instruction — whose required value is a reference or pointer to a named entry in the gap analysis artifact. The field name matters: "inertia gap inherited from [Gap Domain N]" is the canonical form, but "inertia source: Gap N" or equivalent provenance notation also satisfies C-41. The key is that completing the field requires consulting the gap analysis artifact, not free-forming an inertia claim. A card format that has an inertia_gap field but no provenance requirement (the model fills it from scratch) fails C-41.
- **C-41 vs. C-44**: C-41 tests that the Diagnosis Card provenance field references a named gap entry. C-44 tests that the reference uses a stable formal slug identifier (GAP-{slug}) rather than a positional label (Gap 1, Gap N). A variate can PASS C-41 (provenance field references "Gap 2 inertia resistance...") and FAIL C-44 (positional reference, no slug). A variate cannot PASS C-44 while failing C-41 — slug-based provenance presupposes the provenance field exists.
- **C-41 vs. C-47**: C-41 tests the Diagnosis Card's inertia-gap provenance field. C-47 tests whether the same inheritance claim is persisted into the YAML role file's `inertia_gap_inherited` field, completing the three-artifact chain. A variate can PASS C-41 (provenance in Diagnosis Cards) and FAIL C-47 (not carried through to YAML). A variate cannot PASS C-47 while failing C-41.
- **C-42 (enumeration-anchor phase separation)**: the key diagnostic is whether the phase header that introduces the enumeration list is the same header that introduces the first orthogonality check. In R9 V-01/V-04/V-05, "Phase 1 — Anchor-Orthogonality Check" begins by listing verify questions and then checks each against the anchor — two actions under one label. C-42 PASS requires a phase dedicated entirely to listing (no checking begins until the next labeled phase). PARTIAL if two numbered sub-steps appear within a single phase header — the structure is present but the label separation is absent.
- **C-43 (named revision phase in scan)**: look for a phase header (Phase 3, Phase 4, Step 4, or equivalent) dedicated to resolving flagged cards after the pairwise check completes. The distinguishing test: is there a named phase the model enters *after* pairwise checking *and before* YAML writing? A trailing sentence "record resolution before proceeding" fails C-43. A labeled "Phase 3 — Resolve" or "Step 4 — Revise Flagged Cards" before the YAML WRITE phase passes. PARTIAL if revision has an inline label ("Resolution:") but no top-level phase header.
- **C-44 (GAP-{slug} formal identifier scheme)**: look for non-positional keys — identifiers that name the gap domain (e.g., GAP-SCHEMA-DRIFT, GAP-AUTH-BOUNDARY) rather than numbering it (Gap 1, Gap 2). The slug must appear in the gap artifact and be reused by downstream records (POSITIVE SOURCING statements, Diagnosis Card provenance fields) — cross-artifact reference by stable key, not by position. PARTIAL if the gap artifact uses slugs but downstream records revert to positional references.
- **C-44 vs. C-45**: C-44 tests that GAP-{slug} identifiers exist in the gap artifact and are cited in downstream references. C-45 tests that every expert derivation record includes an explicit per-expert sourcing field citing a GAP-{slug} identifier. A variate can PASS C-44 (slugs used in the gap artifact and referenced somewhere) and FAIL C-45 (no per-expert inline sourcing format field in derivation records). A variate cannot PASS C-45 while failing C-44 — per-expert inline sourcing that cites GAP-{slug} presupposes the slug scheme exists.
- **C-45 (per-expert inline POSITIVE SOURCING record)**: look for a named format field within each expert derivation record (not a gate item, not a step-level instruction) whose required value is a POSITIVE SOURCING statement — "this name derives from GAP-{slug} Domain vocabulary: [specific term used]" or equivalent. The distinguishing feature: the sourcing claim lives in the record, not in the gate that checks the record. A gate item "expert names must derive from GAP vocabulary" satisfies C-40; a format field "POSITIVE SOURCING: ..." inside each expert derivation block satisfies C-45.
- **C-46 (YAML-level orthogonality field persistence)**: look for `orthogonality` (or equivalent) in the YAML role file format template for non-anchor domain expert roles. The field must appear in the YAML template definition — not just in the Diagnosis Card format. Verify that the skill instruction references the Diagnosis Card's orthogonality field as the source for the YAML field. A YAML file that happens to include an orthogonality comment satisfies C-46 only if the skill instruction requires it. A variate that records orthogonality only in Diagnosis Cards (C-37 PASS) and does not carry it through to YAML fails C-46.
- **C-46 vs. C-47**: C-46 and C-47 test independent YAML field persistence — orthogonality-to-anchor (from C-37 mechanism) and inertia-gap-inherited (from C-41 mechanism). A variate can PASS one and FAIL the other. They may share a single YAML template section but test different fields and different upstream sources. Evaluate each independently against its respective prerequisite criterion.
- **C-47 (YAML-level inertia-gap-inherited field persistence)**: look for `inertia_gap_inherited` (or equivalent) in the YAML role file format template for domain expert roles. The field's required value must trace to the Diagnosis Card's inertia-gap provenance field (C-41), which in turn traces to the gap analysis artifact (C-39). A YAML field that records the inertia gap without citing its provenance chain (or that is populated from scratch rather than from the Diagnosis Card field) fails C-47. The three-artifact chain (gap artifact -> Diagnosis Card provenance field -> YAML inertia_gap_inherited field) must be explicitly stated in the skill instruction.
- **C-48 (canonical 4-phase scan ordering)**: look for all four phases named as top-level headers in canonical sequence. The most common failure mode is "Phase N -- Pairwise Check and Resolution" -- pairwise and revision sharing a label -- which produces C-48 FAIL even when C-43 FAIL would have caught it first. The second failure mode is enumeration appearing mid-scan (after the anchor-ortho phase) -- C-42 catches this first, but C-48 also FAILs. PARTIAL covers the case where three phases are in canonical order but one is missing or out of position; this is only possible in edge cases since C-42 FAIL and C-43 FAIL would also trigger. Evaluate C-42 and C-43 first; if either fails, C-48 inherits the failure. A variate cannot PASS C-48 while failing C-42 or C-43.
- **C-48 vs C-42**: C-42 tests that the enumeration phase is standalone (not merged with anchor-ortho). C-48 tests the full ordering. A variate can PASS C-42 (enumeration standalone, in first position) and FAIL C-48 (pairwise and revision share a phase label -- e.g., V-01 scenario where Phase 2 is pairwise with trailing revision). A variate cannot PASS C-48 while failing C-42.
- **C-48 vs C-43**: C-43 tests that revision has its own labeled phase. C-48 tests it is in the correct position (after pairwise). A variate can PASS C-43 (revision has a phase label) and FAIL C-48 (revision appears before pairwise). A variate cannot PASS C-48 while failing C-43.
- **C-48 vs C-38**: C-38 tests that the scan has at least three named phases with anchor-ortho separated from pairwise. C-48 tests that all four phases are present and ordered canonically. A variate can PASS C-38 (three phases, anchor-ortho separate from pairwise) and FAIL C-48 (revision merged with pairwise or enumeration out of position). A variate cannot PASS C-48 while failing C-38.
- **C-49 (per-file checklist criterion cross-referencing)**: look for parenthetical or bracketed criterion IDs adjacent to individual checklist items -- "[C-29]", "satisfies C-33", "enforces C-29/C-33", or equivalent. The PARTIAL condition covers checklists that name criteria in a preamble or summary section rather than per-item. The distinguishing feature of PASS: evaluating any single checklist item in isolation should reveal which criterion it enforces without consulting other sections. A checklist that carries two such annotated items PASSES even if other items lack criterion annotations. The criterion IDs need not be verbatim C-NN format -- "CONTRACT VIOLATION (type 1) [C-29]" and equivalent mappings satisfy C-49 if the criterion number appears.
- **C-49 vs C-29**: C-29 tests that the contract block carries CONTRACT VIOLATION framing. C-49 tests that the per-file checklist items carry criterion-ID annotations. A variate can PASS C-29 (labels in contract block) and FAIL C-49 (no criterion IDs in per-file checklist items). A variate cannot PASS C-49 while failing C-29.
- **C-49 vs C-33**: C-33 tests that CONTRACT VIOLATION labels are mirrored in the template annotation. C-49 tests that individual per-file checklist items also name the criterion IDs they enforce. A variate can PASS C-33 (labels mirrored in template) and FAIL C-49 (per-file checklist items cite labels but not criterion IDs). A variate cannot PASS C-49 while failing C-33.
