### Structural notes

**C-54** is the first criterion to define a mandatory cross-axis bridge between the two independent source-quality layers. C-51 made EXPIRED and ASSUMED *co-mandatory* as Step 8 targets; C-54 makes them *interconnected* — EXPIRED entries that cannot be refreshed must collapse into ASSUMED, cascading into the full amendment-confirmation chain (C-23, C-49). The forward-reference directive is the instruction-layer obligation that activates this collapse.

**C-55** introduces the first meta-diagnostic layer in the rubric. Prior audit steps (Step 7, Step 8b, Step 9b under C-52) identify *individual* failures; C-55 requires Step 9b to additionally classify failure *patterns* — column-pattern (one step failed for all stakeholders) vs. row-pattern (one stakeholder fell through multiple steps) — with causally distinct remediation prescriptions. This is the first criterion that interprets audit *structure* rather than just audit *cells*.

**C-56** completes the three-layer pre-Phase-1 competitor preparation structure: C-50 (market classification: Adoption + Switching Cost Bands) → C-53 (intent classification: Response Track) → C-56 (obligation instantiation: Engagement Objective + **Success Criterion** + Downstream Message Requirement per competitor). C-56 is the first criterion in the rubric to require a **Success Criterion** column — an observable measurability gate on a strategic intent declaration.
