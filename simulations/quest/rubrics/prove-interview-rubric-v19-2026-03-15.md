Three new patterns visible in the Round 20 differential:

- **V-02 on C-49**: gate-block-first items include an inline rationale *why* they are first ("this condition is the first gate item because...") — C-49 tests ordering; nothing tests the ordering being explained at the point of placement

# prove-interview Rubric — v19

`(E/5 × 60) + (R/3 × 30) + (A/49 × 10)` | PARTIAL = 0.5

**v19 changes — three new criteria extracted from Round 20 differential (C-49/C-50/C-51 axis):**

| ID | Pattern | Source | Pass Condition |
|----|---------|--------|----------------|
| **C-52** | Gate-item ordering rationale declared at item | V-02 C-49 excellence | Each chain-critical first item in a gate block includes an inline rationale statement naming *why* that item is first — not just position but explanation at point of placement |
| **C-53** | Propagation obligations phase-differentiated with distinct per-phase constraint | V-03/V-04/V-05 C-50 excellence | Each downstream phase named in a propagation obligations block receives a distinct, phase-specific constraint — not a blanket obligation uniformly applied to all phases |
| **C-54** | Contract closure bidirectional — receiving gate cites source and adds anti-parallel-contract item | V-05 C-51 excellence | The receiving gate explicitly cites the source contract gate by phase name AND includes its own anti-parallel-contract gate item, closing the chain in both directions |

**Denominator: 46 → 49.** Formula: `(E/5 × 60) + (R/3 × 30) + (A/49 × 10)`.

C-52 extends C-49 (which tests chain-critical ordering) by testing whether the ordering decision is explained at the gate item itself — making the chain-critical designation auditable from the text rather than inferrable from position alone. C-53 extends C-50 (which tests that propagation obligations exist at source) by testing that each downstream phase receives a phase-specific constraint rather than a uniform obligation: differentiation makes the chain enforcement mechanism auditable at each phase transition. C-54 is the receiving-side companion to C-51 (which tests source-side declaration): the contract chain is closed bidirectionally when the receiving gate names the source authority and adds its own enforcement, rather than only the source gate declaring itself.
