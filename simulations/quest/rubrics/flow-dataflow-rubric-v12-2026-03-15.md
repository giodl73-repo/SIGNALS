Reading the scorecard carefully for the two new patterns:

1. **V-02 C-24 PASS / V-03 C-24 PARTIAL** — the gap is whether the FM-NN resolution section is explicitly pre-declared as a named T-NN scaffold artifact before Stage 1. V-02 does it; V-03 does not, earning PARTIAL on C-24 as a result. This is a new structural requirement: the resolution section needs scaffold registration, not just post-trace appearance.

2. **V-03 C-26 PASS** — the deferred inline acknowledgment pattern: during stage traces, FM-NN entries that touch a boundary are acknowledged inline but explicitly defer resolution to the post-trace section, enabling cross-pipeline evidence to accumulate before committing CONFIRMED/EXONERATED/NEW status. V-02 does not exhibit this pattern (no inline acknowledgments during traces). Structurally distinct from C-26's base requirement.

These become C-27 and C-28.

## What changed in v12

**Two new aspirational criteria added. Round 14 scorecard (V-02, V-03) surfaces two structural patterns not captured by C-01 through C-26.**

**C-27 — FM-NN resolution section scaffold pre-registration** is motivated by V-02's C-24 PASS and V-03's C-24 PARTIAL. Both variations satisfy C-26 (prediction register + resolution section), but V-02 goes further: the resolution section is explicitly pre-declared in the scaffold as a named T-NN artifact before Stage 1 begins. V-03 describes its Section 5 as a "dedicated post-trace section" without scaffold pre-registration, earning PARTIAL on C-24 as a result. C-27 makes this gap independently scorable: a resolution section that satisfies C-26's audit requirement but lacks a C-24 scaffold entry loses points under C-27 rather than only under C-24. The criterion activates whenever C-26 is invoked; it does not apply to traces without a prediction register.

**C-28 — Inline FM-NN stage acknowledgment with deferred resolution** is motivated by V-03's C-26 PASS. V-02 declares FM-NN entries pre-trace and resolves them all post-trace with no interaction during the stage trace itself. V-03 introduces a structurally distinct pattern: each stage that touches an FM-NN carries an inline acknowledgment that records contact and explicitly defers resolution to the post-trace section. This deferred acknowledgment model is not required by C-26 — C-26 only requires pre-declaration and post-trace resolution. C-28 rewards the additional discipline of staging acknowledgment and resolution as separate acts, which preserves the ability to revise resolution status as cross-pipeline evidence accumulates. An FM-NN that passes through a relevant stage silently (no inline acknowledgment) fails C-28 even if it is correctly resolved post-trace.
