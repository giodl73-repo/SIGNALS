**A-28 — Sensitivity analysis states +1 and −1 swing directions as distinct figures** (depth, 5 pts)

Source: V-02 Round 16 A-20 differentiator. V-02 states "if shifted to [current + 1]... if shifted to [current − 1]" as two separate directional figures per high-influence persona. V-01 used "current ±1" notation, which collapses both directions into a single value and earned only PARTIAL on A-20. The distinction matters because a +1 shift and a −1 shift have asymmetric effects on aggregate NPS when the mean sits near the 7.0 threshold — a Detractor shifting to Passive changes band distribution and threshold status; a Passive shifting to Promoter does not. Collapsing ±1 into a single delta hides this asymmetry and can mask a situation where the spec survives a best-case swing but fails under a worst-case one. A-20 requires that numeric swing figures be present; A-28 requires that both directions be stated separately. A sensitivity analysis that shows "delta = ±0.4" passes A-20 but fails A-28; one that shows "+1 → 7.8, −1 → 7.0" passes both. Pass condition: for each high-influence persona identified in the sensitivity analysis, both the +1 aggregate impact and the −1 aggregate impact are stated as distinct numeric figures; a combined ±X notation that presents a single symmetric value does not pass A-28.
