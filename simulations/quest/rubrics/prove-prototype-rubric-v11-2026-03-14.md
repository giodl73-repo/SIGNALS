# Rubric: prove-prototype (v11)

**Updated**: 2026-03-15
**Changes from v10**: Added C-33 and C-34 (Aspirational tier) derived from Round 10 excellence signals.

**C-33** — *CALIBRATE container header enumerates all three pre-build responsibilities as an ordered entry contract* (V-03 gap signal): V-03 demonstrated a numbered CALIBRATE header that announces all three pre-build responsibilities upfront — "1. Measure inertia competitor baseline; 2. Commit B-00; 3. State outperform threshold" — before the container body executes them. The Round 10 scorecard note explicitly identified that no current criteria score this pattern: "criteria are evaluated on structural markers and completion-line content — neither of which V-03 alters." The header enumeration creates an entry contract paired with C-32's exit confirmation. Together they bracket the CALIBRATE container: entry announces what will be discharged, body executes it, completion line confirms it was discharged with values. When C-33 and C-32 are both satisfied, a reader scanning only container boundaries (opening enumeration + closing COMPLETE line) can verify the full CALIBRATE contract without reading the container body. C-32 captures the exit; C-33 captures the entry; neither satisfies the other. C-33 requires C-28 and C-32 as prerequisites.

**C-34** — *Baseline column in results table labeled as inertia competitor, creating a third structural surface for competitor identification* (V-05 ceiling signal): V-05's three-column results table labeled the baseline column "Baseline (inertia competitor)" rather than merely "Baseline" or "Control." C-30 requires a baseline column but does not require it to identify the competitor by name — a column labeled only "Baseline" passes C-30 but fails C-34. Labeling the column as the inertia competitor creates a third independent structural surface for competitor identification: C-29 places the competitor in the CALIBRATE container body; C-32 confirms it in the CALIBRATE COMPLETE line; C-34 requires it to appear as a column label in the results table. The three surfaces span the full experimental lifecycle — container-framing level, completion-line level, and measurement-table level — and are mutually independent: each can be verified by scanning a different structural element. C-34 requires C-30 and C-29 as prerequisites.

**Point totals**: Aspirational 132 → 146. Total 232 → 246.

## Essential Criteria (60 points)

| ID | Criterion | Category | Weight | Pass Condition |
|----|-----------|----------|--------|----------------|
| C-01 | **Hypothesis stated** -- A testable claim is stated before any build description | correctness | essential | A testable claim appears before any construction or measurement activity; the claim names the behavior or property being tested, not merely a goal or feature |
| C-02 | **Scope bounded** -- The prototype is explicitly constrained; the output names what was deliberately excluded | correctness | essential | The scope is stated; at least one deliberate exclusion is named with a reason showing the exclusion leaves the hypothesis testable; an output that describes only what was built without bounding what was left out is not valid without it |
| C-03 | **Measurement defined before building** -- Output shows that success and failure criteria were established before construction, not retrofitted after results | correctness | essential | A "what to measure" section appears logically before the results section; success and failure conditions are stated explicitly |
| C-04 | **Actual vs. predicted reported** -- Output explicitly compares what was predicted to happen with what actually happened | correctness | essential | Both a prediction and an observation are present; the delta (match or mismatch) is called out directly |
| C-05 | **Hypothesis verdict rendered** -- Output states whether the hypothesis is confirmed, refuted, or inconclusive based on the measurements | correctness | essential | A verdict is stated in plain language; it follows from the evidence rather than being asserted without support |
