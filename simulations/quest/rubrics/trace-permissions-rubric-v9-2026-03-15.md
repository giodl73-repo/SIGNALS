## Summary of v9 changes

**Two new aspirational criteria** extracted from Round 8 pass/fail contrasts. Aspirational tier grows from N=23 to N=25 (weight per criterion ~0.400).

| ID | Name | Source |
|----|------|--------|
| C-31 | Verbatim-quote format in cross-audit assertion | R8 V-01 C-29 PASS: `Pre-decl: "[exact text]" | Filed: "[exact text]" | Match: CONFIRMED` — quotes both sites exactly rather than characterizing them |
| C-32 | Intent-anchored equivalence clause in prohibition instructions | R8 V-02 C-30 PASS: equivalence clause names the prohibited mechanism of effect ("that co-mingles content from another structural site", "that delegates mechanism content to another section") in addition to structural form categories |

**Discriminating logic:**
- C-31 separates cross-audits that assert a match conclusion from cross-audits that present verbatim evidence for the match. A characterization-based assertion (`Pre-decl identifies FLS denial; Column cites FLS denial; confirmed`) still requires reader judgment about whether the summaries are co-referential. Verbatim quotation makes the comparison a zero-judgment string operation.
- C-32 separates equivalence clauses that generalize by example-resemblance from equivalence clauses that generalize by effect-membership. Without a named prohibited effect, a novel form producing the prohibited effect may be judged non-equivalent because it does not resemble the named examples. With a named effect, inclusion/exclusion is determined by effect, not appearance.

**Suggested v10 criterion:** inertia anchor falsification condition — when C-22 is satisfied, each design assumption statement includes an explicit falsification condition naming the evidence that would flip the verdict to POSSIBLE. Separates assumptions that state the expected posture from assumptions that define the failure boundary and can be refuted by evidence.
