**Summary of the rubric:**

**5 Essential (60%)** — all must pass:
- **C-01** Areas of work named and grounded in scan evidence (not invented)
- **C-02** Multi-source scan — evidence from 3+ of the 7 listed source types
- **C-03** Headcount signals estimated with supporting rationale
- **C-04** Cross-cutting concerns surfaced with a boundary-spanning note
- **C-05** Output is raw analysis — no org chart, no reporting lines

**3 Recommended (30%)** — output is better with these:
- **C-06** Team boundary candidates proposed with seam rationale
- **C-07** Recommended org shape named and justified from scan findings
- **C-08** Evidence gaps and ambiguities flagged explicitly

**2 Aspirational (10%)** — raise the bar:
- **C-09** 5+ specific file paths cited as auditable evidence
- **C-10** Current state vs recommended state clearly separated

Golden threshold: all 5 essential pass + composite >= 80.

The key design decision: C-05 (not an org chart) is essential because `org-scan` and `org-chart` are distinct skills — a scan that skips straight to an org chart is structurally broken regardless of how good the content is.
