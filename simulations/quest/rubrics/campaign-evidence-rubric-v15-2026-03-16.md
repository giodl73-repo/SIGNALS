**What changed in v15**

Two new aspirational criteria. Aspirational denominator: 29 → 31.

**C-37** closes the structural anachronism gap that C-35 leaves open. Dual-phase checkpoints can be satisfied via inline zone markers — labels embedded in a single stage narrative block that are fully legible but offer no structural resistance to retroactive filling. Implementing the PRE and POST checkpoints as separately-named table artifacts with execution content physically between them changes the compliance posture: the executor must produce execution output in the space between two tables that have not yet been filled, making simultaneous filling of both tables a structurally visible anachronism rather than an honor-system commitment. The temporal constraint is a property of the document structure itself, not of the labeling convention.

**C-38** closes the pre-instantiation gap that C-27 leaves at the invocation level. C-27 requires the gate record be pre-templated as blank cells in the preamble; the same principle applied to the full invocation apparatus — all PRE and POST forms pre-declared before Stage 1 — achieves two independent structural effects: any unfilled invocation cell is visible before execution begins (not only detectable at audit), and per-stage timing directives eliminate the act of remembering which form to fill and when. The combination of preamble templates plus stage-body directives converts the full invocation apparatus from a creative memory task into a mechanical cell-filling task. Both components are required: templates alone preserve the memory requirement (which form applies here?); directives alone require the forms to be created at fill-time, preserving the creative-act gap that C-27 was designed to close.
