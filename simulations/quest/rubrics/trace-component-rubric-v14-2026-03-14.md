**Three new criteria promoted from R13:**

| # | Name | Source | Mechanism |
|---|------|--------|-----------|
| **C-43** | Mutual-Constraint Entanglement Declaration | V-03 + V-05 "Neither satisfied without the other" | Declares the two constraints are mutually inseparable — forecloses partial-satisfaction claims that C-40's co-location alone leaves open |
| **C-44** | Numbered Constraint Enumeration in Placeholder | V-03 + V-05 `(1) content — ...; (2) position — ...` | Gives each constraint a discrete independently-checkable address — compound phrasing allows conflation; numbered items prohibit it |
| **C-45** | Dual-Requirement Count Declaration in Placeholder | V-03 "Two requirements, one instruction" / V-05 "Two simultaneous constraints — one instruction" | Leading N=2 opener establishes the obligation count before constraints are enumerated — without it, two constraints can be read as one compound requirement |

**Ceiling: 158 → 164 pts** (37 aspirational × 2 + 90 essential/recommended)

**Orthogonality:** All three are independent. C-40 (co-location) can be satisfied without any of C-43/C-44/C-45. Each of the three can be satisfied without the other two. V-01, V-02, V-04 satisfy C-40 but carry none of the three; V-03 and V-05 are the sole high-water exemplars carrying all three.
