**What the two new criteria capture:**

- **C-28** distinguishes C-27's system-level definitional claim from per-loop localization of that same logic. C-27 fires once at the charter closure: "declaring format laws without an application layer is not enforcement." C-28 requires that reasoning to reappear at each loop's own section — "Without this step, Loop 2 has no artifacts to audit; without Loop 3, Loop 2's output is uncheckable." The difference is scope: C-27 is the global categorical claim; C-28 distributes that claim to every structural unit it governs. V-05's inertia framing realizes C-28 by design: each section's "Without this step..." line is a micro-definitional claim independent of the charter closure.

- **C-29** distinguishes C-27's presence from register dominance. A document passes C-27 by including a single well-formed definitional closure statement. C-29 requires the definitional voice to be the document's primary explanatory register — the mode in which loop relationships, MoSCoW levels, and design contract obligations are naturally expressed throughout.
