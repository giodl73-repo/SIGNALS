**Two new criteria added for R9:**

**C-29 — Phase-Gate Entry-Point Restatement** (sharpens C-27, from V-05 R8 dual-anchor signal)

C-27 requires the schema gate to be coupled to a named phase-advance condition — but that coupling can be stated once at the column definition and then left behind. V-05 goes further: the gate condition is restated at the entry of Layer 4 itself ("Only after both Layer 3 gate conditions are satisfied..."). This creates a dual-anchor: the model encounters the constraint at schema setup *and* again at the moment of transition. Pass requires both anchors — gate at schema definition and gate at phase entry. A single coupling statement (C-27) does not pass.

**C-30 — Inversion Trigger Causal Specificity** (sharpens C-28, from V-04 R8 causal-precision signal)

C-28 inverts the revision impulse generally: "if you want to revise, that confirms the obligation." V-04 names the specific cause: "if editing the Rationale *would make the comparison sentence completable*, that desire confirms the obligation." The trigger is no longer any revision impulse — it is specifically the inability to complete the comparison sentence. Pass requires the inversion clause to name comparison-sentence incompletability as the causal mechanism. A general inversion (C-28) does not pass.

**Key structural move in v9:** C-29 extends the peer-comparison marking lineage to nine levels deep; C-30 extends the rationalization branch to four levels (C-22 → C-24 → C-28 → C-30). Max raised from 145 to **150**. V-04's causal specificity is the first known formulation to satisfy C-30 (projected 147.5); no R8 variation achieves 150 — that ceiling is open for R9.
