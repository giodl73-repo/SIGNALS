**Design decisions:**

**4 essentials** (not 5) — the fifth candidate (author's voice) is real but too subjective to be a hard gate. Voice failures are caught by C-01 (which bans enumeration) and C-08 (aspirational narrative arc).

**The hard essential is C-04** — cross-signal pattern. This is the core value proposition of `topic-story`. Any output that just lists "scout said X, draft said Y, prove said Z" fails here even if the structure (C-02) is present. Evaluators should apply the test: *could this claim appear in a single signal?*

**C-03 + C-07 split the recommendation** — existence vs. proportionality. This lets you fail a recommendation that exists but contradicts the evidence, without conflating it with the "recommendation is missing" failure.

**C-09 (delta surfacing)** is aspirational because many stories won't have surprises — when the signals are strongly convergent, there may be no "we expected X but found Y." It raises the bar for stories where surprises exist.
