**Cascade logic:**

- **C-50** closes the phrasing-drift risk for C-47 symmetrically with how C-49 closed the notation-lock-in risk for C-46. R6 confirmed that V-04 ("co-equal self-declaration peers in a local-verifiability contract") and V-05 ("three-node self-declaration system... co-equal... locally visible") both satisfy C-47 with distinct phrasings. Without C-50, an implementation using V-04's canonical phrase verbatim leaves evaluators without guidance that V-05's phrasing is equally valid — the same drift that produced C-46's notation problem before C-49 was added.

- **C-51** closes the unnamed-form ambiguity gap that C-49 leaves open. C-49 correctly declares "any notation satisfying the independence property passes" but without naming Form A (bracket notation) and Form B (isolation comments) as labeled classes, evaluators must apply the full independence test to determine class membership rather than matching by label. Named enumeration converts the abstract claim into a testable standard, parallel to what C-50 does for C-47 phrasings.
