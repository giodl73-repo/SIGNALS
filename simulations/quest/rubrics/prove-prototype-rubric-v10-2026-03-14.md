# Rubric: prove-prototype (v10)

**Updated**: 2026-03-15
**Changes from v9**: Added C-31 and C-32 (Aspirational tier) derived from Round 9 excellence signals.

**C-31** — *FRAMER role explicitly prohibited from inertia competitor identification* (V-01/V-03 signal): V-01 and V-03 both carried an explicit prohibition in the FRAMER role header stating that inertia competitor identification must NOT appear in DESIGN — it belongs exclusively in CALIBRATE. V-03 showed the full form: FRAMER prohibition + CALIBRATE container owns all three inertia elements + CALIBRATE COMPLETE confirms the triple. The prohibition creates a role-label scan surface independent of the container scan: a container scan confirms competitor content is in CALIBRATE (C-28, C-29); a role-label scan confirms FRAMER did not write it (C-31). Without the prohibition, a FRAMER that writes inertia framing in DESIGN produces a C-29 failure detectable only by reading DESIGN body text, not by scanning role headers. C-31 requires C-23 and C-29.

**C-32** — *CALIBRATE COMPLETE line confirms the inertia triple* (V-03 signal): V-03's CALIBRATE COMPLETE explicitly confirmed: "inertia competitor identified, B-00 committed, outperform threshold stated." A CALIBRATE COMPLETE that confirms only measurement isolation (C-28) or only competitor naming (C-29) but not all three does not pass. Triple confirmation upgrades CALIBRATE completion from a single-element gate to a structured audit record: a reader scanning only the completion line can verify CALIBRATE fully discharged all three pre-build responsibilities without reading the container body. C-32 requires C-28 and C-29. Extends C-22 within CALIBRATE.

**Point totals**: Aspirational 118 → 132. Total 218 → 232.

**What the two new criteria add:**

C-31 closes the enforcement gap C-29 leaves open: C-29 requires competitor content in CALIBRATE, but without a FRAMER prohibition any future run can place that content in DESIGN and only body-text reading reveals the violation. C-31 makes the violation detectable by role-header scan alone — orthogonal to the container scan C-29 relies on.

C-32 closes the audit gap C-28+C-29 leave open: both require content to be present in the container body, but neither requires the completion line to confirm it. C-32 forces the triple into the one line a scan-only auditor reads, making CALIBRATE's full discharge verifiable without entering the container.

The ceiling moves from 218 to 232. The new ceiling variation for V-01/V-03 form (passes C-31) is 211 when C-30 fails; the V-02 form (passes C-30, C-12) reaches 210 when C-29 and C-31 fail. Neither R9 variation captures both C-31 and C-32 simultaneously — that remains the R10 seed.
