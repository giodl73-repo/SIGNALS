**9 criteria across 3 tiers:**

**Essential (4)** — the skill fails without all of these:
- **C-01 Surprise Orientation** — output contains only unexpected findings, nothing that could have been written before the signals existed
- **C-02 Source Signal Tracing** — every surprise names a specific signal artifact by slug or file ref
- **C-03 Design Impact Assessment** — every surprise states what changes in the design as a result
- **C-04 Named and Countable Surprises** — at least 2 discrete titled items; a single observation is not a synthesis

**Recommended (3)** — output is better with these:
- **C-05 Surprise Diversity** — surprises span different namespaces or concern types; no two collapse into one
- **C-06 Future-Team Framing** — written for the next team, not as personal reflection; durable language
- **C-07 Impact Specificity** — impact clauses name specific artifacts, decisions, or behaviors; not vague

**Aspirational (2)** — raises the bar once the rest is stable:
- **C-08 Cross-Signal Synthesis** — at least one surprise requires 2+ signals to be visible; emergent insight
- **C-09 Counterfactual Awareness** — at least one "without this, we would have..." clause naming a specific wrong turn avoided

The core rubric design tension: `topic-echo` is not a summary skill — it's an inversion skill. C-01 enforces that inversion at the pass/fail gate.
