## Essential Criteria (60 points total)

| ID | Criterion | Category | Weight | Pass Condition |
|----|-----------|----------|--------|----------------|
| C-01 | **Persona identity declared** | correctness | essential | Each subject's identity is declared before the interview begins — role, title, or function is explicit. Anonymous or role-free subjects fail. |
| C-02 | **Prior knowledge scoped** | correctness | essential | Each subject's prior knowledge and blind spots are stated before answers are given — what they know, what they don't know, what they care about. A subject with no prior knowledge section fails. |
| C-03 | **Answers in persona voice** | behavior | essential | Answers are written in the declared persona's distinct voice — vocabulary, concerns, and framing match the role. Answers that could belong to any persona (generic AI output) fail. |
| C-04 | **Evidence explicitly extracted** | coverage | essential | At least one concrete evidence item (insight, concern, requirement, contradiction, or signal) is explicitly extracted per subject — not left implicit in the dialogue. Evidence listed only inside the transcript without a dedicated extraction phase or section fails. |
| C-05 | **Surprising or confirming moment labeled** | depth | essential | Each subject includes at least one moment labeled SURPRISING or CONFIRMING, tied to an explicit prior expectation. Required format: `SURPRISING (expected: X, got: Y)` or `CONFIRMING (validates: Z)`. Unlabeled interesting moments and labels without prior-expectation links do not count. |

At least one follow-up question appears in response to a prior answer within the same interview. |

## Aspirational Criteria (10 points total)

| ID | Criterion | Category | Weight | Pass Condition |
|----|-----------|----------|--------|----------------|
| C-09 | **Cross-interview synthesis** | depth | aspirational | After all interviews, a synthesis section aggregates findings across subjects — naming patterns, contradictions, or convergences and connecting individual evidence items into a composite signal. (N/A when N=1 subject.) |
| C-10 | **Simulation fidelity annotated** | behavior | aspirational | The output includes a brief meta-note distinguishing grounded claims (based on documented persona knowledge or domain context) from constructed plausibility, naming at least one specific basis. Framing as a reflective invitation ("one last thing") or as a structural section both count — the test is presence and specificity, not form. |
| C-11 | **Voice divergence acknowledged** | behavior | aspirational | The output includes a meta-observation naming at least one concrete contrast in how two subjects were made to sound different — citing a specific vocabulary choice, framing preference, or concern priority that distinguishes them. Generic statements ("they had different roles") without citing a specific voice difference fail. |
| C-12 | **Follow-up origin visible** | depth | aspirational | Each follow-up question references a specific moment from the preceding answer — quoting or explicitly pointing to what the subject just said that prompted the new question. Follow-ups that could appear in any order (not anchored to prior content) fail. |
| C-13 | **Expectation register pre-populated** | behavior | aspirational | Prior expectations for each subject are documented in a dedicated register, table, or structured section *before* any interview transcript begins — naming what the interviewer expects the subject to say and why. The register makes C-05 labels structurally mandatory rather than authorial. Expectations added after the fact (inside or after transcripts) fail. |
| C-14 | **INCONCLUSIVE label used where applicable** | depth | aspirational | When a moment neither clearly confirms nor clearly surprises, it is labeled INCONCLUSIVE (or equivalent) rather than forced into SURPRISING or CONFIRMING. At least one INCONCLUSIVE label is present, OR the output includes an explicit note that every labeled moment was unambiguously one or the other. Outputs that apply SURPRISING/CONFIRMING to every notable moment with no acknowledgment of ambiguity fail. |
| C-15 | **Phase-criterion ownership explicit** | behavior | aspirational | Each structural section or phase maps to a single identifiable rubric concern — labeled, numbered, or titled such that its purpose is unambiguous. Phases that bundle multiple concerns without separation, or outputs that rely on the reader to infer phase purpose, fail. (Score PARTIAL for prose-form outputs where structural phases are absent but implied by ordering.) |
| C-16 | **Format templates embedded** | behavior | aspirational | The output embeds at least one explicit format template (e.g., `triggered by: "[exact phrase]"`, `SURPRISING (expected: X, got: Y)`) within the body itself — making the required form self-replicating for subsequent sections or interviews without external documentation. Templates that appear only in a header legend outside the working structure fail. |
| C-17 | **Evidence strength-rated** | depth | aspirational | Each extracted evidence item carries an explicit strength rating (`strong / moderate / weak`) accompanied by a rationale sentence naming why the rating was assigned — e.g., what made the signal reliable, hedged, or ambiguous. Items that are signal-typed (C-08) but carry no strength assessment fail. Ratings without rationale sentences count as PARTIAL. |
| C-18 | **Evidence basis declared** | behavior | aspirational | Each extracted evidence item's origin-of-claim is declared: `verbatim` (directly quoted from the transcript), `inferred` (implied by the answer but not stated), or `corroborated` (consistent signal across two or more subjects). This taxonomy describes how the claim was derived from the transcript, not how experienced the subject is. Outputs that substitute experience-proximity tags (GROUNDED/CONDITIONAL/INFERRED) for origin-of-claim tags fail — the two taxonomies are orthogonal and must not be conflated. Basis declaration may appear in a table column or inline tag — either is accepted. Items with no basis declaration fail. |
| C-19 | **Subject sequence justified** | behavior | aspirational | When multiple subjects are present, their interview order is deliberate and named — e.g., skeptic-first to surface objections before advocates, influence-map order to track cascade, or neutral-first to establish a baseline. A note (one sentence minimum) explains why subjects appear in the given sequence. Arbitrary or unexplained ordering fails. Outputs with a single subject are N/A. |
| C-20 | **Phase completion gated** | behavior | aspirational | Each structural phase ends with an explicit gate, checklist, or checkpoint confirming that required outputs are present before the next phase begins — e.g., "Phase 1 complete when: subject card has role, prior knowledge, and disposition." Phase transitions that flow without gate validation fail. (Score PARTIAL for outputs where gates are implied by section headers or summary lines but not stated as explicit completion conditions.) |
| C-21 | **Objection lifecycle tracked** | depth | aspirational | The synthesis section explicitly names whether the skeptic subject's initial objection *persisted*, *transformed*, or *was resolved* by the time the final interview closed — tracking the objection's fate across the full sequence, not merely noting convergence or contradiction. An assertion that subjects "agreed" or "disagreed" without tracing the objection's movement across interviews fails. (N/A when N=1 subject or when no subject carries a skeptic or challenger disposition.) |
| C-22 | **Vocabulary signature declared as auditable contract** | behavior | aspirational | Each subject has 2–3 role-specific vocabulary terms named in a dedicated pre-interview field or section — terms distinctive enough that any answer can be spot-checked against them. The contract must be declared before the interview transcript begins. Declarations that describe general traits ("uses technical language", "domain expert") without naming specific terms fail. Outputs that name terms for some but not all subjects count as PARTIAL. |
| C-23 | **Subject evidential function declared** | behavior | aspirational | Each subject has a declared *evidential function* — naming what their position in the investigation *does* for the evidence structure, not merely who they are or what disposition they hold. Examples: "evidential baseline that subsequent interviews are measured against", "challenge surface for the advocate's claims", "feasibility probe for implementation realities". A subject described only by role and disposition without a function statement fails. A function statement that restates the role ("the skeptic challenges assumptions") without naming the structural contribution to the evidence chain fails. Score PARTIAL when the function is stated for the first subject but omitted for others. |
| C-24 | **Epistemic standing adjusts synthesis weight** | depth | aspirational | The synthesis explicitly uses subject experience-proximity to adjust the weight of at least one evidence item — naming when an objection or validation comes from someone with direct operational experience versus someone reasoning about a situation they have not yet encountered. The adjustment must be named explicitly (e.g., "this objection is conditional — the subject has not experienced the alternative; treat as a risk to monitor, not a blocker"). Synthesis that treats items from inexperienced and experienced subjects as equivalently weighted without acknowledgment fails. Synthesis that tags epistemic standing per item but never references those tags when drawing conclusions fails. |
| C-25 | **Format template bridged to expectation register** | behavior | aspirational | The SURPRISING/CONFIRMING format template in the extraction section explicitly names the expectation register (C-13) as its source for the "expected:" slot — e.g., `SURPRISING (expected: [Expectation Register → "Expected to say"], got: [what emerged])`. A prompt where the register and the extraction template coexist in separate sections with no named linkage leaves the prior-expectation connection authorial rather than structural: the author must remember to look up, rather than the format enforcing the lookup. Score PARTIAL when the register and template appear in the same structural block but the register field mapping is implied rather than explicitly named. N/A when C-13 is not met at full PASS. |
| C-26 | **Vocabulary signature exercised in transcript** | behavior | aspirational | For each subject with a declared vocabulary signature (C-22), at least one of their named terms appears naturally within that subject's interview transcript — in a question framing or a subject answer. A signature declared pre-interview whose terms never surface in the transcript reveals declaration-without-enforcement: the auditable contract (C-22) was created but never closed. Score PARTIAL when declared vocabulary appears in some subjects' transcripts but not all. N/A when C-22 is not met at full PASS for any subject. |
| C-27 | **Dual evidence taxonomy kept distinct** | depth | aspirational | Evidence items carry both orthogonal tagging dimensions applied separately and non-interchangeably: (1) *experience-proximity* — GROUNDED / CONDITIONAL / INFERRED (how close the subject is to direct operational experience of the claim), and (2) *origin-of-claim* — verbatim / inferred / corroborated (how the claim was derived from the transcript). Outputs that collapse both dimensions into a single tag set — using GROUNDED/CONDITIONAL/INFERRED as if it also answers "was this quoted from the transcript?" — fail. Score PARTIAL when one dimension is fully applied and the other is named but incompletely applied. |
| C-28 | **Moment-label decision taxonomy declared** | behavior | aspirational | The SURPRISING / CONFIRMING / INCONCLUSIVE labeling system is presented as a complete decision taxonomy — with an explicit decision rule for each of the three labels — declared before the first interview transcript begins. The declaration must name the condition under which each label applies: e.g., SURPRISING = prior expectation violated; CONFIRMING = prior expectation upheld; INCONCLUSIVE = signal present but directionally ambiguous, cannot be assigned to either prior-expectation category. Outputs that apply individual labels without declaring the full three-label decision system fail. Outputs that declare only the labels actually used — omitting INCONCLUSIVE because no instance appeared — without stating when INCONCLUSIVE applies fail. The criterion tests whether the labeling is governed by declared rules, not whether all three labels are exercised. Score PARTIAL when two of the three decision rules are declared but the third is omitted or only implicitly addressed. |
| C-29 | **Schema-level template instantiated by example** | behavior | aspirational | When format templates appear as schema-level declarations — table column headers, section-header skeletons, or structural slot listings — the output includes at least one fully instantiated example row, block, or entry demonstrating how the schema applies to real content, not just how the columns or headers are labeled. A table that declares `Origin-of-Claim | Experience-Proximity | Strength` as column headers but provides no populated example row demonstrates schema without instance: the template contract is opened but never closed. Score PARTIAL when an example row is present but one or more template fields are left blank or filled with placeholder text. N/A when the output uses no schema-level template declarations — i.e., all templates are instance-embedded per C-16 rather than header-declared. |
| C-30 | **Prerequisite completion confirmed before dependent mechanism deploys** | behavior | aspirational | When the output deploys a dependent mechanism — a format template bridge (C-25), a vocabulary exercise check (C-26), a dual-taxonomy tagging pass (C-27), or any mechanism that explicitly names a prior criterion as its structural source — it includes a confirmation that the named prerequisite is *fully satisfied* before the dependent mechanism begins. The confirmation may appear as a gate check ("Register fully populated — proceeding to extraction"), a cross-reference ("drawn from the completed register above"), or a structural completion note. A dependent mechanism that deploys without such confirmation treats a PARTIAL prerequisite as equivalent to a full PASS — and it is not: a PARTIAL prerequisite cannot activate a dependent criterion (that criterion scores FAIL, not N/A). Score PARTIAL when prerequisite completion is confirmed for some dependent mechanisms but not all. N/A when the output contains no dependent mechanisms of this type. |
| C-31 | **Pre-interview master gate consolidates all setup prerequisites** | behavior | aspirational | Before the first interview transcript begins, a dedicated master gate confirms that all structural prerequisites for the interview phase are collectively and fully satisfied — naming at minimum: Subject Cards complete, Expectation Register populated, vocabulary signatures declared for all subjects, and moment-label decision taxonomy declared. The master gate must reference its prerequisites explicitly (by name, ID, or section) rather than asserting generic readiness. Individual inline prerequisite checks within setup steps satisfy C-30 but do not substitute for the master gate: the master gate is a synthesis-level closure point at which all pre-interview structure is confirmed complete as a group before Phase 1 can begin. Outputs that confirm prerequisites individually within their own setup sections but lack a consolidating master gate before Phase 1 fail. Score PARTIAL when a master gate exists but explicitly names some prerequisites and omits others. |
| C-32 | **Phase gates formatted as named checklist blocks with enumerated items** | behavior | aspirational | Each phase gate is presented as a consolidated, named block — e.g., "PHASE N GATE: [ ] item one, [ ] item two..." — rather than as scattered inline checkboxes, summary sentences, or gate items embedded within prose. The named block format makes gate scope auditable at a glance: a reviewer can navigate directly to the gate, verify each item independently, and confirm phase completion without searching the section body. Outputs where gate items exist but are distributed across prose or appear as isolated checkboxes without a named gate header score PARTIAL. Outputs where gates are implied by section headings or completion language but enumerate no discrete items fail. |
