**10 criteria across 3 tiers:**

**Essential (5)** — the output is untrustworthy without these:
- C-01: Every achievement has exactly one state (earned/in-progress/locked)
- C-02: Falsified achievement exists as a named, distinct entry
- C-03: Earned/in-progress states cite real artifact evidence
- C-04: In-progress achievements show numeric progress ratios
- C-05: A concrete next recommended action is present

**Recommended (3)** — polish and completeness:
- C-06: All 7 categories represented (or explicitly noted absent)
- C-07: Earned achievements carry dates
- C-08: Locked achievements state unlock conditions

**Aspirational (2)** — raises bar beyond checklist:
- C-09: Narrative synthesis of what the achievement picture means for topic maturity
- C-10: Falsified framed as *positive* signal of intellectual honesty, not failure

The Falsified achievement gets two criteria (C-02 essential + C-10 aspirational) because the skill description calls it out as the most important — one ensures it's present, the other ensures it's framed correctly.
