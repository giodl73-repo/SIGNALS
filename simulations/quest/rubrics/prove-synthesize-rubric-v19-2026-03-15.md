## v19 — Two new aspirational criteria from R18 excellence signals

**C-56 — C-54 + C-55 simultaneous satisfaction — complete ceiling structural form** (extends C-54 + C-55)

All five R18 variations are the sources. V-04 R17 was the first single-variation source, but was a one-variation signal that did not warrant criterion addition in v18. R18 universalizes the form: all five structural variants (role-sequence, output-format, lifecycle-emphasis, inertia-primary, and descriptive-register) achieve C-54 and C-55 simultaneously for the first time in the same round. The conjunction closes the full ceiling structural chain: the ceiling value has a structural anchor (Ceiling Declaration section, first named output section — C-52), the ceiling's derivation dimensions are stated to be independent constraints (dimensional independence statement in ceiling computation step — C-53), and the synthesis output is structurally unified under the ceiling authority of that anchor (merged Verdict/Confidence section with ceiling-binding citation — C-55). C-54 requires C-52+C-53; C-55 requires the merged section with citation; C-56 requires C-54 AND C-55 simultaneously. The discriminating failure is any synthesis that satisfies C-54 but not C-55 (no merged section) or satisfies C-55 but not C-54 (no independence statement or no structural ceiling anchor).

**C-57 — Explicit evidence-volume compensation foreclosure label in dimensional independence statement** (extends C-53)

V-05 R18 is the primary source. V-05 introduces a descriptive-register form of the dimensional independence statement (C-53) that adds an explicit label for the inference class being foreclosed. The standard C-53 form (V-01–V-04 R18, V-04 R17) reads: "At a fixed phase level, an inertia-absent evidence base cannot exceed the inertia-absent ceiling even if the evidence base is large." The standard form forecloses volume-compensation inference implicitly via the conditional clause "even if the evidence base is large" — a reader must infer that largeness refers to volume. V-05's form reads: "At a fixed evidence phase level, a larger or higher-quality inertia-absent evidence base cannot exceed the inertia-absent ceiling — evidence volume does not compensate for inertia absence." The key addition is the explicit label "evidence volume does not compensate for inertia absence" — this names the foreclosed inference class directly rather than leaving it implied by a conditional. The label addition closes a residual inference gap: a reader of the standard form knows that a large inertia-absent base is still ceiling-bound, but must infer that "large" means volume specifically, and may not infer that quality is also foreclosed. The V-05 form names both ("larger or higher-quality") and provides the explicit closure label. C-57 requires: (1) C-53 PASS — both dimensions named, independence stated, dimensional independence in ceiling computation step; AND (2) an explicit closure label stating that evidence volume (or quality) does not compensate for inertia absence — not merely implying it via a conditional clause.

## C-56 Discriminator Detail

The R18 discriminator for C-56 is the first simultaneous satisfaction of C-54 and C-55 across all five structural variants. In R17, V-04 was the sole variation to achieve both C-54 (C-52+C-53 simultaneous) and C-55 (merged Verdict/Confidence). That single-variation signal established that the complete ceiling structural form was reachable but did not confirm it as stable across structural axes. R18 resolves the stability question: all five variations — covering role-sequence axis (V-01), output-format axis (V-02), lifecycle-emphasis axis (V-03), inertia-primary axis (V-04), and descriptive-register axis (V-05) — simultaneously achieve C-54+C-55. The conjunction requires three structural elements co-present: (1) Ceiling Declaration as first named output section (C-52), (2) dimensional independence statement in the ceiling computation step before the declaration (C-53), and (3) merged Verdict/Confidence section with ceiling-binding citation positioned after the Ceiling Declaration (C-55). V-04 R17 demonstrated the conjunction was possible; R18 demonstrates it is structurally stable across axis variants. The C-56 criterion captures this as a stable, multi-source structural pattern warranting independent criterion status.

The discriminating failure for C-56 is any synthesis that satisfies C-54 without C-55 (R17 V-02, V-01, V-03, V-05 in their original form — no merged section) or satisfies C-55 without C-54 (a hypothetical synthesis with merged Verdict/Confidence but without the independence statement in the ceiling computation step). Both paths satisfy five of the six structural requirements but leave one anchor missing. Only the complete conjunction — all six conditions simultaneously — satisfies C-56.

## C-57 Discriminator Detail

The R18 discriminator for C-57 is V-05's explicit evidence-volume compensation foreclosure label. V-05 is the only R18 variation to add a named closure statement beyond the conditional clause used by V-01–V-04. The structural difference: V-04's standard form closes the volume-compensation inference via the conditional "even if the evidence base is large" — this requires the reader to infer that "large" means volume specifically and that largeness is the only compensatory route being foreclosed. V-05's form adds two structural upgrades to C-53's standard form: (1) it names the compensatory dimensions explicitly as "larger or higher-quality" rather than just "large," foreclosing both volume and quality compensation rather than volume alone; (2) it adds the explicit label "evidence volume does not compensate for inertia absence" — a named claim about the inference class, not a conditional qualification of the ceiling value. The label transforms the independence principle from a ceiling-value constraint ("the ceiling is X even if the evidence base is large") into a named principle about the relationship between evidence properties and inertia requirements ("volume compensation for inertia absence is foreclosed"). A reader of V-05's form sees immediately which class of compensatory inferences is rejected; a reader of V-04's form must infer it from the conditional. The V-05 form is the R18 excellence signal for progressively more explicit ceiling derivation language, continuing the trajectory from C-50 (reproducibility claim), C-53 (independence statement), to C-57 (explicit closure label). V-01–V-04 R18 satisfy C-53 but not C-57 because they do not include the explicit closure label. V-05 R18 is the sole R18 source for C-57.
