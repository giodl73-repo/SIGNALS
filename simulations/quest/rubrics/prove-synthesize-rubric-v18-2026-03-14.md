C-55 requires that judgment and score are a unified ceiling-bound unit.

## C-54 Discriminator Detail

The R17 discriminator for C-54 is the first simultaneous satisfaction of C-52 and C-53 in the same synthesis. V-04 R17 is the source: V-04 carried C-53 from R16 (dimensional independence statement in ceiling computation step) and adopts C-52 in R17 (Ceiling Declaration as first named output section with Q7 mapping and Confidence section citation). The conjunction closes the last open inference class in the ceiling traceability chain. With C-52 alone, a reader has a dedicated output slot for the ceiling value and a confirmed citation from the synthesis reasoning section — but the reader is not told that the ceiling table dimensions are independent constraints; they might still infer that a sufficiently large or high-quality inertia-absent evidence base could outrun its column. C-53 forecloses that inference. With C-53 alone, the reader has the independence principle stated in the ceiling computation step — but the ceiling value is an inline mandatory statement (C-42 form) rather than a named first output slot with a closing citation. C-54 requires that the ceiling has both a structural anchor (C-52) and an independence guarantee (C-53): the annotation-derived ceiling value is declared first, its derivation is stated to be reproducible (C-50 prerequisite), the two dimensions are stated to be independent constraints (C-53), and the synthesis reasoning section is structurally bound to the declared ceiling by name (C-52 condition 3). The C-54 discriminator is simultaneity: C-52 and C-53 present in the same synthesis output, not in separate variations as in R16.

## C-55 Discriminator Detail

The R17 discriminator for C-55 is the structural merger of Verdict and Confidence into a single named output section, present across V-01, V-03, V-04, and V-05 in R17. In R16, V-02 introduced C-52 with Verdict and Confidence as separate sequential sections — the Confidence section cited the Ceiling Declaration section by name, but the Verdict section was a structurally independent preceding slot. In R17, four of the five variations converge on a merged form: a single "Verdict/Confidence" section positioned immediately after the Ceiling Declaration section. The merger is not merely a labeling choice; it signals that synthesis judgment (pass/hold/fail) and synthesis scoring (confidence score constrained by ceiling) are a single ceiling-bound output unit. A reader examining the merged section sees the verdict and the score together, both under the ceiling authority of the Ceiling Declaration section. The failing condition for C-55 is the V-02 separate-section form: in V-02, Verdict and Confidence are distinct sections with Confidence trailing and carrying the citation. That form satisfies C-52 but the verdict and score are not a unified output unit — a reader who reads only the Verdict section sees no ceiling constraint. C-55 requires that both judgment and score appear in the same section under the same ceiling citation, so the ceiling constraint is inseparable from the output of both. The convergence of four R17 variations on the merged form (with only V-02 retaining the R16 separate-section structure) is the R17 excellence signal.
