## Discriminating frontier

The three-way tie at 100 under v5 (V-03, V-04, V-05) is broken by independent structural requirements:

- **C-20** separates outputs that add mechanism citations post-hoc from those that commit to structural assumptions before analysis runs
- **C-21** separates prose-embedded mechanism citations from column-isolated ones detectable by structural inspection
- **C-22** separates free-form NOT POSSIBLE verdicts from inertia-anchored "Assumption Confirmed" verdicts
- **C-23** generalizes the evidential asymmetry fix beyond escalation vectors to all no-findings throughout the trace
- **C-24** separates roll-up blocks that restate mechanisms inline from those that back-reference the vector table

A suggested next criterion (for v7) to further separate the field: **CLEAN composite verdict naming mechanism types** -- the roll-up names each vector's mechanism type, not just the count. This would split V-04's restated-mechanism list from V-05's count-based conclusion.
