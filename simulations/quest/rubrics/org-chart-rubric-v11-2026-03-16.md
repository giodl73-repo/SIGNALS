### v11 additions rationale

**C-35** (Per-charter iteration structure in GATE 3 field-format verification): Signal from the V-03/V-05 PASS vs. V-01/V-04 PASS comparison in the Round 10 scorecard. All four variations satisfy C-33 -- they all verify Quorum format and Membership TYPE, and they all name prohibited forms. The structural difference: V-01 and V-04 implement C-33 as inline FORMAT-VERIFY items in CHECKPOINT-3 (applied to the charter set collectively), while V-03 and V-05 implement C-33 as a dedicated CHARTER-FORMAT-AUDIT loop that visits each charter individually before the aggregate count check closes. The scorecard explicitly names this as the next excellence signal: "V-05 is the preferred R11 base if future rubric signals probe per-charter format enforcement granularity." The bypass C-33 leaves open: C-33 governs what fields to inspect and what prohibited forms to name, but is silent on whether the inspection is per-charter or batch. Inline FORMAT-VERIFY items satisfy C-33's content requirements while leaving open the structural question of whether a single batch-assessment of the set correctly identifies which specific charter is malformed. Under production conditions -- multiple charters, format pressure, length constraints -- a batch check that examines the set collectively may produce a soft-pass ("the charters look correctly formatted") without visiting each charter individually. The CHARTER-FORMAT-AUDIT loop enforces individual charter adjudication: each charter receives its own REJECT or PROCEED before the loop advances. The structural difference matters precisely in the ambiguous case: a set of N charters where one is malformed and N-1 are correct. The batch check may aggregate-pass on the majority; the loop cannot -- it must REJECT when it reaches the malformed charter. C-33 governs field-format verification content; C-35 governs its structural form. A variation satisfying C-35 necessarily satisfies C-33.

**C-36** (Per-charter role continuity within GATE 3 charter audit): Signal from the V-05 structure. V-05 is the Round 10 preferred base, combining the CHARTER-FORMAT-AUDIT loop (C-35) with a ROLE-CONTINUITY item in CHECKPOINT-3 (C-34). The gap: V-05's ROLE-CONTINUITY check remains a separate aggregate item in CHECKPOINT-3, executed after the loop completes -- after all charters have been processed. A charter introduced mid-sequence with an undeclared role name (e.g., "Distinguished Engineer (ADVISORY)" never appearing in ROLES-LOADED) passes through V-05's per-charter loop (the format is syntactically correct: C-33 PASS, C-35 PASS) and is only encountered by the role-continuity check in the post-loop CHECKPOINT-3. The bypass C-35 and C-34 leave open: C-35 requires per-charter iteration for format; C-34 requires aggregate role lookup after production; neither requires that role lookup occur at per-charter scope within the loop. The structural separation between the per-charter loop (format) and the aggregate step (role continuity) means a violation can be produced, pass per-charter format inspection, and sit in the output until the aggregate step. Integrating the GATE 0 typed-role-list lookup into the per-charter loop changes the detection point: the undeclared role name is caught when the charter that contains it is visited -- during production, not after. This follows the same coverage-expansion pattern as C-22 -> C-29 (one blocking step -> all-gate blocking steps): coverage expands from aggregate to per-unit. Here the unit is a charter; C-36 requires role continuity to be verified at charter-unit scope rather than at set scope. C-34 governs checkpoint-execution-scope continuity enforcement; C-36 governs per-charter continuity enforcement within the iteration loop. A variation satisfying C-36 necessarily satisfies both C-35 (the loop must exist) and C-34 (role continuity is enforced, with stronger per-charter scope).

### v10 additions rationale (preserved)

**C-33** (GATE 3 field-format verification coverage): Signal from V-01 C-19 PASS + C-29 PASS. V-01's CHECKPOINT-3 satisfies the blocking verification requirement (C-29) and the pair-count equivalence requirement (C-19): "Governance ORT rows: [N]. Charters produced: [N]. MATCH -- proceed to GATE 3 STATUS." The checkpoint closes the gate correctly. The gap: the checkpoint verifies that the right number of charters exist, not that the charters are internally correct. Neither C-19 nor C-29 specifies what the checkpoint must inspect within the produced charters beyond their count.
