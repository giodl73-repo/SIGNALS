**Rubric summary:**

**5 Essential (60 pts):**
- C-01: Named surprises present — departure framing required, not just findings
- C-02: Signal tracing per surprise — each must name its source artifact
- C-03: Design impact assessed per surprise — must name the affected decision
- C-04: Synthesis not summary — surprise/expected partition must be structural
- C-05: Surprise specificity — falsifiable against this investigation, not generic

**3 Recommended (30 pts):**
- C-06: Expectation counterfactual — "expected X; found Y" structure explicit per entry
- C-07: Institutional framing — addressed to the next team, not the current one
- C-08: Cross-signal pattern identification — if surprises share a cause, name it

**2 Aspirational (10 pts):**
- C-09: Surprise hierarchy — ranked by design impact with argued rationale
- C-10: Riskiest surprise flagged — names the assumption at risk + what would need to hold

**Golden threshold:** all essential pass + composite >= 80. Max = 100.

The key design tension in this rubric: C-01/C-04 enforce the synthesis-not-summary boundary from both sides (must have surprises; must have *only* surprises). C-06/C-07 pull in opposite directions (backward-facing counterfactual vs. forward-facing institutional address) — both passing is what distinguishes a finished echo from a draft one.
