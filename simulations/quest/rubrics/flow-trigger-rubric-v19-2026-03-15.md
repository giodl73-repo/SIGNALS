## flow-trigger Rubric v19

### Changes from v18 to v19

**Excellence signals from R19** — all five R19 variations inherit full passes on C-01 through C-56. Scoring differentiates on three new aspirational criteria C-57, C-58, C-59, each failing exactly two or three variations on a distinct structural gap. V-05 passes all three, confirming the three properties are independently auditable and collectively sufficient.

**C-57 added** — *Rule 4 uses em-dash two-part structure: assertion clause + em-dash + operational restatement naming the exclusion boundary and governing authority*

V-02 and V-03 use period-separated independent sentences for Rule 4's scope restriction. Each sentence stands alone, making the assertion-restatement relationship inferential rather than structural: a reader can internalize the assertion ("Row N conformance does not imply Row N+1 conformance") and stop before encountering the exclusion restatement. V-01, V-04, and V-05 use an em-dash bound form ("does not imply Row N+1 conformance — phase-body gate-statement verification is governed separately by Invariant 2") that makes the assertion and exclusion restatement structurally inseparable: a reader cannot internalize the assertion without reading through the em-dash to find the governing-authority statement. The same em-dash architecture governs Invariant 3 (C-53) and Rule 1's sub-clause (C-50); C-57 extends that architecture to Rule 4's scope restriction. C-56 requires a scope declaration to exist; this criterion requires the em-dash structural form that makes the assertion-restatement pair a single bound artifact.

**C-58 added** — *Rule 2 ends with a forward reference to Rule 3's self-tagging requirement, closing the obligation-to-consequence chain within a single rule*

V-01 and V-03 end Rule 2 at the obligation statement ("non-conforming under this Rule and under Invariant 3") with no reference to Rule 3. A reader auditing a gate cell against Rule 2 can fully satisfy all Rule 2 requirements without ever discovering that Rule 3 imposes a self-tagging consequence on non-conforming cells. V-02, V-04, and V-05 append "; non-conforming cells are subject to Rule 3's self-tagging requirement" to Rule 2's closing clause. This closes the obligation-to-consequence chain within Rule 2: a reader encounters the downstream consequence without sequential document reading or prior knowledge of Rule 3's existence. Without this forward reference, the two-rule chain from obligation to consequence is structurally silent at the obligation end — the consequence is only discoverable by a reader who already knows to continue to Rule 3.

**C-59 added** — *Invariant 6 declared before Phase 1, naming the co-authority verification obligation and assigning FM-26 as the taggable defect for partial co-authority verification*

V-01, V-02, and V-04 declare co-authority in Rule 1 (which names both sources) but provide no invariant obligating a verifier to check both simultaneously. A verifier who satisfies Rules 1–4 has violated no stated obligation by checking only one co-authoritative source; the co-authority relationship is a navigational declaration rather than an enforcement requirement. V-03 and V-05 add Invariant 6: a verifier SHALL check both co-authoritative sources simultaneously; a verifier who checks only one source when both are in effect violates Invariant 6 and SHALL be tagged FM-26. FM-26 in the catalog makes partial-verification defects taggable inline — transforming co-authority from a declared structural property into an auditable verification obligation with a named defect class. Without Invariant 6, FM-26 cannot exist as a meaningful catalog entry; without FM-26, Invariant 6 is an obligation with no enforcement mechanism. The two are structurally coupled and must be declared together.

**Scores updated:** Aspirational 49 → 52 pts. Max 139 → 142.
