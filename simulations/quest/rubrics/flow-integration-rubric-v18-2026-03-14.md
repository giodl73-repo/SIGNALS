## What changed

**Two new criteria from R17:**

**C-45 — C-43 failure-class conditional sub-type: SHOULD failure mechanism (5 pts)**
From V-01 (R17). SHOULD introduces *conditional-recommendation* epistemic weakening ("recommended but not required") — semantically distinct from MAY's discretionality in that some residual obligation force remains ("you ought to, but you may not"). A single SHOULD substitution cascades deterministically to fail C-37, C-39, C-40, and C-43 — the entire stakes-anchor criterion chain collapses. Closes Q1 (R17) SHOULD pathway. Requires C-43.

**C-46 — C-43 failure-class discretionary sub-type: MAY failure mechanism and compound conditionality (5 pts)**
From V-02 (R17). MAY introduces *discretionary-possibility* weakening ("optional" — no obligation force at all), distinct from SHOULD's conditionality. The V-02 form compounds MAY with a conditional clause ("if not documented"), producing the first two-mechanism failure pattern observed: each mechanism (modal weakening, conditional subordination) independently disqualifies; neither rescues the other. Closes Q1 (R17) MAY pathway. Completes the C-43 failure-class taxonomy: MUST/SHALL (pass, C-43) / SHOULD (conditional sub-type, C-45) / MAY (discretionary sub-type + compound conditionality, C-46). Requires C-43 and C-45.
