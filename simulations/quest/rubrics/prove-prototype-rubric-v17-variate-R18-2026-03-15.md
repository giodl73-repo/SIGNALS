## Competitor Lifecycle Framing

The inertia competitor moves through four states. At each boundary, here is what you
need to know about why the gate exists.

**DESIGN — NOT YET IDENTIFIED — do not name the competitor here**
If you name the incumbent now, your scope choices and success thresholds will be written
against a known target, not against the hypothesis. You will unconsciously design a
prototype that exploits specific weaknesses of the named competitor. Keeping the
competitor unnamed means your design is valid against any status-quo approach — you are
testing the hypothesis, not exploiting a specific target.

**CALIBRATE — IDENTIFIED AND COMMITTED — name it and measure it in one step**
If you name the competitor without immediately measuring B-00, you create a window where
the outperform threshold could be positioned after you already know the competitor's
profile. The two sub-transitions — naming (NOT YET IDENTIFIED -> IDENTIFIED) then
measuring (IDENTIFIED -> IDENTIFIED AND COMMITTED) — happen in sequence within Phase 4.
This ordering means the baseline is always independently measured before any threshold
is set against it.

**BUILD — REFERENCED — build and measure, no comparison yet**
If you compare to B-00 now, your prototype choices and the way you record the measurement
will be shaped by how the comparison is going. The raw result needs to be a clean
observation before it is interpreted. Record the number; CLOSE will do the comparison.

**CLOSE — DISCHARGED — incorporate the competitor into both structural surfaces**
The competitor is not fully discharged until its name appears in the results table
baseline column label (Phase 7) and in the terminal arc record (CLOSE COMPLETE). If
either is missing, a later reader cannot verify from the structural surfaces alone that
the competitor was present and measured. Both surfaces together create a complete and
scannable accountability chain.

NOT YET IDENTIFIED -> IDENTIFIED AND COMMITTED -> REFERENCED -> DISCHARGED

REQUIRED: Each transition gates at the boundary specified. FORBIDDEN: Status MUST NOT
advance before that boundary.

## Competitor Lifecycle Framing

REQUIRED reading before execution. The inertia competitor progresses through four states.

  against a known target rather than against the hypothesis; the test will measure
  target-exploitability rather than hypothesis validity
- ISOLATION PURPOSE: unnamed competitor through DESIGN guarantees scope and metric are
  hypothesis-driven, not target-driven; the design is valid against any status-quo
  approach, not just the identified one
