### Design Rationale

**Why R1 V-05 only scored 1/5 aspirational:** V-05's Phase 2/3 templates had steelman and Decides/Escalates *slots* but no minimum-content guards. A run can fill in "coordinates well today" and "owns the area" and satisfy both columns syntactically without passing C-11 or C-12.

**What changed:**
- **V-01** adds a numbered list with a floor of 2 concrete mechanisms before the rebuttal can begin — the slot now has a count requirement
- **V-02** replaces the single Decision Scope column with a mandatory two-column split, names the failure condition (`"owns X" does not pass either column`)
- **V-03** is the only R2 variation that targets C-09/C-10 — these were *never* in R1 and require their own sections
- **V-04** combines C-11+C-12+C-13 in a clean imperative register (proven in R1 to cover essentials reliably)
- **V-05** extends the R1 V-05 phase structure with two new phases (Evolution Roadmap, Anti-Pattern Watch) and promotes the aspirational slots to explicit phase deliverables with failure conditions named inline
