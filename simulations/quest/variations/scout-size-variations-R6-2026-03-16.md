## Summary

Five complete variations, three single-axis then two combined:

| Variation | Axis | Primary Mechanism | Key Rubric Targets |
|-----------|------|-------------------|--------------------|
| **V-01** | Role sequence | Two-phase charter separation prevents C-15 conflation structurally — conflation = charter violation | C-15, C-20 |
| **V-02** | Structural format | Column headers + field label slots make tier violations observable in the skeleton itself | C-13, C-16, C-18 |
| **V-03** | Inline failure examples | WRONG/CORRECT blocks appear *before* each field — not in post-output checklist — so the failure mode is active at generation time | C-17, C-19 |
| **V-04** | Inertia framing | Status-quo as Step 1 anchors all subsequent fields in workaround cost — C-03 becomes the analytical lens rather than a late obligation | C-03 |
| **V-05** | Multi-axis (V-01 + V-02 + V-03) | All three mechanisms layered: role separation + structural encoding + inline guards | C-15, C-16, C-17, C-18, C-19, C-20 |

**Key architectural decisions carried across all variations:**
- Sensitivity conditions always name destination tier in the field label or FORMAT line before the model fills the slot
- Gap field always carries a prose non-overlap rule or a charter prohibition on the same sentence as the field definition
- Inertia check always requires both workaround name AND cost direction — "named but no direction" is a documented failure mode in every variation

**V-05 is the highest-rubric candidate** — it is also the most structurally complex and most likely to produce verbose output. V-02 is the cleanest for human-readable artifacts; V-03 is the best teaching tool.
