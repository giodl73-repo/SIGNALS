LABEL: `INVESTIGATION-ATTEMPT-1` before writing any finding.

WRITE: INERTIA-FINDING-A — name the specific workflow, tool, or process in production that this agenda item displaces.
  ENFORCE: precision required; generic "current process" fails.
  VALIDATE: finding label is the first token on the line.
    ACCEPTABLE: `INERTIA-FINDING-A: legacy-approval-workflow — the team currently routes all changes through...`
    ACCEPTABLE: `INERTIA-FINDING-A: daily-deploy-script — invoked by every team member before each push`
    FAILS: `(a) legacy-approval-workflow` — parenthesized letter, not the INERTIA-FINDING-A token; fails C-27
    FAILS: `Finding A: legacy-approval-workflow` — abbreviated label; full token required

WRITE: INERTIA-FINDING-B — name the integration surface at risk; specific systems, APIs, contracts, or downstream consumers.
  VALIDATE: finding label is the first token on the line.
    ACCEPTABLE: `INERTIA-FINDING-B: webhook-contract — the Notification Service depends on...`
    FAILS: `(b) webhook-contract` — parenthesized letter; fails C-27

WRITE: INERTIA-FINDING-C — name the team whose cognitive habits would break and the specific decision-making habit they rely on today.
  ENFORCE: name both the team and the habit; generic attribution fails.
  VALIDATE: finding label is the first token on the line.
    ACCEPTABLE: `INERTIA-FINDING-C: platform-team-gate-habit — the Platform team relies on the daily gate review as their primary change-visibility signal`
    FAILS: `(c) platform-team` — parenthesized letter; fails C-27

WRITE: INERTIA-FINDING-D — the non-obvious switching cost the proposal author almost certainly did not account for; specific enough that the author would say "I missed that."
  VALIDATE: finding label is the first token on the line.
    ACCEPTABLE: `INERTIA-FINDING-D: undocumented-rollback-dependency — the deployment script is the only automated rollback trigger; removing it leaves no recovery path for failed releases`
    FAILS: `(d) undocumented-rollback-dependency` — parenthesized letter; fails C-27

GATE-1 intra-phase retry loop:
CONFIRM: Does INERTIA-FINDING-D reveal a non-obvious cost the proposal author would not have anticipated?
WRITE: Answer YES or NO with one-sentence basis.

ENFORCE: the gate loop runs within Phase 1; Phase 2 is always reached:
  IF GATE-N Answer: YES → CONFIRM loop exit; fill ## INERTIA RECORD and PHASE-1-COMMIT immediately; Phase 2 is unconditional.
  IF GATE-N Answer: NO → INCREMENT N; LABEL: INVESTIGATION-ATTEMPT-N+1; WRITE: four new INERTIA-FINDING-* labels rewritten from a different angle; GATE: GATE-N+1 same question; re-evaluate; REPEAT.

REQUIRE: each rewrite carries a new sequential `INVESTIGATION-ATTEMPT-N+1` label; a rewrite without the incremented label fails C-22 and C-24.

WRITE: ## INERTIA RECORD — fill from the passing attempt's findings:
  PRINT: INERTIA-FINDING-A: [one-phrase anchor — the specific workflow or artifact named in this finding]
  PRINT: INERTIA-FINDING-B: [one-phrase anchor — the integration surface named in this finding]
  PRINT: INERTIA-FINDING-C: [one-phrase anchor — the team and habit named in this finding]
  PRINT: INERTIA-FINDING-D: [one-phrase anchor — the non-obvious switching cost named in this finding]
  VALIDATE: each ## INERTIA RECORD entry carries a content anchor readable without Phase 1 prose.
    ACCEPTABLE: `INERTIA-FINDING-A: workflow-disruption` — keyword identifies the finding
    ACCEPTABLE: `INERTIA-FINDING-B: re-training overhead` — two-word anchor identifies the cost
    ACCEPTABLE: `INERTIA-FINDING-D: undocumented-rollback-dependency` — names the non-obvious cost specifically
    FAILS: `INERTIA-FINDING-A:` — bare label, no anchor; fails C-34
    FAILS: `INERTIA-FINDING-A: [one-phrase anchor]` — unfilled skeleton placeholder; fails C-34

PRINT: INERTIA INVARIANT — the terminal line of ## INERTIA RECORD.
  PRINT: `INERTIA INVARIANT: sealed at PHASE-1-COMMIT — findings may not be added, removed, or modified without reopening Phase 1.`
  VALIDATE: seal declaration is present and complete — three independent failure modes enumerated:
    ACCEPTABLE: `INERTIA INVARIANT: sealed at PHASE-1-COMMIT — findings may not be added, removed, or modified without reopening Phase 1.` — commit reference present AND prohibition clause present; structural contract complete; passes C-40 and C-44
    FAILS (a): `INERTIA INVARIANT: sealed at PHASE-1-COMMIT` with nothing following the commit reference — commit-present/prohibition-absent; navigational reference without stated prohibition; fails C-44
    FAILS (b): `INERTIA INVARIANT:` — label present but both commit reference and prohibition clause absent from the line; fails C-44
    FAILS (c): ## INERTIA RECORD closed with no INERTIA INVARIANT line — seal element absent entirely; fails C-40

ENFORCE: ## INERTIA RECORD is an independently addressable section; it appears before PHASE-1-COMMIT.
ENFORCE: PHASE-1-COMMIT: is the terminal element of Phase 1 — no Phase 1 content may appear after it.
PRINT: PHASE-1-COMMIT: [locked] — Investigation attempt [N] complete, GATE-[N] answered YES. Citation-anchor manifest declared in ## INERTIA RECORD above — modifications to that record require updating this commit; modifications to this commit require updating that record. Findings A–D locked. Downstream CITE: and RESPONDS-TO: valid only against labels enumerated in ## INERTIA RECORD. Phase 1 closed.
  | ENFORCE: terminal position — NO PHASE 1 CONTENT MAY FOLLOW THIS LINE.

ENFORCE: the bidirectionality clause in PHASE-1-COMMIT is a declared coupling contract — both directions stated explicitly.
VALIDATE: bidirectionality clause states both directions of coupling obligation.
  ACCEPTABLE: PHASE-1-COMMIT includes "modifications to that record require updating this commit; modifications to this commit require updating that record" — forward and reverse coupling obligations both named; passes C-41 and C-45
  FAILS: PHASE-1-COMMIT includes only "modifications to that record require updating this commit" without the reverse direction — half-coupling; reverse obligation absent; fails C-45
  FAILS: PHASE-1-COMMIT reads "Citation-anchor manifest declared in ## INERTIA RECORD above; findings A–D locked" with no coupling clause — navigational pointer, no contract; fails C-41

**PHASE 2 fill rules:**

LOAD: all participants from Phase 0 roster.
ASSIGN: Tier 1 (CHALLENGERS) — roles whose charter orientation interrogates feasibility, risk, or disruption to existing systems; ENFORCE: Inertia-Advocate is always Tier 1.
  ENFORCE: these roles speak before all others.
ASSIGN: Tier 2 (CONDITIONALS) — roles that hold approval pending specific named conditions.
ASSIGN: Tier 3 (ADVOCATES) — roles aligned with the proposal's stated goals.
  ENFORCE: these roles speak last.
ENFORCE: tie-break within any tier — higher institutional veto authority speaks first.

SORT-CHECK fill rule (intra-phase loop):
PRINT: `Test: Tier 1 and Tier 2 both empty?`
CONFIRM: Result YES or NO.
VALIDATE: if Result YES — REQUIRE reassignment: name the mis-sorted role, name the target tier, state the reason specific to this agenda item and active INERTIA-FINDING findings; RE-PRINT corrected TIER SORT block; RE-PRINT new SORT-CHECK block; LOOP until Result NO.
  ACCEPTABLE: `Result: NO — Tier 1 contains [Inertia-Advocate] and [Architect]; challenger-first ordering valid`
  FAILS: `Result: YES` followed by continuation without reassignment — gate answered YES but no reassignment performed; fails C-18 and C-22
ENFORCE: SORT-CHECK must appear as a discrete labeled gate with both `Test:` and `Result:` fields; correct ordering without SORT-CHECK fails C-25.
CONFIRM: Result NO is the Phase 2 exit condition.
ENFORCE: PHASE-2-COMMIT: is the terminal element of Phase 2 — no Phase 2 content may appear after it.
PRINT: PHASE-2-COMMIT: [locked] — TIER SORT complete, SORT-CHECK Result NO, Inertia-Advocate in Tier 1, challenger-first ordering confirmed. Phase 2 closed.
  | ENFORCE: terminal position — NO PHASE 2 CONTENT MAY FOLLOW THIS LINE.

**PHASE 3 fill rules:**

SYNTHESIZE-CHECK fill rule (Phase 1→Phase 3 continuity gate):
CONFIRM: the sorted Phase 3 participant set contains at least one role whose orientation is grounded in defending existing workflow or switching costs (an inertia-focused role).
  IF YES → fill SYNTHESIZE-CHECK field as `YES — [role name] covers inertia perspective in Tier 1`; proceed to voice blocks.
  IF NO → fill SYNTHESIZE-CHECK field as `NO — INJECTING Inertia-Advocate into Tier 1`; ADD Inertia-Advocate as an additional Tier 1 participant with orientation `defends current-state workflow against disruption — synthesized for Phase 1→Phase 3 continuity`; note `[SYNTHESIZED]` in the participant voice header.
  ENFORCE: INERTIA RECORD findings must have an owner-voice in Phase 3; this is a structural guarantee — not charter-dependent, not tier-sort-dependent; fails C-46 if absent.

ENFORCE: Tier 1 voices before Tier 2 voices before Tier 3 voices.

PRINT: `STANCE: [BLOCK / CONDITION / APPROVE]` as a standalone labeled line before any prose for each participant.
VALIDATE: STANCE: is a committed structural declaration — prose must not soften, qualify, or contradict it.
  ACCEPTABLE: `STANCE: BLOCK` on its own line — label + single token; position unambiguous; passes C-06
  ACCEPTABLE: `STANCE: CONDITION` on its own line — declares conditional hold before any endorsement text
  FAILS: `The architect views this proposal as too risky to approve at this time` — stance in prose, no STANCE: label; fails C-06
  FAILS: `STANCE: BLOCK (pending clarification)` — qualified token; parenthetical softens the commitment; fails C-06

WRITE: 2-4 sentences per participant from their charter-documented role orientation responding to this specific agenda item.
REQUIRE (Tier 1): ground concern in a named INERTIA-FINDING label from ## INERTIA RECORD where relevant.
REQUIRE (Tier 1 / Inertia-Advocate): ENFORCE Inertia-Advocate explicitly references INERTIA-FINDING-D by label — the non-obvious finding is the Inertia-Advocate's primary argument.
REQUIRE (Tier 2): name the specific approval condition precisely; "address concerns" fails.
REQUIRE (Tier 3): fill CITE: and RESPONDS-TO: before any endorsement prose.

CITE fill rule (Tier 3 only):
PRINT: `CITE: INERTIA-FINDING-[A/B/C/D] — [content of that finding supporting this endorsement]`
ENFORCE: named label is the first element after `CITE:`; prose before the label fails C-20.
VALIDATE:

  ACCEPTABLE: `CITE: INERTIA-FINDING-C — re-training overhead: the phased rollout reduces this because...`
  FAILS: `CITE: The workflow disruption finding supports adoption...` — finding referenced by paraphrase, no label; fails C-20
  FAILS: `CITE: (a) — workflow-disruption` — parenthesized letter, not the INERTIA-FINDING-A token; fails C-20

RESPONDS-TO fill rule (Tier 3 only):
PRINT: `RESPONDS-TO: "[named concern]" — [one sentence addressing it]`
REQUIRE: quoted string names a specific finding or concern.
VALIDATE:
  ACCEPTABLE: `RESPONDS-TO: "INERTIA-FINDING-A: workflow-disruption" — the migration plan stages the transition to avoid hard cutover`
  ACCEPTABLE: `RESPONDS-TO: "[Architect]'s concern that the webhook contract will break" — the adapter layer preserves the existing contract`
  FAILS: `RESPONDS-TO: "Inertia concerns have been acknowledged"` — generic, no attribution; fails C-21
  FAILS: `RESPONDS-TO: "The challenger raised valid points"` — no specific attribution; fails C-21

VALIDATE: at least one participant declares STANCE: CONDITION or STANCE: BLOCK; all-APPROVE means Phase 2 sort is invalid — REOPEN Phase 2; RE-ASSIGN tiers; RE-PRINT corrected TIER SORT; RE-RUN SORT-CHECK.

    FAILS (a): `STANCE INVARIANT: sealed at PHASE-3-COMMIT` with nothing following the commit reference — commit-present/prohibition-absent; navigational reference without stated prohibition; fails C-44
    FAILS (b): `STANCE INVARIANT:` — label present but both commit reference and prohibition clause absent from the line; fails C-44
    FAILS (c): ## STANCE MANIFEST closed with no STANCE INVARIANT line — seal element absent entirely; fails C-40

**PHASE 5 fill rules:**

  WRITE: Owner — named role from the Phase 0 participant roster; ENFORCE: not a team, a specific role.

ENFORCE: PHASE-5-COMMIT: is the terminal element of Phase 5 and the final element of the simulation.
