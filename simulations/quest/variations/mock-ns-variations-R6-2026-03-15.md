## Round 6 Variations — Summary

**Five axes selected:**

| # | Axis | C-21 | C-22 | Predicted |
|---|------|------|------|-----------|
| V-01 | Phrasing register (catch-all at A-1, no affirmative closure at S-3) | No | Yes | ~99.3 |
| V-02 | Inertia framing (affirmative closure at S-3, no catch-all at A-1) | Yes | No | ~99.3 |
| V-03 | Lifecycle emphasis (explicit phase labels, R5 baseline — neither new criterion) | No | No | ~98.6 |
| V-04 | Role sequence + output format (table-structured anti-displacement table for C-22; standalone affirmative closure row for C-21) | Yes | Yes | 100 |
| V-05 | Full convergence (prose, all 22 criteria) | Yes | Yes | 100 |

**Key design decisions:**

- **V-01 vs V-02** is the primary discriminant for Round 6: which new criterion is the higher-leverage single addition? C-22's catch-all covers unrecognized instruction types at the consumption site; C-21's affirmative closure converts path enumeration from a list to a positive fact at the compute site.

- **V-03** (lifecycle baseline) is the control. R5 V-05 already carried C-21 language — V-03 intentionally strips both C-21 and C-22 to isolate their individual contributions. The lifecycle phase framing (`== COMPUTE PHASE ==` / `== ASSEMBLY PHASE ==`) is the axis variation, not new protection language.

- **V-04** tests the table-structured presentation of C-22 specifically: the anti-displacement table makes the "ANY OTHER INSTRUCTION — NO" row visually undeniable rather than a tail clause in a prose sentence.

- **V-05** is the reference convergence with all 22 criteria in clean prose form, matching the R5 V-05 structure extended to include C-22.
