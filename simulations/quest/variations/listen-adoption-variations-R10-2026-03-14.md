## Round 10 Variations — listen-adoption

**Rubric v10 targets:** C-28 (spread label), C-29 (answer-form citations), C-30 (per-gate stamps)

### Single-axis

| Variation | Axis | Target | Key change |
|-----------|------|--------|------------|
| **V-01** | Output format | C-28 | Adds **TABLE 3** after PART 2 with fixed column header `"Spread Mechanism: [named signal or artifact — not generic word-of-mouth]"`. Generic entries are structurally incompatible with the column type. |
| **V-02** | Phrasing register | C-29 | Champion Q3 and PHASE 3 chasm use **answer-form questions** — "Which Named Inertia ID is driving the chasm?" — so the ID is the *answer*, not a fill-form slot value. Omission leaves the question syntactically incomplete. |
| **V-03** | Lifecycle emphasis | C-30 | SECTION K adds a mandatory **Execution Stamp column**. `"PASS — no correction triggered"` is the required stamp for every passing gate — empty rows are prohibited. Resolves the paradox-of-strength. |

### Combined

| Variation | Axes | Targets | Notes |
|-----------|------|---------|-------|
| **V-04** | Output format + Phrasing register | C-28 + C-29 | TABLE 3 + answer-form questions. C-13/C-14 also benefit from question-form pressure (V-04-R9 evidence). C-30 is the remaining gap. Predicted ~190. |
| **V-05** | Output format + Phrasing register + Lifecycle | C-28 + C-29 + C-30 | All three new criteria structurally enforced. Maximum R10 ceiling. Predicted ~195. |

### Design principle for C-30

The "paradox of strength": V-04-R9's best architecture produces high compliance for C-13/C-14, meaning H and I almost never fire corrections, meaning C-19 (requires a visible correction block) fails in the strongest case. V-03/V-05 close this by making the terminal section prove the audit ran *regardless of outcome* — a passing gate that leaves no stamp is unverifiable, which is treated as a SECTION K failure.
