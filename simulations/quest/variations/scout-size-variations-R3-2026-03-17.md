## scout-size R3 — Variation Summary

Five variations targeting the four new v3 aspirational criteria (C-14 through C-17):

| ID | Axis | Core mechanism | C-14 | C-15 | C-16 | C-17 |
|----|------|----------------|:----:|:----:|:----:|:----:|
| **V-01** | Unknowns-as-section | Dedicated `## OPEN UNKNOWNS` with `Unknown:` / `Impact:` / `Movement:` typed fields — blank row = visible omission | H | L | L | M |
| **V-02** | Hypothesis-revision lifecycle | `## PRELIMINARY HYPOTHESIS` before analysis; synthesis must explicitly confirm/revise with required phrasing forms | L | H | L | L |
| **V-03** | Section self-indictment | Inline `> FAILURE MODE FOR THIS SECTION:` block co-located inside synthesis and unknowns instructions | M | L | L | H |
| **V-04** | Unknowns + Hypothesis (combined) | Axes A+B together — unknowns section improves hypothesis accuracy; hypothesis constrains synthesis | H | H | L | M |
| **V-05** | Full integration | All axes + AMEND cascade instruction inside synthesis step + 17-criterion self-check | H | H | H | H |

**Key structural decisions made:**

- **C-14**: The gap moves from a single `Gap:` prose line inside the confidence section to a structurally separate section with typed fields. Missing entries become missing rows, not absent prose — making omissions self-evident.

- **C-15**: The `## PRELIMINARY HYPOTHESIS` block appears *before* all analysis sections. The synthesis must choose one of three required forms (`confirmed` / `revised` / `partially revised`), each of which requires naming the dimension that changed. Post-hoc rationalization cannot satisfy the required form.

- **C-16**: V-05 adds an **AMEND INSTRUCTION** paragraph inside the synthesis step itself — after completing any amendment, re-read synthesis and update or explicitly reaffirm it. This is co-located where the contradiction would occur, not buried in a preamble.

- **C-17**: Anti-pattern language is **co-located inside** the synthesis and unknowns sections (not in a separate step as in R2 V-05). The callout is phrased as "This section fails if..." — an active quality test, not a passive advisory.
