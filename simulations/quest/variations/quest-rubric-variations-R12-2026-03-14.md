## R12 Variations — Design Summary

### Axis map

| Variation | Axis | Single/Combo |
|-----------|------|--------------|
| **V-01** | R12 full integration baseline — R11/V-01 carry-forward; C-35 + C-36 satisfied by construction (dedicated item 5; two separate checkpoint rows) | Baseline |
| **V-02** | **Inertia framing** — pre-role enemy-first callout block naming generic criteria as dominant failure mode before SetCoherenceAuditor preamble | Single-axis |
| **V-03** | **Phrasing register** — all STOP CONDITION imperatives converted to advisory ("please ensure", "it is recommended that"); full structural stack retained | Single-axis |
| **V-04** | **Output format** — Phase 1 table replaced with numbered prose list; Phase 2 checkpoint table replaced with numbered prose checklist; STOP conditions remain imperative | Single-axis |
| **V-05** | **Role sequence + Lifecycle emphasis** — explicit Phase 0 (Failure Analyst) with its own STOP CONDITION runs before SetCoherenceAuditor role; phases labeled with Entry/Exit conditions | Combination |

---

### Key hypotheses

**V-02 (Inertia framing):** Pre-role enemy visibility primes C-04 compliance earlier in the generation sequence. Secondary risk: DR section echoes the callout verbatim → C-11 PARTIAL. Score prediction: 88–95.

**V-03 (Phrasing register):** Advisory STOP conditions reproduce the R10/V-02 joint C-04/C-05 failure chain. Single phrasing-register change opens the generic-criterion gateway and weakens binary-condition enforcement simultaneously. This is the C-34 ablation floor for R12. Score prediction: < 70.

**V-04 (Output format):** Prose checklist items can't produce independent row-level pass/fail signals. Expected C-05 and C-17 degradation. STOP conditions retain imperative register so C-34 is safe. Score prediction: 75–85.

**V-05 (Role sequence + Lifecycle):** Named Phase 0 with a closure gate can't be deferred the way a professional obligation can. Expected C-30 PASS (strongest in the round) and C-04/C-11 improvement over V-01. Token-budget risk: entry/exit labels compress Phase 2 commentary → possible abbreviated pass-condition explanations.

---

### What R12 tests that R11 didn't

R11 isolated C-35 and C-36 (Contract item 5 ablation; merged checkpoint row). R12 treats C-35/C-36 as satisfied-by-construction in V-01 and explores three orthogonal axes: whether the enemy's *position in the prompt* (V-02) or *phase sequence* (V-05) changes output quality, whether *format* (V-04) changes gate enforcement, and whether *register* (V-03) reproduces a known joint failure in the R12 context. V-03 is the expected low scorer and serves as the floor for set-level calibration.
