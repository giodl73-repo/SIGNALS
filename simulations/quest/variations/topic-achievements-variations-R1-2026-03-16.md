## Summary

| Variation | Axis | Hypothesis |
|-----------|------|-----------|
| **V-01** | Output format (table-first + state tokens) | Tabular `[E]/[~]/[L]` tokens make C-01 state classification visually unambiguous; columns enforce C-04 numeric ratios and surface C-07/C-08 gaps |
| **V-02** | Lifecycle emphasis (explicit 4-phase: scan → classify → synthesize → recommend) | Explicit scan phase before classification prevents invented evidence (C-03); Falsified gets protected space in PHASE 2 before synthesis abstracts over it |
| **V-03** | Phrasing register (conversational / second-person / motivational) | Motivational tone naturally frames Falsified as positive (C-10) because good news framing applies to all achievements; concrete ratios become *the good news to deliver* |
| **V-04** | Format + Register (prose synthesis-first + formal) | Opening with the maturity synthesis before category detail (inverted order) satisfies C-09 structurally and primes Falsified's positive framing before the reader reaches it |
| **V-05** | Role sequence + Lifecycle emphasis (scanner → classifier → recommender with named roles) | Named roles create per-phase accountability: Scanner can't invent artifacts, Classifier can't skip ratios, Recommender must be actionable; Falsified gets its own rule outside the general loop |

**Key design decisions across all 5:**
- C-02 (Falsified as distinct entry) is satisfied by explicit instruction in every variation
- C-10 (positive framing) gets progressively stronger treatment from V-01 (one sentence) through V-03 (full motivational framing) and V-04 (interpretive frame set before the entry)
- V-05 is the most mechanical — best for scoring reliability; V-03 is the most likely to produce natural positive framing for C-10; V-04 is the most likely to satisfy C-09 first
