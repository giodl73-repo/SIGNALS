## What R5 delivers

Five complete variations testing the three R4 candidate subpatterns as explicit axes, plus role sequence (never tried before):

| V | Axis | Key Bet |
|---|------|---------|
| **V-01** | Role sequence | CAUSAL GAP-first — mechanism question evaluated while inertia anchor is freshest; SEQUENCE then reads in light of mechanism verdict |
| **V-02** | Inertia angle in PART 2 | Explicit inertia coaching prompts per dimension in PART 2 — tests whether "does this beat doing nothing?" improves advisory specificity without crossing into analysis register |
| **V-03** | PART 2 word-count ceiling | Hard 150-word ceiling with per-step budget allocation — tests lower bound of advisory substance under strict compression |
| **V-04** | Role sequence + inertia-relevance column | Per-artifact inertia tagging feeds directly into CAUSAL GAP (first step); creates a tighter inventory → mechanism analysis loop |
| **V-05** | Phrasing register + PART 2 ceiling | Conversational "working notes" voice + 150-word PART 2 ceiling — tests whether brevity fits informal register |

**Fixed baseline** in all 5: C-15 (inertia anchor at Step 0) and C-16 (two-register by document shape) are architectural — not variation axes.

**Three open research questions** for the R5 scorecard:
1. Does CAUSAL GAP-first improve mechanism depth, or does losing the timeline baseline weaken SEQUENCE coherence?
2. Does inertia angle in PART 2 make observations more actionable, or does it push into analysis register (C-05 risk)?
3. Does the 150-word ceiling produce better team briefs, or sacrifice C-07 when multiple dimensions flag?
s: Role Sequence (CAUSAL GAP-first)

analysis accumulates cognitive overhead. SEQUENCE then reads in light of the mechanism verdict

analysis-order change improves CAUSAL GAP depth and makes SEQUENCE findings more relevant.

to satisfy C-07 (every flagged issue gets a next action) and C-05 (advisory not
verdictive) simultaneously when multiple dimensions are flagged. This test establishes
whether the constraint has a lower bound for advisory substance.
