## Round 16 Variations — Design Summary

**Floor preserved** (all five variations): Defense Register at Step 2b (C-47), `Defense basis` column in Defender Challenge Table (C-48), 4-rule COLUMN CONTRACT, schema commitment verbatim statements, Step 9 scoped to non-withdrawn proposals.

### Three single-axis dimensions

| V | Axis | Core structural change | Primary target |
|---|------|----------------------|----------------|
| **V-01** | Enforcement — named read-lock artifact | After Step 3a: boxed `READ-LOCK LOCK-1 ENGAGED` declaration naming every locked step + `inventory gap — [field]` fallback notation. Phase 2 gate adds LOCK-1 row. Phase 3 gate adds LOCK-1 self-cert row. | C-49 — does naming the lock as a first-class artifact (not a phase-gate side-effect) reduce re-reading in Steps 3b–6? |
| **V-02** | Framing — inertia as co-equal option | Preamble introduces Defender as co-equal ("every proposed change competes against the decision to leave strategy.md unchanged"). Step 7b adds `Challenge strength` column (Rule 1: Strong / Moderate / Weak). Calibration check: rubber-stamp audit / over-zealous audit declarations. | C-07/C-08/C-09 — does co-equal framing produce stronger challenges, higher withdrawal rates, better-calibrated verdicts? |
| **V-03** | Format — narrative-forward execution | Steps 2, 3b, 4, 5, 6 each produce a 2–3 sentence analytical claim before the table. The narrative must state what was found and why it matters — not describe the step. Step 3a and Step 2b are exempt (read step and commitment step, respectively). Phase gates add narrative-completeness rows. | C-06/C-16 — does committing to an analytical claim before filling the table improve specificity in `Implicit evidence` and `Strategy assumed / Signal revealed` cells? |
