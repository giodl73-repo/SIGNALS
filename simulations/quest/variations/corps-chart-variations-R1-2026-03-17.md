## Round 1 — corps-chart Variations

| ID | Axis | Hypothesis |
|----|------|------------|
| **V-01** | Phrasing register | Conversational "will/should" reduces instruction-density cognitive load while preserving all structural requirements — less risk of DO NOT being read as a blocker on output |
| **V-02** | Inertia framing | Flat team wins by default; structure must beat it. Forces the Rebuttal to do real argumentative work instead of boilerplate "as the team grows" verdicts |
| **V-03** | Role sequence | Role names injected as required cross-references into the mechanism table and Rebuttal — grounds the inertia assessment in actual team composition, reduces C-01/C-06 drift |
| **V-04** | V-01 + V-02 | Soft register + inertia-first framing — model engages the question rather than executing a rule list |
| **V-05** | V-02 + V-03 + Format contract | All table schemas declared in a FORMAT CONTRACT block before any output begins, plus inertia-first and role cross-reference — attacks the two most common failure modes (merged Decides/Escalates and short-form Quorum) by giving the model the schema contract up front |

**Predicted winners**: V-05 is most likely to eliminate format failures (C-03, C-05). V-02 is most likely to produce richer C-06 inertia content. V-01 is the cleanest baseline to score against.

**Predicted risk**: V-03 alone may not improve scores significantly unless the underlying model already handles role-grounding — the cross-reference injection adds constraint density without a format anchor.
