## Variate R3 -- draft-brainstorm

**5 variations targeting 120/120 across C-15..C-18.**

### Single-axis variations (isolate one R3 criterion each)

| Variation | Axis | Base | Passes | Projected |
|-----------|------|------|--------|-----------|
| **V-01** | C-15: Structural Spine Guarantee | R2-V-03 | C-15+C-16+C-18 | 117.5 |
| **V-02** | C-17: Peer-Comparison Defensibility Test | R2-V-02 | C-15+C-17+C-18 | 117.5 |
| **V-03** | C-16: Generation-Phase Sequential Guard | R2-V-02 | C-15+C-16+C-18 | 117.5 |

**V-01** (C-15 focus): Changes "use as category scaffolding" to "required `##` sections -- do not collapse into flat list, do not omit." Inherits C-16 from R2-V-03's "do not mark top-5 yet" in Phase 1b. Keeps Check B interrogative to isolate C-15.

**V-02** (C-17 focus): Adds `"Why [Idea Name] and not [name one specific unmarked idea in the same Dimension]?"` as a required peer test in Top-5 Selection rules. Inherits C-15/C-18 from R2-V-02. Guard stays in selection step (C-16 intentionally absent).

**V-03** (C-16 focus): Moves `"Do not mark any idea with ** in this section"` from Top-5 Selection into the Axis Map (generation phase), with the explanation "the first ideas written are the most available, not the best." Inherits C-15/C-18 from R2-V-02. No peer sentence added.

### Combination variations (target 120)

**V-04** (R2-V-02 base + C-16 + C-17 + explicit C-18): Adds the gen-phase guard to Axis Map, adds the peer test to a named Self-Review Phase (Reviews A/B/C/D), and upgrades C-18 from implicit artifact-order to explicit `"Review A -> top-5 marking"` named connections.

**V-05** (Table format, all four): Upgrades R2-V-05 -- Category View becomes a "required structural output; model cannot satisfy with flat list" (C-15), `"Leave blank during Step 1"` moves guard into table-building step (C-16), Review B peer test gates Top-5? marking (C-17), Step 2 becomes named Self-Review Phase with Review A/B/C/D each feeding a numbered downstream step (C-18).

### Key design decisions

- **C-16 placement**: The generation-phase guard reads: *"Do not mark any idea with `**` in this section. Marking during generation introduces sequential bias -- the first ideas written are the most available, not the best."* The bias explanation makes the prohibition harder to skip.

- **C-17 formulation**: The peer test is imperative, not interrogative: *"You must name a specific peer and articulate the distinction. If you cannot complete this sentence for a candidate, that candidate does not belong in the top-5."* This distinguishes from R2-V-03's failing Check B which asked "can you complete?" without requiring completion.

- **C-18 upgrade (V-04/V-05)**: Each review explicitly names its downstream output: `"Review A (feeds top-5 marking)"`, `"Review B (feeds top-5 marking)"`, `"Review C (feeds AMEND finalization)"`. This makes the feedback loop auditable, not assumed.
