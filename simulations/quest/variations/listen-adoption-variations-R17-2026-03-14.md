### Key structural innovations per variation

**V-01** — Phase-open inheritance makes each phase body explicitly bracketed: the `Displacement state at phase entry:` field *re-cites* the previous phase's ledger verbatim. PHASE 1 gets a fixed unchallenged declaration; PHASE 3 uses a crossing-event variant that re-cites PHASE 2's exit state before the chasm diagnosis begins.

**V-02** — SECTION K grows a second table (Citation Contract Completion Record) with 19 rows covering all five I-0X IDs across their required downstream locations. The terminal section can now prove citation compliance standalone, without asking the reader to navigate to SECTION H.

**V-03** — The DOWNSTREAM CITATION CONTRACT in SECTION A becomes a three-column table with an empty `Gate H Status` column. Gate H's audit instruction: *reproduce this form and fill each row* — not *generate your own audit structure*. This is the audit-form equivalent of C-29's answer-form enforcement.

**V-04** — Combines A+C. Hypothesis: seeing the pre-declared contract form at SECTION A generation time (Axis C) creates generation-time pressure that makes the phase-open inheritance fields (Axis A) more likely to thread through to the correct I-0X citations — the form and the chain interact.

**V-05** — All three. The pre-declared audit form (C) feeds the terminal certificate (B), and the entry-state chain (A) adds six additional structural locations where displacement state must remain consistent with the contract.
