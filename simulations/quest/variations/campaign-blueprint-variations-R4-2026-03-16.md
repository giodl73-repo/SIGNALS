## R4 Context

R3 V-04 (matrix + template) established the v4 ceiling at 128 by earning both C-17 and C-18 full credit. All five R3 variants earned Golden under v3; under v4 they split into four bands (128 / 125.5 / 123 / 120.5). R4 explores three new single axes and two combinations that all target 128.

---

## Variation Axes

**Three single-axis variations:**

**V-01 — FINDINGS Gate Separation.** Moves FINDINGS out of the pitch section into a named `CAMPAIGN REFLECTION` phase with its own GATE (`Do not begin Campaign Reflection until pitch is written to disk`). The double-prohibition becomes a phase description ("begins only after... not before... not during") rather than an instruction clause — making C-18 structural rather than instructed.

**V-02 — Contract Anchor Reminders.** Keeps R3 V-04's full matrix + inline "not before, not during" + named FINDINGS template, but adds a condensed `Contract reminder — READ: ... | PRESERVE: ...` line at the top of each artifact section. Tests whether proximity of the obligation declaration to its enforcement point adds PRESERVE/C-06 reliability over the matrix alone.

**V-03 — Conviction Staging.** Adds a `CONVICTION TYPE` row to the contract matrix (Factual / Optionality / Emotional) and labels each artifact section header with its conviction type. The CONVICTION DELTA audit is rephrased as a typed question: *"what emotional conviction does this establish that the factual and optionality artifacts could not?"* — more precise framing for C-13.

**Two combinations:**

**V-04 — FINDINGS Gate + Contract Reminders (V-01 + V-02).** Layered defense: matrix + per-artifact contract reminder lines + post-pitch phase gate. Tests whether three-layer structural coverage is more reliable than two.

**V-05 — Conviction Staging + FINDINGS Gate + Minimal Density (V-03 + V-01 + skeleton).** The density limit test: CONVICTION TYPE matrix row, typed section headers, gated CAMPAIGN REFLECTION phase, and minimal word count. Confirms whether 128 is achievable at any word count through compact structural elements.
