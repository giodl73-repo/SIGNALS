## Round 13 — Variation Plan

| Var | Axis | Core change from R12 V-01 | C-32 mechanism | C-33 mechanism |
|-----|------|--------------------------|----------------|----------------|
| **V-01** | Inertia framing | `Without echo:` slot added to entry template; INERTIA COMPARISON added to Step 9 | Prose schema slot in Step 7 | Named block in Step 9 |
| **V-02** | Role sequence | Dedicated **Inertia Auditor (InA)** role owns `Without echo:` enforcement (Step 7) and INERTIA COMPARISON assembly (Step 9.2) | InA ARTIFACT-SPECIFIC verdict per row | InA assembles block in Step 9.2 |
| **V-03** | Output format | Step 7 entry template is a **table**; `Without echo:` is a required column | Column presence = structural schema slot | Table rows in INERTIA COMPARISON |
| **V-04** | Inertia + Lifecycle emphasis | V-01 fields + **expanded Steps 0/9**: Step 0 adds BELIEF vs ASSUMPTION + REVERSIBILITY field; Step 9 adds WORD BUDGET contracts on INERTIA COMPARISON | Same as V-01 | WORD BUDGET contract on Step 9.2 (C-29 extension) |
| **V-05** | Full synthesis | All three single axes + declarative register: InA role + table format + expanded lifecycle | Table column + InA verdict | InA assembles from table `Without echo:` column; direct citation required |

**Key discriminating pairs:**
- **V-01 vs V-02** — does dedicated role ownership improve `Without echo:` fill quality beyond prose field alone?
- **V-01 vs V-03** — does table column format strengthen C-32 at the cost of C-03 (falsifiability in cells)?
- **V-02 vs V-04** — does lifecycle expansion add value when dedicated role is already present?
- **V-04 vs V-05** — does table format disrupt synthesis when all other properties are at maximum?

**The R13 thesis** tested by all five variations: C-32/C-33 are additive slots, not architectural replacements. Every variation carries R12 V-01's full CB-ID infrastructure (C-11/C-12/C-24/C-25 preserved) while integrating the inertia-artifact fields at different structural points.
