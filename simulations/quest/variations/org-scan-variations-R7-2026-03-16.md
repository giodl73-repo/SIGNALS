## org-scan V-01 through V-05 (Round 7)

Five complete variations targeting all 28 rubric criteria:

---

**V-01 — Role Sequence**
Three named roles: SCANNER → GATEKEEPER → SYNTHESIZER. Role handoff is the primary structural frame. Hypothesis: named accountability at each role transition makes the gate more behaviorally binding than phase labels alone.

**V-02 — Output Format**
Schema defined first as the organizing spine. Six named tables (A–F) govern all output. All synthesis sections rendered as tables, not prose. Hypothesis: schema-first layout reduces free-form prose drift and makes C-19/C-21/C-25/C-27 mechanically enforced.

**V-03 — Lifecycle Emphasis**
Entry conditions, exit conditions, and transition rituals exhaustively spelled out for every phase. Phase 1 COMPLETE handoff message, gate checklist, Phase 2 entry confirmation, Phase 2 exit verification. Hypothesis: elaborate transition rituals make gate evaluation feel real rather than structural decoration.

**V-04 — Inertia Framing + Role Sequence (combo)**
Dictionary leads the entire prompt as the central framework. Roles reordered: Pattern Classifier primes hypotheses first (before reading files), then Evidence Collector fills in paths, then Synthesizer. Hypothesis: classification-before-evidence-collection tests whether the inertia frame is internalized as a lens vs. retrofitted to findings.

**V-05 — Phrasing Register + Output Format (combo)**
Full imperative/command register. Numbered short-sentence instructions. No explanatory prose in process sections. Tables only appear at scan and synthesis output. Hypothesis: command-mode instructions reduce meta-commentary and improve gate/schema compliance density.

**All 28 criteria covered in every variation.** New R7 criteria (C-25–C-28) placed:
- C-25: schema column-order constraint declared explicitly in OUTPUT SCHEMA
- C-26: invalidity sentence verbatim in every dictionary block
- C-27: every table schema annotates Status for every column, not just evidence columns
