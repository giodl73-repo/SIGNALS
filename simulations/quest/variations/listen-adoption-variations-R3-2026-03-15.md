## Round 3 — Variation Summary

**Rubric target:** v3 (C-01 through C-15, max composite 100)

**Variation axes chosen:**

| Axis | Dimension | R3 target |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| Lifecycle emphasis | Phase-Embedded Champion Formation Event | C-14 |
| Output format | Mechanically Cited Traceability Audit | C-15 |
| Role sequence | Pre-committed Adoption Schedule | C-15 enabler |

---

### V-01 — Single Axis: Lifecycle Emphasis

**What it adds:** A `CHAMPION FORMATION EVENT` fill-contract block fires inside any simulation phase where a champion first emerges. Three required fields: role, bridging mechanism, incumbent argument targeted. SECTION 3's champion table gains a `Formation phase/month` derivation column — a champion without a simulation-body event fails C-14 at that row.

**Hypothesis:** R2 V-05's "Champion forming?" is a question; a named block with required fields is a structural guarantee. Making champion emergence a first-class simulation event with named fields forces timing to be structurally required, not inferable.

---

### V-02 — Single Axis: Output Format

**What it adds:** A standalone `TRACEABILITY AUDIT` block (separate from THREE-AXIS Axis A) with a `Row Citation` column requiring `[PHASE N / Month M / Row K]` format. "PHASE 2" alone fails. Non-adopters cite `[PHASE 5 CLOSE / non-adopter list]`.

**Hypothesis:** Axis A's source column accepts phase-level labels. C-15 requires row-level citations — a specific structural address, not an approximate location. Parseable format makes the audit mechanically checkable rather than interpretable.

---

### V-03 — Single Axis: Role Sequence

**What it adds:** SECTION 1 gains a `Committed adoption month` column, filled before any simulation. The simulation executes the declared schedule; deviations require inline explanations. PHASE 5 CLOSE includes a `SCHEDULE RECONCILIATION` table comparing committed vs. actual months.

**Hypothesis:** Roles that would silently collapse into late-majority cohorts must be individually declared before the simulation. Pre-commitment converts C-08 coverage from a post-hoc check to a pre-declared obligation. Committed months also seed Axis B's temporal completeness.

---

### V-04 — Combined (V-01 + V-02)

**Mutual reinforcement:** V-01's `CHAMPION FORMATION EVENT` creates well-defined rows with champion roles, months, and mechanisms — natural `[PHASE N / Month M / CHAMPION FORMATION EVENT]` citations for V-02's TRACEABILITY AUDIT. Champion roles are the easiest to trace precisely, establishing the citation pattern for all other roles.

---

### V-05 — Full (V-01 + V-02 + V-03)

**Non-overlapping closure:** V-03 seeds SECTION 1 with per-role committed months (schedule obligation). V-01 makes champion emergence a named simulation event (C-14 structural guarantee). V-02 makes all 16 roles mechanically traceable via parseable citations (C-15). The artifact becomes self-verifying: SECTION 1 declares, the simulation executes, TRACEABILITY AUDIT + SCHEDULE RECONCILIATION confirm without re-reading phase bodies.
