## V-01 — Single Axis: PART 2 Mechanism Verdict Anchor

**Axis**: Role sequence (PART 2 STEP A quotes MECHANISM VERDICT verbatim)
**Hypothesis**: C-19 makes SEQUENCE architecturally dependent on the mechanism verdict.
R6 V-04 required PART 2 STEP A to "connect to the MECHANISM VERDICT" — instructional.
This variation extends the verbatim-quote requirement into PART 2: STEP A must open with
`Quoting mechanism verdict: "..."` before stating the inertia case judgment. The bet is
that grounding the coaching conclusion in the literal analytical finding (not independently
re-inferring it) creates a traceable bridge between the two registers. Tests whether the
PART 2 inertia case changes character when the analyst cannot produce it without first
restating the mechanism verdict in its exact form.

**R7 research questions:**
1. Does requiring PART 2 STEP A to open with the verbatim MECHANISM VERDICT (V-01, V-04,
   V-05) produce a richer inertia case assessment than instructionally "connecting to" the

   already consumed in SEQUENCE?

   produce more actionable coaching in PART 2 and more useful pattern analysis in STEP 3?
   Or does the binary create false precision when the timing is genuinely ambiguous?
3. Does the explicit empty-pool branch in CAUSAL GAP (V-03, V-04, V-05) surface
   structurally empty mechanism cases that would otherwise be reported as "thin evidence"?
   Or is the empty-pool condition rare enough that the branch adds overhead without
   changing outputs?
