## V-01 — Single Axis: STALENESS Mechanism-Stale Binary

**Axis**: Output format (STALENESS emits a named binary output for STEP 3 and PART 2 consumption)
**Hypothesis**: C-23 extended the named-binary pattern to SEQUENCE's Pre-specification gap.
The same architecture applied to STALENESS produces `Mechanism-stale: YES/NO` — a named
output that signals specifically whether the inertia-pool comparison may be outdated. STEP 3
can consume this alongside the Pre-specification gap label to distinguish root patterns:
"evidence gap" vs. "freshness gap." PART 2 STEP C coaching urgency differs: a mechanism-stale
gap can be addressed by running discover-competitors again; a pre-spec gap may require spec
revision. The bet is that the binary sharpens PART 2 action specificity for the freshness
dimension without adding prompt overhead.

## V-02 — Single Axis: COHERENCE Mechanism-Relevance Tagging

**Axis**: Lifecycle emphasis (COHERENCE contradiction analysis tagged and converted to a
named binary output)
**Hypothesis**: When COHERENCE identifies contradictions between named signals, not all
contradictions are equally damaging. A contradiction between two peripheral signals (e.g.,
two scout artifacts disagreeing on market size) is less urgent than a contradiction that
involves an inertia-pool artifact and weakens the mechanism case. R7 instructs COHERENCE to
"note if the contradiction weakens or strengthens the mechanism verdict" — but this is
prose-only and does not produce a named output. Adding `Mechanism-relevant contradiction:
YES/NO` converts the inertia-impact question into a labeled output that STEP 3 and PART 2
can consume by name. The bet is that this tag discriminates between contradictions worth
acting on immediately (mechanism-relevant) vs. contradictions that can be revisited at
a natural decision point (peripheral).
