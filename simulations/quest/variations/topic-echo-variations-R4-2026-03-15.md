## Round 4 Variations — Design Summary

**Rubric v4 targets:** C-15 (Structural Counterfactual Induction) and C-16 (Misunderstanding-Category Synthesis).

**What changed from R3 → R4:**

R3 V-03's Archaeologist role earned C-15 PARTIAL (not PASS) because the schema fields still used original-team vocabulary — a model could adopt the role and still fill "Prior assumption:" from historical recall rather than future-team perspective. Both new criteria needed a stronger structural enforcement.

**The three single axes:**

| Variation | Axis | Target | Mechanism |
|-----------|------|--------|-----------|
| V-01 | Role sequence | C-15 | Archaeologist orientation embedded in schema field names and definitions — "Inherited assumption" is only answerable from future-team vantage |
| V-02 | Output format | C-16 | Blind Spot Map section with explicit pass/fail label examples + non-derivability constraint on synthesis sentence |
| V-03 | Phrasing register | C-15 (alt) | Future-team field vocabulary without a named role — tests whether vocabulary alone achieves the same structural induction as V-01's role embedding |

**The two combinations:**

| Variation | Axes | Test |
|-----------|------|------|
| V-04 | V-01 + V-02 | Do Archaeologist-framed entries produce better blind-spot category assignments than standard-framed entries? |
| V-05 | V-03 + V-02 + PORTABILITY AUDIT | Is vocabulary-based C-15 induction more susceptible to generic drift than role-based? Does a named-failure-pattern audit for the new field vocabulary catch it? |

**Key structural innovation vs. R3:** The non-derivability constraint in V-02/V-04/V-05 closes the gap that kept C-16 at PARTIAL — explicitly requiring the synthesis to state something not visible from reading individual entries or tallying category assignments.
