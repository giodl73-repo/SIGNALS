## Design Rationale

**The C-20 problem in R7:** V-03 and V-05 R6 tied at 112 under v5; under v6 only V-03 earns C-20++ (114) because it has named VIOLATION-NN identifiers. V-05 R6 has every other criterion at ++ but C-20=o.

**V-01 takes the minimal path:** V-05 R6's structural machinery is complete — adding just a VIOLATION REGISTRY table (8 violations, each mapped to an existing rule) earns C-20 without touching any stage template. Zero regression risk on C-09–C-19.

**V-02 and V-03 stress-test the 114 architecture:** Does prohibition mode survive role reordering (V-02)? Does enriching violation descriptions with explanatory prose keep the taxonomy intact (V-03)? Both should stay at 114 since no structural mechanism is removed.

**V-04 introduces VIOLATION-09** (omitted INERTIA: check), making inertia a named structural element rather than a prose aside. The ninth violation strengthens C-20 ++ while the Inertia Link column in the finding table and risk register enriches C-07 and C-10.

**V-05 combines all three axes** to probe interaction effects. The three axes are designed to be structurally independent — ordering, inertia, and phrasing register each affect different layers of the schema. The hypothesis is no regression; the risk is that teams-first + inertia together increase output complexity enough to introduce omissions.
