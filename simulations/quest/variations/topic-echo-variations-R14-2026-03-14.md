## R14 Design Notes

**R14 structural candidate: C-31 — Cross-layer synthesis claim coherence audit**

R13 is the first round where all five variations simultaneously pass C-27+C-29+C-30 — all three synthesis-claim layers carry a Composability-claim. This universality creates a new evaluable property: the three claims for the same pair can be compared for mutual consistency. Structural completeness (three layers present) ≠ coherence (three layers agree). A PREREQUISITE-classified ★BASELINE claim and a PARALLEL-classified live-inspection claim cannot both be true about the same pair.

The C-31 implementation: a discrete **cross-layer coherence audit** section after all pair inspections and before the final declaration. It names all three claim layers (C-30 baseline, C-29 canonical, C-27 live) for each pair where three layers exist, restates each verbatim, applies a vocabulary-class consistency check, and blocks writing until any contradiction is resolved.

All five R14 variations include C-31. This converts it from an observed-in-one-variation candidate to a baseline expectation — paralleling what R13 did for C-30.

## V-01 — Full-Stack R14 Baseline

**Axis:** Full-stack R14 baseline. All R13 proven structures constant. R14 addition: **cross-layer synthesis claim coherence audit** — after all pair inspections, names the C-30/C-29/C-27 claims for each three-layer pair, restates each verbatim, checks vocabulary-class consistency, blocks on incoherence.

**Hypothesis:** C-31 requires a discrete post-inspection comparison step. Structural completeness (C-27+C-29+C-30 all individually present) does not prevent silent archetype drift between layers. Without the audit step, a PREREQUISITE baseline and a PARALLEL live inspection can coexist undetected. The audit makes cross-layer incoherence surfaceable during pre-write.

## V-02 — Single-axis: Role Sequence

**Axis:** Gate order **Check B → CAT → NGT** (portability first, authority second, newcomer-accessibility third). All C-31 structure held constant.

**Hypothesis:** The cross-layer coherence audit is gate-order-independent — it runs in the fixed post-inspection pre-write window, not interleaved with per-surprise gates. C-31 passes regardless of gate sequence. Observable secondary effect: Check B runs before NGT, creating a later failure point for accessibility — a portable-but-jargon-laden surprise passes Check B, then fails NGT. No criterion is suppressed; one failure surfaces later.

## V-03 — Single-axis: Inertia Framing

**Axis:** A dedicated **pre-manifest inertia block** names the "without this skill" state: two specific failure modes — (1) silent mechanism antagonism (compound degradation invisible to per-mechanism testing) and (2) archetype drift (same pair classified differently across rounds, making cross-round genealogy incoherent). The manifest's CONSISTENCY GATE and cross-layer coherence audit are explicitly named as the prevention mechanisms.

**Hypothesis:** Inertia framing improves C-21 analytical depth (mechanism identification precision) without affecting any structural criterion. A reader who sees the specific failure modes prevented before beginning pair inspections is more likely to produce precise archetype classifications. C-30 and C-31 are structural properties of the manifest template; they are preamble-independent. C-17: the inertia block AMPLIFIES the archetype taxonomy — it increases classification precision without competing with the vocabulary check.

## V-04 — Combination: Role Sequence + Inertia Framing

**Axis:** Check B→CAT→NGT (V-02) + inertia preamble (V-03). Formal register, C-31 held constant.

**Hypothesis:** Gate reorder and inertia framing are structurally independent (PARALLEL). Inertia framing is preamble-level; gate reorder is per-surprise-level. Neither affects the cross-layer coherence audit. No compound degradation expected.

## V-05 — Combination: Role Sequence + Inertia Framing + Phrasing Register

**Axis:** Check B→CAT→NGT (V-02) + inertia preamble (V-03) + **conversational imperative register** (R13 V-01). Direct second-person commands throughout: "Pick the archetype first. Run the vocabulary check. Write the mechanism. Then write the claim."

**Hypothesis:** All three axes are PARALLEL at the structural enforcement level — register reformats without restructuring, gate reorder changes sequence without removing gates, inertia framing adds context without modifying the manifest template. C-31 PASS in conversational format: the coherence audit retains its three named layers, verbatim-restate instruction, and COHERENT/INCOHERENT verdict gate. Secondary effect: conversational register compresses the incoherence example into a directive ("If your live claim uses PARALLEL vocabulary and the baseline uses PREREQUISITE vocabulary: rewrite the live claim now") — format changes, structure survives.
