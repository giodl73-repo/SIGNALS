## R8 Variations — `topic-story` (v8 Rubric, 30 Criteria)

**New criteria this round**: C-28 (stated checklist invariant), C-29 (three separate inertia fields in Section 4), C-30 (recommendation sentence form cross-references `**Disproof condition**:`)

**R7 baseline**: All five scored 100.0 on C-01–C-27. R7 V-05 (consolidated `**Position delta**:`) would fail C-29 — the one new criterion that has a clear failure mode from prior work.

### Key design decisions

**C-28 variance across variations**: V-01 promotes the invariant to a named block (`**Checklist Design Invariant**` heading + blockquote). V-02/V-04 keep it as a standalone sentence before the checklist. V-03 uses a compact inline label (`**Checklist Invariant**:`). V-05 places it as the governing rule in the inventory preamble in bold. The question: does the heading change the score or is the invariant's presence (in any stated form) the criterion?

**C-29 across all five**: All five have `**Prior position**:`, `**Baseline distance**:`, `**Signals establish**:` as separate fields in Section 4. V-03 adds an explicit "Three inertia fields required — separate, not consolidated" instruction. V-05 annotates all three with "Inertia measurement — required separate in S4 (C-29)."

**C-30 variance**: V-01/V-03/V-05 use the standard sentence form with `[**Disproof condition**]` field reference. V-02 uses `[**Revision condition**]` (a derived copy). V-04 extends the sentence to cross-reference both `[**Prior position**]` and `[**Disproof condition**]`.

**Key open question for scorecards**: Does V-02's `**Revision condition**:` satisfy C-30's "structural cross-reference to the disproof condition"? C-30 fires on the sentence form naming the disproof condition — V-02's field IS the disproof condition by definition, but the name differs from the Section 2 field. The scorecard should adjudicate whether field-to-field derivation satisfies the structural cross-reference requirement.
