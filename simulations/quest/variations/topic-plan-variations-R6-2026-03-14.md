# topic-plan — Round 6 Variations (v6 rubric)

## Design Summary

Round 6 targets the four new v6 criteria. Each variation addresses a distinct structural bet:

| Variation | Axis | Primary Target | Key structural bet |
|-----------|------|----------------|--------------------|
| **V-01** | Lifecycle emphasis | C-24 | Unstated assumption extraction is most effective as a *named act* (Step 1b substep with Assumption ID + Why column) — not a row embedded in the stated-fields table |
| **V-02** | Inertia framing | C-25 | Consequence reasoning is better produced when the *preamble* frames every proposal as a cost-of-inaction decision, and when "Nothing changes" is explicitly prohibited as an If unchanged entry |
| **V-03** | Output format | C-26 | Consolidating all 6 table schemas + all null templates into one pre-read block means the model cannot encounter any table without having already committed to its structure |
| **V-04** | Output format + Phrasing register | C-26 + C-27 | Schema-first priming + three verifiable schema-commitment statements (Step 3 / Step 6 / Step 7) create both pre-commitment and a multi-point evidence trail — isolating the cascade mechanism without depth criteria |
| **V-05** | Full stack | C-24 + C-25 + C-26 + C-27 | The Step 6 checkpoint statement explicitly names "If unchanged ★" — meaning inertia is both required by content (C-25) and committed to as verifiable output (C-27) |

## Discriminator Coverage

| | C-24 | C-25 | C-26 | C-27 |
|---|------|------|------|------|
| V-01 | **PASS** | FAIL | FAIL | FAIL |
| V-02 | PARTIAL | **PASS** | FAIL | FAIL |
| V-03 | PARTIAL | FAIL | **PASS** | FAIL |
| V-04 | PARTIAL | FAIL | **PASS** | **PASS** |
| V-05 | **PASS** | **PASS** | **PASS** | **PASS** |

Single-axis variations cleanly isolate each mechanism. V-04 tests whether the cascade checkpoint cascade is achievable without the depth criteria. V-05 tests whether stacking all four produces synergistic or merely additive compliance — particularly whether naming "If unchanged ★" in the Step 6 checkpoint statement (C-27) tightens C-25 beyond what the column instruction alone achieves.
