## Round 18 Variations — Summary

**Three single-axis variations (targeting C-56, C-57, C-58 individually), two combinations:**

---

### V-01 — Inertia framing: recurrence as verbatim interrupt blocks
**Axis**: C-56 recurrence form. The INERTIA-GATE template is a pre-printed verbatim block the model must reproduce in full at Steps 4, 4b, and 7. A missing block is structurally visible (section absent), not semantically detectable (sentence shorter than expected).

**Hypothesis**: A recalled sentence ("Recall: inertia is a co-equal option") is subject to the same compression risk as any inline instruction. Making the recurrence an artifact — reproduced verbatim, not generated — converts skip-risk from semantic to structural.

---

### V-02 — Enforcement: pre-printed slot architecture at all decision sites
**Axis**: C-57 generalized. Extends the calibration checkbox architecture to every closed-set decision site: null declarations, schema commitments, confirmation gate choices, and the empty-type rows. Every site that previously used a conditional branch ("If X → write Y") now has pre-printed options with "select exactly one and write it as output."

**Hypothesis**: If pre-printed slots reduce calibration omission rates (C-57), they should reduce omission rates at other structurally-skippable sites too. Tests whether the slot-as-artifact pattern generalizes.

---

### V-03 — Format: degenerate register discrimination at every narrative site
**Axis**: C-58. Every narrative gate carries the explicit FAIL register label ("Step-description narrative") alongside the PASS label ("Narrative: conclusion before evidence"), with concrete FAIL examples. Gate checklist items are register-discrimination checks, not binary presence checks.

**Hypothesis**: Naming the degenerate register makes the wrong output feel like a category error rather than a mild formatting miss. Tests whether the FAIL example is the load-bearing variable, not just the PASS requirement.

---

### V-04 — Role sequence: schema → inertia → role
**Axis**: Pre-task contract ordering. Output schema commitment appears before both the inertia framing and the role line. The model commits to a 7-artifact output structure before encountering any role identity or framing.

**Hypothesis**: Extends V-01/R17's insight (inertia-before-role) one step further: schema-first creates a constraint the role operates within rather than an appendix discovered mid-execution. Pre-role schema + pre-role inertia = strongest possible pre-task contract.

---

### V-05 — Combined: interrupt blocks (V-01) + slot architecture (V-02) + degenerate register (V-03)
**Axis**: All three R18 innovations together. Includes the INERTIA-GATE artifact blocks (C-56), pre-printed slots at all decision sites (C-57), and REQUIRED/DEGENERATE register contrast with FAIL examples at every narrative site (C-58).

**Hypothesis**: The three failure modes addressed are independent (preamble-level bias, slot-level enforceability, narrative-level register discrimination), so the innovations should be additive. Full coverage requires all three layers.
