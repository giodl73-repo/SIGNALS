### Design rationale

R16 closed the cross-type register matrix: all four cells (all-desc, all-imp, imp/desc,
desc/imp) confirmed to compose without interaction. R16 V-05 established the first N=3
cross-type composition (C-51). The remaining open space partitions into three independent
dimensions.

**N-count beyond 3.** C-37 predicts no N-ceiling but the highest directly confirmed N is 3.
V-02 advances to N=4 using three section bodies plus the canonical inter-section peer slot.
The composition reduces modularly: positions 1+2+3 form a same-type triple (C-53 absorbs);
position 4 is a cross-type fourth placement inert by the closed matrix. C-52 extracts the
N=4 principle.

**All-same-type N=3.** C-51 was N=3 spanning two position types. No prior variation has
tested N=3 using only section bodies (no inter-section peer slot). C-46 covers pos 1+2;
pos 3 adds a third section body in a new register. V-03 makes this the first explicit
confirmation. C-53 closes the all-same-type N=3 cell, which C-51 left open because C-51's
structure required a cross-type third placement.

**Position-1 generality.** Every multi-placement test across Rounds 1-16 used flow-lifecycle
body as position 1. C-35 confirmed all five section bodies qualify individually (in isolation),
but not as position 1 in a multi-placement composition. V-04 uses flow-conversation body as
position 1, confirming that first-qualifying-occurrence satisfaction is uniform across named
section bodies. C-54 closes this structural gap, complementing C-35.

V-05 combines N=5 with all same-type (all five section bodies, alternating imp/desc/imp/desc/
imp). This is the most structurally dense same-type test possible within this prompt format.
C-55 extracts the all-section-bodies density principle.

**Cross-references to update in v18 rubric:** C-37 (N-ceiling note; add C-52, C-53, C-55),
C-34 (register-sensitivity note; extend to C-52, C-53, C-54, C-55), C-51 (N=3 note; note
C-52 extension to N=4), C-35 (individual confirmation note; add C-54 multi-placement
generalization), C-46 (R17 note; absorbed by C-53 as subproblem), C-48 (R17 note; directly
absorbs V-04 pos-2 inertness).
