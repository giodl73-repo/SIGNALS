## Variations — campaign-evidence / Round 2

Three single-axis, two combined. Axes chosen: **role sequence**, **output format**, **phrasing register**, then combinations with **lifecycle emphasis** and **named rules**.

---

### V-01 — Role Sequence (Evidence-First)
**Hypothesis**: Running websearch → intelligence → hypothesis (finalized) → analysis → synthesize forces C-10 structurally. The hypothesis card is written *after* evidence stages complete, so falsification status is grounded in actual findings rather than pre-anchored beliefs.

**Key targets**: C-10, C-03, C-02, C-06, C-07, C-08, C-09, C-12
**Risk**: Stage 3 (hypothesis) may feel redundant to a model that already threaded beliefs through stages 1-2; C-01 stage coverage may be abbreviated.

---

### V-02 — Output Format (Per-Finding Confidence Table)
**Hypothesis**: A pre-defined table schema with a confidence column per row forces C-02 and C-07 to pass by construction. A `CALIBRATION CHECK` embedded in the Stage 5 schema targets C-11 structurally — it appears as a required step, not a prose suggestion.

**Key targets**: C-01, C-02, C-03, C-04, C-06, C-07, C-08, C-09, C-11, C-12
**Risk**: Mechanical schema filling without substance; self-check may be written but not applied.

---

### V-03 — Phrasing Register (Conversational)
**Hypothesis**: Question-driven framing ("What does the public web say?") produces natural stage attribution through prose rather than schema filling, satisfying C-05 and C-06 organically.

**Key targets**: C-04, C-05, C-06, C-07, C-08, C-09, C-12
**Risk**: No formal stage headers — C-01 coverage discipline at risk; C-02 and C-03 precision likely weaker.

---

### V-04 — Combined: Lifecycle Emphasis + Output Format
**Hypothesis**: Explicit gate checks per stage (`Gate check: 3+ sources with URLs?`) prevent shallow passes. The `CALIBRATION GATE` at Stage 5 turns C-07 and C-11 into mandatory checkpoints rather than aspirational goals.

**Key targets**: C-01, C-02, C-03, C-04, C-06, C-07, C-08, C-09, C-11, C-12
**Risk**: Gate language becomes prose commentary; five gates add verbosity and may produce shallower stage outputs.

---

### V-05 — Combined: Role Sequence (Evidence-First) + Named Rules
**Hypothesis**: `CALIBRATION RULE` + `FALSIFICATION RULE` + `ATTRIBUTION RULE` + evidence-first ordering makes violation of the hardest criteria (C-02, C-03, C-07, C-10, C-11, C-12) a *visible named failure* rather than an omission. The `[POST-EVIDENCE]` label on Stage 3 adds design rationale — "a hypothesis anchored before evidence is a bias, not a hypothesis."

**Key targets**: C-01 through C-12 (broadest coverage of any variation)
**Risk**: Named rules may be treated as style guidance rather than structural requirements.

---

### Predicted Leaderboard

**V-05 > V-04 > V-02 > V-01 > V-03**

V-05 is the only variation with structural defense for both C-10 (sequencing) and C-11 (named anti-calibration guard). V-03 is most likely to fail C-01 and C-02 due to its unstructured register.
