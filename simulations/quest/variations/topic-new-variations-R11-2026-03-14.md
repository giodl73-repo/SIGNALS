## Round 11 Variations — topic-new (v10 baseline)

C-32, C-33, C-34 are structural invariants embedded in all five variations. The exploration frontier moves into three new axes.

---

### V-01 — Inertia Framing: Behavioral-Default Consequence Column
**Axis**: Inertia framing  
**Hypothesis**: A second consequence column — "Team Default When I Skip This" — names the organizational behavior that fills the vacuum when each phase is skipped. This converts the skip choice from "will I cause a failure?" to "will I enact the default that is already waiting?" The named default is more motivationally salient than a hypothetical harm because it is currently active. The executing model must actively override it, not merely avoid a future state.

**Key structural feature**: Pipeline overview has two consequence columns: first-person `If I Skip This Phase, I Will...` (C-32) + `Team Default When I Skip This` (new, R11-ES-1 candidate).

---

### V-02 — Role Sequence: Consumer-Anchored Signal Planning
**Axis**: Role sequence  
**Hypothesis**: Naming the Consumer role *before* assigning Priority grounds the vocabulary at fill time — "essential" means "the consumer cannot decide without this," which "high" cannot mean. Priority-vocabulary substitution (C-04's hardest failure mode) becomes structurally harder when each priority term has a decision-dependency referent that the wrong vocabulary cannot satisfy. This is independent of and additive to C-31's column-order enforcement.

**Key structural feature**: F-05 becomes `Producer → Consumer`. Phase 1 produces a decision table. Priority is defined operationally at Phase 2 fill time, not just enforced by rubric.

---

### V-03 — Lifecycle Emphasis: Phase Handoff Artifacts
**Axis**: Lifecycle emphasis  
**Hypothesis**: A phase is complete when its handoff artifact exists — not when it was executed. Phase entry gates require confirming the previous phase's artifact. Silent phase-skipping produces a detectable artifact absence rather than a gate-override. This extends C-21 (exit gates declared) to entry-gate enforcement by artifact dependency.

**Key structural feature**: PIPELINE OVERVIEW has a `Handoff Artifact` column. Each phase begins with "Entry requirement: [previous phase's artifact] is present." Phase-completion is artifact-detectable, not self-reported.

---

### V-04 — Combination: Inertia Framing × Lifecycle Emphasis
**Axis**: V-01 + V-03  
**Hypothesis**: Behavioral-default framing makes the motivational cost of skipping explicit; handoff-artifact tracking makes the skip mechanically detectable. A model that skips a phase both knows *what it is enacting* (the named default) and knows *the skip is verifiable* (the artifact will be absent). These are independent deterrents that compound: inertia framing closes the rationalization pathway, artifact tracking closes the detection evasion pathway.

---

### V-05 — Full Combination: All Three Axes
**Axis**: V-01 + V-02 + V-03  
**Hypothesis**: Three mechanisms at three structural layers:
- **Overview-reading time** (V-01): named behavioral defaults make inertia explicit before execution begins
- **Fill time** (V-02): consumer-anchored priority grounds vocabulary substitution prevention at the moment of assignment
- **Gate time** (V-03): artifact entry requirements make phase-completion objectively verifiable

No two substitute for the other. Together they close behavioral rationalization, vocabulary substitution, and silent advancement — three independent failure modes.
