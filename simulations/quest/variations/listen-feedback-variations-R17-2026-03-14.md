## Round 17 Variations Summary

**Primary target:** A-28 — directional separation of ±1 swing figures in sensitivity analysis

All 5 variations pass A-28. The single-axis variations each encode directional separation differently; the combinations integrate it with complementary structural improvements.

---

### V-01 — Output format: Two-bullet directional block
The ±1 collapse recurs because a single sentence can hold both directions. A two-bullet sub-structure per persona makes that form architecturally impossible — if either bullet is absent, the entry is incomplete by counting, not by parsing. Round 16 V-01/V-03/V-04/V-05 all used prose delta sentences that admitted collapse; this eliminates the escape route.

### V-02 — Lifecycle emphasis: Threshold-proximity annotation per direction
Goes beyond stating separate figures — each direction is annotated with its threshold consequence ("crosses threshold from NOT MET to MET" / "clears threshold by +0.3"). Near-threshold means (6.8–7.2) have one-way boundary crossings: a +1 shift may flip threshold status; the corresponding -1 shift from the same below-threshold baseline cannot cross the same boundary in reverse. Without annotation, a reader must compute this manually.

### V-03 — Phrasing register: Causal band-transition narrative per direction
Each direction is narrated as a band-transition event with D/P/Pr count changes, not just an aggregate delta. A Detractor→Passive transition changes both band distribution and threshold status; a Passive→Promoter transition has no threshold consequence. The narrative makes this structural asymmetry explicit where separate numbers alone leave it implicit.

### V-04 — Combination: Two-bullet format + numbered manifests (A-23/A-26)
Extends positional verifiability to three independent tiers: card level (count to 9 fields), aggregate level (count to A5), sensitivity level (count to 2 bullets per persona). A single verification pass detects completeness failures at all three tiers without semantic reading.

### V-05 — Combination: Directional separation + inertia sub-fields + failure mode annotation (A-16/A-24/A-27 on A-28)
Deepest inertia-comparative variation from Round 16 (Step 0 sub-fields + conjunctive gains/losses cross-reference), upgraded with a failure mode annotation on the A-28 constraint itself: "without separate +1 and -1 figures, a spec that survives a best-case swing but fails under a worst-case one cannot be detected." Converts A-28 from a structural rule into a self-explaining contract.
