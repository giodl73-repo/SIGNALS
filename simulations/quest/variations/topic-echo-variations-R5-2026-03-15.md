## Round 5 — `topic:echo` Variations

**Design context:** C-17 and C-18 from the R4 scorecard are the targets. C-17 established that correction-register vocabulary at the *field level* is load-bearing — "Expected"/"Finding" imposed a ceiling that rules couldn't compensate. C-18 established that a post-production audit layer (phrase-trigger scanning) is structurally distinct from prohibition.

**Three axes selected:**

| Axis | Target | Mechanism |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Output format | C-17 extension test | Section-level headers in correction register ("CORRECTION RECORD" not "SCHEMA") |
| Lifecycle emphasis | C-18 strengthening | Explicit PASS/FAIL gate per entry with visible declarations |
| Phrasing register | C-18 alternative mechanism | Paired-contrast audit (transformation rules, not prohibition lists) |

**Variation map:**

- **V-01** — Section headers renamed throughout in correction register. Tests whether section-level framing adds to field-level vocabulary, or if field vocabulary is already sufficient for C-17.
- **V-02** — PASS/FAIL gate per entry with visible, auditable verdict blocks. Tests whether commitment-declaration outperforms phrase-list scanning for C-18.
- **V-03** — Paired-contrast register audit replacing prohibition list. Discovery-register → Correction-register paired transformations for each field type. Tests whether teaching the register shift catches novel drift patterns unlisted phrases miss.
- **V-04** — V-01 + V-02. Section framing sets the pre-production frame; gate scoring enforces post-production quality.
- **V-05** — All three. Audit cleans entries before the gate; section frame primes throughout; gate creates the final commitment record.

**Predicted new criteria emerging:**
- **C-19: Section-Frame Alignment** — if V-01 outscores V-04 from R4, section-level correction vocabulary extends C-17's mechanism to the section level
- **C-20: Gate-Cleared Commitment** — if V-02 outscores R4 V-05 on C-18, the load-bearing component of the audit is active commitment, not passive phrase-detection
