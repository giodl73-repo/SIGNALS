## corps-achievements — Variate R7

**Variation axes** (all differ from R6's structural C-21/C-22/C-23 axes):

| Variation | Axis | Hypothesis |
|-----------|------|------------|
| **V-01** | Phrasing register: formal/technical | Uniform imperative voice prevents gates from softening into suggestions — "Correct the table" carries the same weight as "Glob `simulations/`" |
| **V-02** | Phrasing register: conversational/narrative | Team-report voice ("here's what the team earned") increases weekly adoption while still satisfying all 23 criteria through unchanged gates and tables |
| **V-03** | Lifecycle ordering: milestone-first | Computing milestones before per-topic achievements establishes team context first, reducing per-topic tunnel vision |
| **V-04** | Formal + milestone-first | Formal imperative language + milestone-first order creates the most auditable, team-oriented output — neither axis alone achieves both properties |
| **V-05** | Conversational + inertia framing throughout | Surfacing cost-of-inertia at every section (not only Next Actions) makes each gap feel consequential; narrative voice makes it feel like coaching rather than compliance |

**Key structural choices consistent across all five:**
- C-21/C-22/C-23 are baseline requirements, not axes — all variations apply the correction loop, derivability elimination test, and numbered sub-step gate language
- C-20 (criterion IDs in gate labels) is honored in all gate labels (`[C-01/C-15]`, `[C-19/C-21]`, etc.)
- The stagnation pattern registry uses the same 5 semantic labels: `SOLO_ISLAND`, `NAMESPACE_MOAT`, `SPRINT_FREEZE`, `SHALLOW_POOL`, `ORPHAN_TOPIC`

**Differentiating patterns to watch for in scoring:**
- V-01 vs V-02: Does register affect gate compliance, or only readability?
- V-03 vs V-01: Does milestone-first ordering change how per-topic achievements are framed?
- V-04 vs V-03: Does formal voice tighten the milestone-first phase or just restate it?
- V-05 vs V-02: Does section-level inertia framing add cost without redundancy, or does it create prose bloat that distracts from the tables?
