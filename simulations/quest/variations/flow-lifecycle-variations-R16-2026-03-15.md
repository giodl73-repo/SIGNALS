# flow-lifecycle — Round 16 Variations (rubric v16: C-48, C-49)

Round 16 holds the R15 V-05 full floor and probes residual delivery risk for C-48 and C-49. R15 established that both can be co-satisfied; R16 asks which structural delivery mechanism produces the most resilient compliance under output compression, token-budget pressure, and partial-reading conditions. The two new criteria are not changed — the rubric is unchanged from v16 as formalized from R15 evidence. The probe is mechanism variety: can shorter tokens satisfy C-48, can a pre-table rationale block carry C-49 load, can imperative voice inside cells substitute for declarative anti-pattern vocabulary?

## V-01 — Output Format: Compact Two-Clause Anti-Pattern Tokens

**Variation axis:** Output format — every STATUS-QUO PROCESS DECLARATION field definition is reduced to the tightest token form that still satisfies C-48: each cell carries exactly one "does not count" or "is a fail" clause plus a "Mandatory" token where applicable. The verbose multi-clause qualification chains in R15 V-05 are trimmed to the single most-discriminating anti-pattern per field. C-49 is held at full R15 V-05 floor: the Status-Quo Gap column header carries the full mutual-audit design principle designation with both the principle name and the structural-exposure mechanism.

**Hypothesis:** C-48 passes because the criterion requires only one inline token per field — it does not specify verbosity. R15 V-05's multi-clause field definitions over-deliver C-48 at the cost of very long column cells that are at risk of being truncated or compressed by output-length pressure. Compact tokens reduce truncation risk while preserving the point-of-use enforcement signal at each field row. Prediction: V-01 shows equivalent C-48 compliance to V-05 with materially shorter total prompt length, confirming that token economy on C-48 does not degrade compliance.
