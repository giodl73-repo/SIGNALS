## Round 17 Variations — topic-plan

Five complete variations targeting the six new v16 criteria (C-50–C-55).

---

### Variation axes chosen

**Single-axis (V-01, V-02, V-03):**

| # | Axis | Primary target |
|---|------|---------------|
| V-01 | Inertia framing | C-51 — co-equal preamble as first line, recurring at proposals, Defender Table, and gate |
| V-02 | Output format | C-50 + C-52 — Defender Challenge Table pre-printed as a template with Challenge strength slot; calibration check as three pre-written verdict strings to select |
| V-03 | Phrasing register | C-54 + C-55 — explanatory register throughout: every rule opens "This rule exists because..."; every step opens "This step exists so that..." |

**Combined (V-04, V-05):**

| # | Axes | Primary target |
|---|------|---------------|
| V-04 | Lifecycle emphasis + narrative | C-53 — Phase 1 and Phase 3 gates carry narrative-completeness checklists that block advancement if any of the five narrative slots is absent or is a step-description rather than a conclusion |
| V-05 | Combined | C-51 + C-53 + C-54 + C-55 — three-layer bias prevention: preamble-level prior (C-51), rationale-anchored steps and rules (C-54/C-55), conclusion-before-evidence tables (C-53) |

---

### Key structural decisions

**C-51** is placed as the literal first line in V-01 and V-05 — before any schema, role, or step — to ensure the co-equal framing is encountered before the model builds any structural prior about the task.

**C-50/C-52** are hardest to omit in V-02: the Defender Challenge Table is pre-printed with `Strong / Moderate / Weak` as a visible column header, and the calibration check uses a pick-one format with all three verdict strings already present.

**C-53** is hardest to skip in V-04: the Phase 3 exit gate explicitly checks each of the five narrative slots against a format rule ("states what was found + why it matters — NOT a description of the step") before allowing advancement to the confirmation gate.

**C-54/C-55** are deepest in V-03, where the explanatory register is the document's natural voice from Rule 1 through Step 8 — omitting a why-clause would be a register violation, not just a missed instruction.

**Structural risk summary** — risk of C-50/C-52 omission: V-02 < V-05 < V-03 < V-04 < V-01. Risk of C-53 omission: V-04 < V-05 < V-03 < V-01 < V-02.
