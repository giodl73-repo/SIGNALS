## R8 Diagnosis

Under rubric v7, V-05 R7 is at the ceiling: 21 raw / 100 composite / tie-break 21. No variation from R7 can exceed this score under v7. The R8 question is whether new structural patterns exist that would justify rubric evolution beyond v7.

The open frontier after V-05 R7: the ROB Summary contains three synthesis tables that carry conclusions (status labels, theme assertions, resolution states) without requiring primary-evidence citations. The C-27/C-28 pattern -- column-level citation obligation where a status label alone does not satisfy the cell -- applies at the finding level but not yet at the ROB Summary synthesis layer.

## Unifying Excellence Pattern

> **Citation Columns in Synthesis Tables**: Any structured table that draws a conclusion (status label, theme description, resolution state) from primary-evidence artifacts (F-IDs, findings, handoff packets) should carry a dedicated citation column pointing to those artifacts. Status labels without citation evidence are structurally indistinguishable from assertions. The column-level obligation introduced by C-27 for finding rows should extend to all tables in the ROB Summary where the evidence-to-conclusion hop is currently prose-dependent.

This pattern has three instantiations in R8 (one per synthesis table). V-05 is the full integration. If the scorer sees distinct output quality differences between V-01/V-02/V-03 and the baseline (V-05 R7), these three patterns are candidates for rubric v8 criteria C-30/C-31/C-32 in a progressive chain analogous to C-27/C-28/C-29.
