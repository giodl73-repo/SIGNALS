## Round 3 Variations — prove-interview

Five complete prompts targeting the three new v3 criteria (C-13, C-14, C-15). Ceiling: 125.

---

### Variation Map

| V | Axis | Targets | Absent by design | Predicted |
|---|------|---------|-----------------|-----------|
| V-01 | Inertia framing | C-14 + C-15 | C-13 | 120/125 |
| V-02 | Lifecycle emphasis | C-13 | C-14, C-15 | 115/125 |
| V-03 | Output format | C-13 | C-14, C-15 | 115/125 |
| V-04 | Inertia + Lifecycle | C-13 + C-14 | C-15 | 120/125 |
| V-05 | All five axes | C-13 + C-14 + C-15 | — | 125/125 |

---

### Design rationale

**V-01 (inertia framing, single-axis):** Baseline as Subject 0 with Evidence Pull + global synthesis quote rule (C-14) + Critical Contradiction table with mandatory Quote A/Quote B columns (C-15). No Tension Log. Tests whether Baseline-as-Subject-0 does double duty for both new depth criteria from one axis change, without lifecycle restructuring.

**V-02 (lifecycle, single-axis):** Named TENSION LOG phase between Round 1 and Round 2. T-number addressing required in all Round 2 INTERVIEWER turns. Three-step record/address/resolve in prose. No Baseline subject. Clean C-13 isolation — parallel to R2's C-12 isolation test in V-02.

**V-03 (output format, single-axis):** Four-column TENSION TRACEABILITY TABLE built incrementally across interviews (Source quote → Addressed in → Address question → Resolution quote). Empty cells are structurally visible. Tests C-13 format neutrality: does prose Tension Log (V-02) and columnar table (V-03) both pass C-13? Parallel to R2's finding that C-12 was format-neutral for disposition ordering.

**V-04 (inertia + lifecycle, combination):** Incumbent as Subject 0. Tension Log seeded directly from Incumbent Evidence Pull — same verbatim quotes serve both artifacts, no separate extraction step. The coupling hypothesis: C-14 structural position (Subject 0 with Evidence Pull) generates C-13 content as a side effect. C-15 absent — tests whether coupling alone reaches 120 without both-sides sourcing.

**V-05 (all axes, combination):** Full v3 ceiling candidate. Incumbent-seeded Tension Log + Q1 current-practice anchor per human subject (second C-13 mechanism) + global synthesis quote rule (C-14) + both-sides Contradiction table (C-15). Q1 anchor adds a behavioral forcing mechanism that makes C-13 grounding visible at the turn level — contrasting Q1 current-practice answer against feature-reaction answer is the behavioral evidence. All three new criteria targeted simultaneously.

---

### R3 research questions

1. **Can one axis change (inertia framing) carry two new criteria?** V-01 attempts C-14 + C-15 together — does the Baseline architecture provide structural conditions for both depth criteria, or does each require a separate mechanism?
2. **Is C-13 format-neutral between V-02 (prose) and V-03 (table)?** R2 found C-12 was format-neutral (column asymmetry = phase structure = both pass). Does the same hold for C-13's record/address/resolve requirement?
3. **Does the V-04 coupling mechanism remain valid at the C-13 + C-14 level (120) without C-15?** Confirms the coupling thesis is not artifact of V-05's full specification.
4. **Do C-13 and C-15 conflict or reinforce?** Both require verbatim SUBJECT quotes but in different structural locations — Tension Log source quotes (C-13) vs. Contradiction both-sides quotes (C-15). V-05 tests whether they coexist.
hat C-12 was format-neutral for disposition ordering.)
3. Does the V-04 coupling mechanism (Incumbent Evidence Pull seeding Tension Log) remain valid
   when C-15 is absent — i.e., is C-13 + C-14 reachable at 120 without both-sides sourcing?
4. Can all three new v3 criteria coexist without structural conflict in V-05?

## V-01: Inertia Framing — Baseline as Subject 0 + Both-Sides Contradiction Table

**Axis:** Inertia framing (single-axis).
**Hypothesis:** Naming a Baseline subject (the current solution) as Subject 0 with its own
interview and Evidence Pull, combined with a global synthesis quote rule (C-14) and a Critical
Contradiction table requiring Quote A + Quote B (C-15), achieves both criteria without a Tension
Log or Q1 anchor phase gate. C-13 absent by design — clean (C-14 + C-15) isolation.

## V-02: Lifecycle Emphasis — Named Tension Log Phase

**Axis:** Lifecycle emphasis (single-axis).
**Hypothesis:** A named TENSION LOG phase with explicit three-step record/address/resolve
traceability (T-number entry → T-number addressing instruction in INTERVIEWER turns → TENSION LOG
UPDATE after each interview) is sufficient for C-13 compliance, independent of Baseline subject
architecture or synthesis quote requirements. C-14 and C-15 absent by design.

---

```
# prove-interview

---

## PHASE 1 — ROSTER

Declare all interview subjects before beginning any interview.

| Subject | Role / Title | Prior knowledge | Knowledge gaps | Concerns | Disposition |
|---------|-------------|----------------|----------------|----------|-------------|
| S-01 | [job title] | [what they know about the domain and {{topic}}] | [what they don't know] | [what they care about] | [positive / skeptical / neutral] |
| S-02 | [job title — non-overlapping with S-01] | [what they know] | [what they don't know] | [concerns] | [positive / skeptical / neutral] |

Minimum 2 subjects with meaningfully different roles, knowledge levels, or concerns. Do not begin
any interview until the roster is complete.

---

## PHASE 2 — PRIOR KNOWLEDGE

For each subject, write a third-person prior knowledge statement before the first INTERVIEWER turn
of their interview.

Template:
[Subject role] has [background context relevant to {{topic}}]. They know [what they know]. They do
not know [what they don't know]. Their primary concern: [concern]. Their current assumption about
[topic or approach]: [assumption — a falsifiable claim the interview can test].

This statement is required before the first INTERVIEWER turn for each subject. Do not merge with
Q&A.

---

## PHASE 3 — ROUND 1 INTERVIEWS

Interview each subject in the roster.

**Transcript format:**

INTERVIEWER: [question — open-ended, grounded in this subject's declared prior knowledge and
concerns; not leading or yes/no]
SUBJECT: [answer — vocabulary, concerns, and framing must reflect the declared role; distinguishable
from other subjects without reading the role label]

INTERVIEWER: [FOLLOW-UP — question that probes or builds on the prior SUBJECT answer]
SUBJECT: [answer — role-specific vocabulary required]

INTERVIEWER: [additional question]
SUBJECT: [answer]

Minimum 3 INTERVIEWER turns per subject. At least one labeled FOLLOW-UP per interview. Generic
answers that could come from any persona with any declared prior knowledge are a grounding failure.

**Surprise — [Subject role]:**
One SUBJECT turn that produced an unexpected answer, tension, or reveal. Quote the SUBJECT turn
verbatim. State what was assumed before the interview and what the answer revealed instead.

**Evidence Pull — [Subject role]:**
| E-# | Signal | Quote from SUBJECT turn | Confidence | Rationale |
|-----|--------|------------------------|------------|-----------|
| E-1 | [insight, concern, requirement, contradiction, or signal] | "[verbatim SUBJECT quote — not paraphrased, not summarized]" | HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW | [one-line rationale] |
| E-2 | [signal label] | "[verbatim SUBJECT quote]" | HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW | [rationale] |

Minimum 2 rows per subject. Confidence required on every row.

---

## PHASE 3b — TENSION LOG

Before beginning Phase 4, record tensions surfaced in Phase 3. A tension is a claim or assumption
made by one subject that a subsequent subject could confirm, dispute, or qualify.

| T-# | Tension item (testable proposition) | Source subject | Source quote (verbatim SUBJECT turn from Phase 3) | Status |
|-----|-------------------------------------|---------------|---------------------------------------------------|--------|
| T-1 | [tension framing — phrased so another subject can confirm or dispute it; not a statement of the obvious] | [role who raised it] | "[verbatim SUBJECT turn — not paraphrased]" | OPEN |
| T-2 | [tension framing] | [role] | "[verbatim SUBJECT turn]" | OPEN |

Minimum 2 entries. Source quote must be verbatim.

Do not begin Phase 4 until the Tension Log has at least 2 entries with verbatim source quotes.

---

## PHASE 4 — ROUND 2 INTERVIEWS

Return to each subject with questions grounded in the Tension Log.

**Prior knowledge block** (required before the first INTERVIEWER turn — restate from Phase 2 or
note any updates):
[Subject role — same prior knowledge from Phase 2, or record any context updates from Phase 3]

**Transcript:**

INTERVIEWER: [opening question naming a Tension Log entry by T-number: "In an earlier part of this
session we heard that [T-N: tension item]. Does that match what you encounter in your context?"]
SUBJECT: [answer — addresses T-N explicitly; confirms, disputes, or qualifies; stays in persona
voice and domain vocabulary]

INTERVIEWER: [follow-up that probes the T-N response]
SUBJECT: [answer]

At least one INTERVIEWER turn per subject must directly name a Tension Log entry by T-number.
Referencing the tension thematically without the T-number does not satisfy this requirement.

**Tension Log Update** (required after each Phase 4 interview — do not advance to the next subject
until this is recorded):
| T-# | Status after this interview | Resolution quote (verbatim SUBJECT turn that addressed or disputed the tension) |
|-----|-----------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| T-1 | ADDRESSED / OPEN | "[verbatim SUBJECT turn from this interview]" or "Not addressed in this interview" |
| T-2 | ADDRESSED / OPEN | "[verbatim SUBJECT turn]" or "Not addressed in this interview" |

**Surprise — [Subject role], Round 2:**
One SUBJECT turn that contradicted an assumption from Phase 3 or the Roster. Quote verbatim. State
what was assumed and what was revealed.

**Evidence Pull — [Subject role], Round 2:**
| E-# | Signal | Quote from SUBJECT turn | Confidence | Rationale |
|-----|--------|------------------------|------------|-----------|
| E-1 | [signal label] | "[verbatim SUBJECT quote]" | HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW | [rationale] |

Minimum 1 row per subject per round.

---

## PHASE 5 — SYNTHESIS

Do not begin synthesis until: (a) all Phase 4 interviews complete, (b) all Tension Log entries
updated.

### Pattern
At least one signal that appeared across two or more subjects. Cite a supporting quote per subject.

### Contradiction
The single most significant contradiction between subjects. Name it, state why it outranks others
(what it confirms, undermines, or leaves unresolved about the interview question), and cite both
SUBJECT turns verbatim.

### Tension Log Resolution
| T-# | Final status | Resolution quote or explicit declaration |
|-----|-------------|------------------------------------------|
| T-1 | RESOLVED / OPEN | "[verbatim SUBJECT turn that resolved this]" or "No subject addressed T-1 directly — OPEN" |
| T-2 | RESOLVED / OPEN | "[verbatim SUBJECT turn]" or "No subject addressed T-2 directly — OPEN" |

### Arc Signal
State whether convergence or contradiction was the dominant signal across this session. Cite one
SUBJECT turn that best evidences the dominant arc.

### Prior Assumption Revisited
One Roster assumption that the interviews revised. State what was assumed (cite the Roster) and
what is now understood.
```

```
## TENSION TRACEABILITY TABLE

Build this table across all interviews. Each row tracks one tension from origin through addressing
and resolution. Populate each column at the moment it becomes available — do not backfill at
synthesis time.

| T-# | Source quote (verbatim SUBJECT turn from the interview where this tension surfaced) | Addressed in (role who addressed it in a later interview) | Address question (verbatim INTERVIEWER turn used to raise this tension — must include enough context that the subject could respond to it) | Resolution quote (verbatim SUBJECT turn from the addressing interview) |
|-----|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------|
| T-1 | "[verbatim SUBJECT turn from Subject 1 — the statement that creates the testable proposition]" | [Subject 2 role — fill when addressing interview begins] | "[verbatim INTERVIEWER turn: 'In an earlier interview we heard [tension item]. Does that match what you encounter in your context?']" | "[verbatim SUBJECT turn from Subject 2 — fill immediately after interview]" |

Column rules:
- **Source quote**: verbatim SUBJECT turn — not paraphrased, not summarized. Must be the turn that creates the testable proposition.

- **Address question**: the verbatim INTERVIEWER turn used to raise this tension. An empty cell is visibly incomplete. Paraphrasing the turn does not satisfy this column.

Minimum 2 rows. Do not write synthesis until at least one row is fully populated (all four columns
non-empty).
```
