## corps-pr — Variate R2 Summary

5 complete prompt bodies. Single-axis first (V-01–V-03), then combinations (V-04–V-05).

---

### Axes and hypotheses

| Variation | Axis | Hypothesis | Key criteria targeted |
|-----------|------|-----------|----------------------|
| **V-01** | Inertia framing | Inertia Advocate runs first, their section is an explicit reference baseline the consensus section compares all technical findings against | C-11, C-05, C-09 |
| **V-02** | Output format | All-table output makes C-01 (file-to-role mapping) and C-07 (per-role severity summary) structurally impossible to fail — a missing row is visible in a way a missing sentence is not | C-01, C-07, C-06, C-04 |
| **V-03** | Phrasing register | Describing each role's professional anxieties rather than issuing PRODUCE/FORMAT instructions activates the persona from inside, producing domain-loaded findings rather than labeled generic observations | C-05, C-02 |
| **V-04** | Role sequence + Lifecycle emphasis | Hard ROUTE → REVIEW → SYNTHESIZE → DECIDE phase gates with entry/exit conditions force the complete routing table to exist before any review begins — C-01 becomes a structural property, not something the model remembers | C-01, C-03, C-09, C-12 |
| **V-05** | Inertia framing + Consensus mechanism | Combines Inertia-first (C-11) with an explicit anti-perspective-label prohibition in divergence explanations (C-12), including examples of both valid and prohibited phrasings | C-11, C-12, C-09, C-10 |

---

### Design decisions worth noting

**V-01 vs V-05**: V-01 makes the Inertia Advocate structurally first and their verdict the reference baseline — but leaves the consensus section free-form. V-05 adds the C-12 mechanism-naming prohibition explicitly, including a "Prohibited divergence explanations" block with examples. V-05 is the higher-ceiling variation but also the one most likely to produce mechanical compliance if the model pattern-matches on examples.

**V-02** is the only variation where the TOTAL row on each findings table is a hard structural requirement — the table schema forces it. This makes C-07 the hardest-to-fail criterion in V-02.

**V-03** is the only variation using first-person reviewer voice ("what I found", "what I'm looking at"). Hypothesis: natural language activation of the role lens produces better C-05 satisfaction than procedural checklists.

**V-04** is the only variation with explicit phase entry/exit conditions. If the model tends to conflate routing and reviewing in a single pass, V-04 is the one that breaks that pattern structurally.
