## Competitive Brief: Signal Plugin

**Strategic insight:** Signal is pre-spec. Every named competitor is post-spec. Kiro generates a spec. Cursor plans the implementation. Copilot writes the code. LaunchDarkly validates the rollout. None ask whether the thing is worth building.

---

### 1. The Primary Competitor — Inertia (Threat: HIGH)

Teams skip pre-build validation because no structured process exists for it. The status quo is sprint planning as research, Googling as competitive analysis, and "ship and learn" as validation strategy. Inertia is sticky because:
- No "pre-build investigation" slot exists in sprint workflow — adoption requires a process change, not just a tool install
- The cost is invisible until post-launch rework
- Speed beliefs ("we can't slow down") are false but deeply held

### The Whitespace Signal Owns

The **pre-build evidence layer is uncontested**. The market has Kiro (spec), Cursor (impl planning), Copilot (code), CodeRabbit (PR review), LaunchDarkly (rollout). Nothing operates in the evidence-before-spec phase. Signal defines this category.

### --mode risk (exec framing)

A 3-engineer feature at loaded cost: **$12K–$48K per attempt**. Signal's pre-build stage: **2–8 hours**. If a team ships 20 features/year with 3 preventable rework cycles at $25K each: **$75K/year in avoidable waste per team**. The ROI is not competitive comparison — it's risk mitigation math.
