## Competitive Brief: Signal Plugin

**The insight first:** Signal occupies genuine whitespace — pre-commit evidence gathering across engineering namespaces — but every adjacent player could enter from their current position. The #1 threat is inertia. The #2 threat is GitHub controlling the commit surface.

---

### Primary Competitor: Status Quo (Inertia) — HIGH

Teams do *something* — Slack threads, 20-minute Google sessions, Confluence pages written after code merges. What makes inertia sticky: zero switching cost, deniability ("we do research informally"), velocity pressure, no artifact trail, no accountability. Signal's answer isn't a feature comparison — it's the artifact trail. You cannot claim you knew what you knew if there is no artifact.

---

### The Whitespace

No competitor owns **pre-commit evidence gathering across engineering namespaces** as a structured, artifact-producing workflow. LaunchDarkly owns post-decision runtime. Productboard owns PM-side discovery. ADRs own post-decision documentation. Linear owns execution tracking. No one owns the moment before the decision from the engineering team's perspective.

### Risk Mode (exec frame)

40-60% of features deliver zero measurable user value after shipping. The pre-commit moment is the last low-cost intervention point. Signal's argument: the artifact trail is evidence of due diligence — its absence is evidence of a bet. 5 features × 2 hours of Signal = 10 hours paid. One failed feature = 200-600 hours of rework.
