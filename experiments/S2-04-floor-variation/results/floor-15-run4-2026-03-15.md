# Competitive Brief: Signal Plugin (R7 — Full Run)

**25 findings across 22 named competitors.**

---

## Strategic Lead

Signal has one uncontested position: the pre-build evidence layer inside the developer's own repo. Every named competitor either activates after the build decision, lives outside the IDE, or covers a single axis (code generation, project tracking, web research). The real threat is the combination of Anthropic's first-party `/feature-dev` plugin (adjacent execution-phase slot) and raw inertia (the decision to skip entirely).

---

## Primary Competitor: Inertia

**Threat: HIGH (always)**

- F-01: Inertia is an active, socially reinforced choice. Signal wins by being faster than "is this worth the time?" — not by being more comprehensive.
- F-02: The cost of skipping is invisible and deferred. CTO frame: *"What did your last rework cost? That number is the budget Signal competes for."*

## Whitespace (F-24)

No competitor owns all four:
1. Repo-native artifacts
2. Developer-facing UX (slash commands, namespace vocabulary)
3. Multi-axis evidence (12 namespaces: PM, technical, research, post-ship, metrics, goals)
4. Topic narrative layer (`/topic:story`, `/topic:echo`)

The whitespace is a **category framing gap**, not a feature gap. First-mover advantage in category definition is real — but finite.

---

## Exec Risk Frame (F-25)

Signal's argument is **decision confidence + audit trail**, not cost savings. In regulated industries, the decision audit trail is a compliance requirement. `/prove:publish` and `/scout:compliance` address this directly.
